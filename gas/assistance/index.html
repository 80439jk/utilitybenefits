Gas Bill Assistance: LIHEAP, State Programs, and Winter Protections

Federal LIHEAP covers natural gas, propane, and heating oil. Layered on top: state programs that often pay more (PIPP Plus in Ohio, USF in NJ), utility hardship programs, and winter shutoff moratoriums in most cold-weather states.

Quick Answer

The main federal program for gas heating help is LIHEAP ($400–$1,500 per heating season for households at or below 150% FPL). Oil and propane households often see higher per-unit benefits than natural gas because per-BTU costs are higher. Most cold-weather states have additional state-level gas programs (PIPP Plus in Ohio, HEAP supplements in NY, USF in NJ, MA Fuel Assistance) that stack with LIHEAP. Your gas utility likely runs its own hardship program (Columbia Gas WarmChoice, National Grid CARE, Atmos Sharing the Warmth, etc.). And in most cold-weather states, winter shutoff moratoriums protect you from disconnection roughly November through April.

LIHEAP for gas heating (canonical guide)

LIHEAP is the federal backbone for utility heating assistance, and the canonical guide lives under our electricity section because LIHEAP serves both: Full LIHEAP guide →.

Gas-specific things to know about LIHEAP:

  • Same income limits as electric LIHEAP (150% FPL or 60% SMI baseline; categorical via SNAP, SSI, Medicaid, TANF in most states).
  • Higher per-unit benefits for oil and propane. Heating oil prices in the Northeast and propane prices in rural areas have larger benefit allocations than typical natural gas.
  • Heating-season focus. Most states open LIHEAP applications October or November and process throughout the heating season.
  • Crisis component is gas-aware. Empty fuel tanks, no-heat situations, and shutoff threats trigger expedited processing year-round.
  • Heating Equipment Repair (where available). Some states' LIHEAP funds cover broken furnace and boiler repair/replacement — see your state's specifics.

Winter shutoff moratoriums

Most cold-weather states prohibit residential utility shutoffs during heating season — but the rules vary widely. Some protect virtually all customers; others only income-qualified or registered customers; some require active enrollment in a payment plan.

Heating oil and propane assistance

If you heat with oil or propane instead of natural gas, LIHEAP still covers you — and benefit amounts are often higher because per-BTU fuel costs are higher. Specific things to know:

  • Heating oil: Northeast-concentrated. State LIHEAP programs in MA, ME, NH, VT, CT, NY, PA, NJ all have specific oil delivery procedures. Some states pay direct to oil delivery vendors; others reimburse customers.
  • Propane: Rural and exurban concentration. Tank ownership rules affect supplier switching. LIHEAP covers but applications can be slower because of vendor coordination.
  • LIHWAP (water assistance) ended in 2023 but LIHEAP continues for energy.
