Editorial standards

How we write, source, fact-check, and update our content

Utility-bill content sits in Google's "your money, your life" category — wrong information has real financial consequences for households who can least afford it. We hold ourselves to standards that match.

Authorship

Every cluster article, pillar page, and state page on this site has a named human author. Authors are listed on our author bios page with their credentials and beats. Bylines are not honorary — the listed author wrote or substantially revised the content.

Authors disclose conflicts of interest via the editorial team. Any author with a personal financial relationship to a utility company, assistance program administrator, or competing comparison service must recuse from the relevant coverage.

Editorial review

Every page is reviewed by at least one editor other than the author. For program-eligibility content (LIHEAP rules, Lifeline qualification, state benefit amounts), a second technical reviewer with subject-matter expertise verifies factual claims before publication. The reviewer is named on the page when relevant.

Sourcing rules

For factual claims about government programs, we cite primary sources whenever possible:

  • For LIHEAP: HHS Administration for Children & Families (acf.hhs.gov), the LIHEAP Clearinghouse, and individual state energy assistance offices.
  • For Lifeline and broadband: FCC consumer pages, USAC's Lifeline Support Center, and California PUC for state-specific Lifeline.
  • For utility rate data: U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), state Public Utility Commissions, and individual utility tariff filings.
  • For state programs: the state administering agency directly (CSD in California, OTDA in New York, ODSA in Ohio, DEO in Florida, etc.).

Secondary sources (news articles, advocacy organization reports, industry analyses) can supplement but cannot substitute for primary sources on factual claims.

External links open in new tabs and use rel="noopener". We generally do not add rel="nofollow" to authoritative sources — passing some equity outward is a confidence signal.

The annual data refresh cycle

Utility-assistance information is highly date-sensitive. Several federal and state data points change annually on a predictable schedule:

Data pointSourceUpdate timing
Federal Poverty Level (FPL)HHSAnnually each January
State Median Income (SMI)U.S. Census BureauAnnually mid-year
State LIHEAP application windowsState agenciesEach fall (heating season)
State LIHEAP benefit amountsState agenciesAnnually, varies by state
Lifeline benefit amountFCCAdjusted occasionally
Categorical eligibility programsFCC, HHS, state agenciesPeriodic regulatory updates

A designated editor owns the annual refresh process and is responsible for re-verifying every state-specific page on our site within 60 days of upstream changes. The "Last verified" date at the top of each page reflects the most recent verification, not the page's original publication date.

"Last verified" date

Every page displays a Last verified date in the header and footer. This date means: a member of our editorial team confirmed all factual claims on the page were accurate as of that date. It does not mean the page was rewritten or majorly updated — small edits don't reset the date if the content remains accurate.

If you find a factual claim on this site that contradicts an authoritative source, please email editorial@utilitybenefits.com with details. We respond to factual concerns within 5 business days.

Corrections policy

If we publish a factual error, we correct it as soon as possible. Major corrections are logged at the top of the affected page in a Correction note, including the date and the nature of the error. Minor edits (typos, wording changes) are made silently. Substantive content changes that change the meaning of the page are logged in the page footer with a date.

We do not retract pages for factual errors — we correct them and disclose the correction. We may un-publish pages that become wholly outdated (e.g., coverage of a program that has been permanently discontinued) but the wayback record remains accessible.

AI-assistance policy

We use generative AI tools for first-draft research, summarization of source materials, and language polishing. We do not publish AI-generated content without substantial human editorial review.

Specifically:

  • Every page has a human author who is responsible for the published content.
  • Every factual claim is verified by a human against a primary source before publication.
  • AI-generated text that survives review is rewritten by humans for voice, accuracy, and judgment-sensitive framing.
  • We do not list AI tools as authors. Bylines reflect human responsibility.

Conflict of interest disclosures

Our business model is described in detail at How We Make Money. The short version: we earn referral fees from a call center that helps callers apply for programs, and we earn affiliate revenue from a small number of comparison products (primarily home security, where consumer-facing affiliate programs are common).

These revenue sources do not influence editorial decisions. Specifically:

  • We do not give favorable coverage to any provider in exchange for affiliate revenue.
  • We do not exclude programs from coverage because they don't generate revenue (federal LIHEAP, for example, is a public benefit with no commercial incentive — we cover it because it matters most to our readers).
  • Our editorial team and our business team are operationally separate. Editorial calls are not subject to business approval and vice versa.

What we don't do

  • We do not run sponsored content as editorial. Any sponsored content (if we ever do this — we currently don't) would be clearly labeled as sponsored.
  • We do not accept payment for inclusion in "best of" lists or comparison tables. Rankings reflect our editorial judgment.
  • We do not write content using AI alone. See AI-assistance policy above.
  • We do not use stock images of "happy families on government program websites" to imply official endorsement. Visual choices are deliberately not government-style.
  • We do not include affiliate links inside core eligibility-calculator results or inside FAQ answers where the visitor is in a research/decision phase.

Reader feedback

We take reader feedback seriously. If our coverage is wrong, incomplete, or outdated, we want to know. Email editorial@utilitybenefits.com or use the feedback link in the footer of any page.

Questions about a specific page?

Email editorial@utilitybenefits.com with the URL and the question. We respond to all editorial inquiries within 5 business days.
