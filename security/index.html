Quick Answer · Speakable schema

For property security, the best DIY systems in 2026 cost $200–$500 upfront with $0–$25/month monitoring (Ring, SimpliSafe, Wyze). Professional systems (ADT, Vivint, Brinks) run $30–$80/month with installation. For medical alerts (PERS — Personal Emergency Response Systems), expect $25–$50/month for a basic in-home unit, or $40–$60/month for mobile/GPS-equipped models. Medicaid covers medical alert costs in some states (typically through Home and Community-Based Services waivers), and VA benefits cover qualifying veterans through Aid and Attendance and other programs. Most homeowners insurance gives 5–20% discounts for installed monitored systems.

Two genuinely different needs

Home security covers two largely separate use cases that get conflated by most consumer-search content:

  • Property security — burglary deterrence, fire/CO monitoring, package theft prevention. Goal: protect house and contents. Solutions: Ring, SimpliSafe, ADT, Vivint, Wyze, Cove. Mostly commercial; affiliate revenue territory; not typically eligible for assistance programs.
  • Personal safety / medical alerts — fall detection, medical emergency response, GPS tracking for cognitive decline. Goal: protect a person, often elderly. Solutions: Medical Guardian, MobileHelp, Bay Alarm Medical, Lively, Aloe Care. Often covered by Medicaid or VA; assistance angle real.

This vertical is unusual on UtilityBenefits.com because it leans more commercial than the others — most of our other content emphasizes assistance programs. For home security, the assistance angle is concentrated in medical alert systems specifically.

Best security systems of 2026 (overview)

Quick read on the major options. Full reviews live on individual pages under /security/systems/.

Best DIY:

  • SimpliSafe — best balance of price, ease, and professional monitoring quality. $200–$400 starter kit, $20–$30/month monitoring.
  • Ring — best for households already in Ring's ecosystem (doorbell cameras, etc.). Strong app, good integration with Amazon devices.
  • Wyze — cheapest viable option. $100 starter kit. Works well for self-monitoring; weaker pro monitoring.
  • eufy — best for privacy-focused users (local-only options). No monthly fees on basic packages.

Best professional:

  • ADT — most established, widest dealer network. Best for insurance discount maximization (insurers recognize the brand). $30–$70/month.
  • Vivint — best smart-home integration, premium pricing. $50–$80/month plus equipment.
  • Cove — newer, lower-cost professional option. Good middle ground.

Homeowners insurance discounts

Most homeowners insurance carriers give discounts of 5–20% for installed monitored security systems. The discount depends on the system type:

  • Local alarm only (no monitoring): 2–5% typical discount
  • Monitored system (24/7 professional): 10–20% typical discount
  • Smart home integration with monitored security + smoke + water leak: 15–25% typical discount

To claim the discount, request a Certificate of Installation from your security provider and submit it to your insurance company. Most insurers require the system be professionally monitored (not self-monitored) to give the full discount.

If you rent

  • SimpliSafe and Ring are renter-friendly (no permanent install). Take with you when you move.
  • Renters insurance discounts for security systems are typically smaller (2–5%) than homeowners discounts but still real.
  • Avoid contracts. Most professional security companies require multi-year contracts with early termination fees that don't make sense for renters.

Frequently asked questions

Is a $0/month self-monitored system worth it?

For tech-comfortable users in low-risk areas, yes. The downside is no professional monitoring — if you don't see the alert (asleep, in the shower, on a flight), no one is dispatching police. For most households, the $20–$30/month professional monitoring is worth it.

Do I really need professional installation?

No. Modern DIY systems install in 30 minutes with adhesive mounting. Professional installation makes sense for whole-home automation (Vivint), wired systems, or for users who prefer not to handle setup themselves.

Are these systems hackable?

All connected systems have some risk. Major brands (Ring, SimpliSafe, Vivint, ADT) have invested heavily in security since high-profile incidents in 2019–2020. Use strong unique passwords, enable two-factor auth, and update firmware regularly. Local-only systems (eufy local, some DIY) reduce attack surface but lose remote access.

Will Medicaid cover a medical alert for my parent?

Depends on your state. Some states cover PERS through Home and Community-Based Services (HCBS) waivers, but eligibility is restrictive — typically requires the recipient to be Medicaid-eligible AND require nursing-home-level care that they're avoiding by aging in place. Talk to your state Medicaid office or our specialists. Medical alert guide has the state-by-state breakdown.

What's the difference between Life Alert and Medical Guardian?

Both provide PERS. Life Alert is the most heavily marketed brand (the "I've fallen and I can't get up" company) but is among the most expensive — typically requires a 3-year contract. Medical Guardian, MobileHelp, and Bay Alarm Medical offer comparable or better technology at lower cost with no long-term contracts.

How much does ADT actually cost?

ADT's pricing is famously opaque — quoted prices vary by dealer, region, and your negotiation. Typical 2026 ranges: $35–$70/month for monitoring, $0–$1,200 upfront for equipment depending on the package, often with a 3-year contract. Always negotiate; ADT dealers have significant pricing flexibility.

Looking for medical alert with Medicaid or VA coverage?
