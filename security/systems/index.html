Best Home Security Systems 2026: DIY & Professional Compared

Side-by-side analysis of the major DIY and professional security systems. We rank by total 3-year cost of ownership rather than headline pricing — which matters more once you account for monitoring fees, contracts, and equipment.

MRBy Marcus Reid, Senior Editor · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer

For most homeowners in 2026, SimpliSafe offers the best balance of price, features, and professional monitoring quality — typical 3-year cost ~$1,000 with full monitoring. Wyze is the cheapest viable option (~$300 over 3 years self-monitored). ADT is the right pick if you want maximum homeowners insurance discount and don't mind multi-year contracts. Vivint is best for premium full-home automation. Ring works best for households already invested in Amazon's ecosystem. Cove is a solid newer professional option at moderate price.

How we rank these

Most home security comparison content rank systems by feature checklists, which obscures the only number that actually matters: three-year total cost of ownership for the typical household need. We weight differently:

  • 3-year total cost — equipment + monitoring + activation/install + early termination risk
  • Contract flexibility — month-to-month vs locked-in multi-year
  • Insurance discount eligibility — which systems qualify for the largest homeowners discount
  • Renter-friendly — wireless, no-permanent-install, portable
  • Monitoring quality — UL-certified, redundant call centers, response time

SimpliSafe (our top overall pick)

SimpliSafe wins on the core trade-off: it's a DIY-installed system with genuinely good professional monitoring at a reasonable price, with no long-term contract. Setup takes about 30 minutes for a typical 4–6 sensor kit. Monitoring is UL-certified through their own dispatch centers (not contracted out). The mobile app is competent but not the prettiest — function over form.

Best for: Most homeowners. Renters too (system is fully portable).

Watch out for: The cheapest "Foundation" kit is too small for most homes. Expect to spend $300–$500 once you add enough sensors and a camera. Their proprietary equipment doesn't work with other systems if you switch later.

Wyze Home Monitoring

Wyze is the budget option that works. $100 starter kit, $10/month for professional monitoring (or $0/month self-monitored). The catch: Wyze's professional monitoring is contracted through Noonlight, not their own staff. Response is generally good but the layered communication can introduce delays.

Best for: Cost-sensitive users in lower-risk areas, or as a starter system you'll upgrade later.

Watch out for: Wyze had several security incidents in 2022–2024 around camera image leakage. They've improved practices significantly but the brand carries some lingering trust questions.

ADT

ADT is the most established brand in U.S. home security, with the widest dealer/installer network and the strongest brand recognition with insurance carriers. The trade-off is opaque pricing and multi-year contracts. ADT typically requires 36-month contracts with significant early termination fees — read carefully before signing.

Best for: Homeowners maximizing insurance discount (some carriers explicitly recognize ADT certificates), users who want professional installation with a single point of contact.

Watch out for: Pricing varies wildly by dealer; always negotiate. The "free" equipment offer typically means you're paying for it through monthly fees over the contract period.

Vivint

Vivint is the premium option for whole-home automation — security plus locks, cameras, thermostats, garage doors, lighting all integrated into one system with one app. Equipment is professionally installed, wired into the home, and includes a touchscreen control panel. Monthly costs are highest in the category.

Best for: Higher-income homeowners building or buying premium homes with full automation expectations.

Watch out for: 5-year contracts are common. Equipment is proprietary and stays installed but can be hard to repurpose. High-pressure door-to-door sales tactics are common — don't sign on the spot.

Ring Alarm

Ring is best understood as Amazon's home security ecosystem — built around the Ring doorbell which most households start with. Ring Alarm is the security system that integrates natively. Voice control through Alexa, video sharing through Ring's neighborhood feature, integration with Amazon's Sidewalk mesh network.

Best for: Households already in the Amazon ecosystem with Ring doorbell or other Ring devices.

Watch out for: Ring has had its share of privacy controversies — police access to footage, neighbor sharing controversy, etc. Privacy-conscious users prefer eufy or Abode.

Cove

Cove is a newer entrant (launched 2018) trying to compete with SimpliSafe on price and lock-in. DIY installation, no contracts, lifetime equipment warranty, and lower monthly monitoring than SimpliSafe. Customer service has gotten strong reviews. Equipment selection is more limited than SimpliSafe.

Best for: Users who want a DIY-pro hybrid at slightly lower cost than SimpliSafe.

Watch out for: Smaller equipment ecosystem; fewer third-party integrations.

Renter-friendly options

For renters, the right answer is almost always DIY without contracts:

  • SimpliSafe — fully wireless, removable, portable. Take with you when you move.
  • Ring Alarm — same characteristics. Doorbell may need landlord OK for hardwired install (battery version exists).
  • Wyze — cheapest, fully portable.
  • eufy — local-only options reduce monthly fees to zero.

Avoid ADT, Vivint, Brinks for rentals — multi-year contracts and professional installation don't make sense if you might move.

Best for seniors aging in place

For seniors, the priority shifts from break-in deterrence to fall detection, medical emergency response, and easy-to-use interfaces. The right products are typically medical alert systems rather than traditional security systems. See our medical alert hub for that vertical.

If a traditional security system is also needed, look at:

  • SimpliSafe with their separate Medical Alert add-on (combination)
  • ADT Health — ADT's medical alert offering
  • Lively — purpose-built for seniors with simple interfaces

Maximizing the homeowners insurance discount

To get the full insurance discount (typically 10–20% on the dwelling portion of your policy):

  1. Get a professionally monitored system — self-monitored doesn't qualify for the full discount with most insurers.
  2. Add fire and CO monitoring. Comprehensive monitoring often unlocks an additional 2–5% discount on top of burglary monitoring.
  3. Request a Certificate of Installation from your security provider after install. This is the document insurance needs.
  4. Submit to your insurance carrier with a request for review. Discount typically applies on next renewal.

Frequently asked questions

Is professional monitoring worth $25/month?

If you can answer "yes" to any of these: you're often asleep or unavailable when alerts fire, you live in a higher-risk area, you want the homeowners insurance discount, or you'd want a real human dispatching police if something happened. Self-monitoring works for tech-comfortable users in low-risk areas who'd respond quickly themselves.

Do I need a landline?

No. All major systems in 2026 use cellular for monitoring (some have WiFi backup). Landline-only systems are obsolete.

What's the deal with ADT contracts?

Standard ADT contracts are 36 months with significant early termination fees (typically 75% of remaining contract value). Some dealers offer shorter terms; always negotiate. Read the contract before signing.

Can I move my SimpliSafe / Ring system?

Yes. Both are designed to move with you. Pull off the adhesive sensors, take the base station, reinstall at the new location. Update your address with the monitoring service.

Are smart locks worth adding?

Yes for most users. Smart locks let you grant temporary access codes, see lock activity, lock remotely if you forgot. The right pick depends on your security system — Ring has Ring Smart Locks, Vivint integrates Schlage, SimpliSafe integrates with August.

Do these systems work during power outages?

Most have battery backup good for 24–72 hours. Cellular monitoring continues through outages. WiFi-dependent features (some camera streaming) won't work without internet — but core security alarming/monitoring continues.

Looking for medical alerts instead of security?

Different products, different vendors, different (often partially-Medicaid-covered) economics. Our medical alert hub walks through the options.
