Medical Alert Coverage by State

All 50 states + DC. Click any state for its Medicaid HCBS waiver coverage, income/asset limits, wait list status, and application path.

Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer

Medical alert systems (PERS) are covered by Medicaid in most U.S. states through Home and Community-Based Services (HCBS) waivers. Coverage and wait lists vary significantly. Standard rules: income limit ~300% of the Federal Benefit Rate (~$2,829/mo), asset limit $2,000 individual / $3,000 couple (CA eliminated, NY $31,175, IL $17,500, others vary), and a functional eligibility assessment requiring nursing-facility level of care need. Wait lists range from none (entitlement-based: WA, OR, WI, AZ, VT) to 10+ years (TX non-managed-care, LA, OK, GA in some areas).

Coverage patterns to know

  • Entitlement-based states (no wait list) — WA, OR, WI, AZ, VT, NJ, PA, VA, NY, MA, CA, MI, NM, MN, NV, NH, NC, RI, IA, ID, KS, KY, ME, MD, ND, OH, SC, SD, TN, UT, WY. These offer HCBS as a Medicaid State Plan benefit or via 1115 waiver — meaning eligible applicants are enrolled rather than wait-listed.
  • Wait-list states (1–4 year typical) — AL, AR, CO, CT, FL, GA, IL, IN, KY, MS, NE, OK, WV. Wait lists vary by region within the state.
  • Long-wait states (5+ years) — LA, OK in some regions, TX non-managed-care waivers (CLASS, HCS). For TX, STAR+PLUS managed care is the practical access path.
  • Higher asset limits — NY ($31K), IL ($17.5K), MO ($5.3K), DE/RI/DC/MS ($4K), MN/ND ($3K), NH ($2.5K). California eliminated the asset test entirely in 2024.

If your state has long wait lists

Don't wait. Apply for the HCBS waiver immediately (the wait list itself doesn't cost anything), AND in parallel pursue these alternatives:

  • State Aging Department state-funded programs — most states have above-Medicaid PERS subsidies through their state aging agency. Sliding fee scale common.
  • Older Americans Act services — federally funded through Area Agencies on Aging. Often cover PERS for 60+ regardless of income.
  • VA Aid & Attendance — for qualifying wartime veterans. Cash benefit can pay for PERS.
  • Medicare Advantage supplemental benefits — some Part C plans cover PERS.
  • Direct purchase — Medical Guardian, MobileHelp, Bay Alarm Medical at $25–$50/month. Provider comparison →
