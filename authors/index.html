How the team works

Sourcing

Every program statistic, rate, eligibility threshold, or program name on this site is cited to a primary source — HHS, FCC, CMS, state Public Utility Commissions, BLS, or the agency that administers the program. Secondary sources (NerdWallet, Bankrate, etc.) are not used as the basis for any claim. See our editorial standards for the full sourcing policy.

Review cycle

Each page lists a "Last reviewed" date in the reviewer block at the top. We commit to a quarterly review cycle — every page is verified at least once every 90 days by the desk that owns it. When a critical change happens (e.g., an income-limit revision or a program-funding change), we update affected pages immediately rather than waiting for the next quarterly review.

Editorial firewall

UtilityBenefits.com earns referral fees from some partners. Editorial content is independent of these monetization relationships. Page rankings, recommendations, and the program-coverage scope are decided by the relevant research desk; the business team has no editorial authority. See how we make money and our editorial standards.

Why we don't publish individual bylines

We chose collective attribution for two reasons. First, our review process is collaborative — an electricity state page typically passes through a primary researcher, a desk lead, and an editorial-standards reviewer. Naming a single byline misrepresents who actually produced the page. Second, byline naming creates incentives for individual reviewers to defend prior framing rather than update content as facts change. Team attribution removes that bias.
