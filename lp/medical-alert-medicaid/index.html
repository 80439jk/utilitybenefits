Common questions

What is a PERS device?

A Personal Emergency Response System is a wearable pendant or wristband connected to a 24/7 monitoring center. Press the button — for a fall, chest pain, or any emergency — and the monitoring center calls 911 and your emergency contacts. Modern devices include fall detection and cellular, GPS, or in-home base units.

How does Medicaid cover this?

Through Home and Community-Based Services (HCBS) waivers — Medicaid programs that pay for support services that let people live at home instead of in a nursing facility. Most states' "aged & disabled" or "elderly" waiver covers PERS as a standard service.

Who qualifies?

Three things usually have to be true: (1) you'd qualify for nursing-facility-level care, (2) your income is at or below the state's waiver limit (often 300% of the SSI Federal Benefit Rate), and (3) your countable assets are below the state's limit (typically $2,000 for a single applicant, with home and one vehicle excluded).

Are there wait lists?

Some states have wait lists for HCBS waivers; others enroll immediately. Your state page on UtilityBenefits.com shows the current waitlist status. If your state has a list, ask about "Medicaid State Plan PERS" — some states cover PERS through the regular Medicaid State Plan with no waiver involved.

Which device do I get?

States contract with one or more PERS vendors. You'll typically get a base unit plus a wearable pendant; many states now offer cellular and GPS-enabled mobile units. The vendor handles installation and 24/7 monitoring.

What if my state doesn't cover it?

Three states (and a few specific waivers) don't cover PERS through Medicaid. In those cases, Medicare Advantage plans, the VA's Aid & Attendance benefit, or local Area Agencies on Aging often have programs that cover the device or monthly fee. We'll show every option.
