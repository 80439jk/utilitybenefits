Common questions

Will my power go out when I switch?

No. Switching electricity suppliers does not affect physical service. The same wires still deliver power; the same utility still owns the meter and handles outages. The only thing that changes is which company supplies the electrons and bills you for them.

Why is my current rate higher than these?

Most households on a deregulated grid are on the utility's default "price-to-compare" rate, which is reset every 6 months and usually runs 1–3 ¢/kWh higher than the lowest fixed offers on the open market. That's where most of the savings come from.

Are there any fees?

Switching is free. The new supplier handles the switch with your utility. Some suppliers charge an early-termination fee if you leave before the contract ends — we show that fee on every plan card so you can avoid it if you want.

What is a "fixed" rate vs. "variable"?

A fixed-rate plan locks your supply rate (cents per kilowatt-hour) for the entire contract — usually 6, 12, 18, or 24 months. A variable rate can change month to month and has caused some bad headlines after winter price spikes. We default the comparison to fixed-only.

Which states are deregulated?

Texas, Pennsylvania, New York, Ohio, Illinois, New Jersey, Massachusetts, Connecticut, Maryland, Delaware, DC, Maine, New Hampshire, Rhode Island, Michigan (limited), and Virginia (limited).

Why is this free?

We earn a small fee from suppliers when a household chooses their plan. That fee comes out of the supplier's marketing budget — not added to your bill.
