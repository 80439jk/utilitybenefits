Common questions

What is the Lifeline program?

Lifeline is a federal program (run by the Universal Service Administrative Company) that provides a discount of up to $9.25/month on phone or broadband service for qualifying low-income households. Tribal lands receive up to $34.25/month. Most Lifeline carriers offer plans where the federal subsidy fully covers a basic plan, making it free at the point of use.

Who qualifies?

Households qualify two ways: (1) by income — household income at or below 135% of the federal poverty guidelines, or (2) by participation in a qualifying program — SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, Federal Public Housing Assistance, Veterans Pension/Survivors Benefits, or certain Tribal programs.

Is this the same as the Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP)?

No. ACP ended in May/June 2024 when funding ran out. Lifeline is the older, separately-funded program that continues to operate. Some states have launched state-level ACP-replacement programs — we'll show you what's available in your state.

Will I really get a free phone?

Most Lifeline carriers offer a free smartphone with enrollment, but the exact device varies by state and carrier. Some offer entry-level Android phones; a few offer mid-range models. We don't promise a specific device — your carrier will.

Is there any cost?

There is no application fee and no cost from us. Most Lifeline plans are fully covered by the federal subsidy and have no monthly charge. A few carriers offer upgraded plans with extra data for a small monthly fee — those are optional.

How long does enrollment take?

Most applications are approved same-day or within 24 hours. Once approved, your phone typically arrives in 3–7 business days.
