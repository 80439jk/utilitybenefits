Common questions

What's an MVNO?

A Mobile Virtual Network Operator — a smaller carrier that buys wholesale capacity on the big three networks (Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile) and resells it for less. Examples: Visible, Mint Mobile, Cricket, US Mobile, Total by Verizon, Tello, Boost. You get the same coverage as the underlying network — usually for half the price or less.

Will my phone work?

Yes, in almost every case. If your phone is unlocked or paid off, it works on any compatible MVNO. Phones bought directly from Verizon work on Verizon-based MVNOs (Visible, Total, US Mobile Verizon); AT&T phones work on AT&T MVNOs (Cricket, Mint, US Mobile AT&T); etc. We show compatibility on every plan card.

Will my number transfer?

Yes. By federal rule, you have the right to port your number to any new carrier — we walk you through the steps. The switch typically takes under an hour and your old service stays active until the new one activates.

Is the coverage really the same?

Yes — MVNOs use the exact same towers as the major carrier they ride on. The only practical difference is that some MVNOs are "deprioritized," meaning their data slows down before the host carrier's customers when a tower is congested. We flag deprioritization on every plan.

How does Lifeline work?

Lifeline is a federal program that subsidizes phone or broadband service for low-income households. The subsidy ($9.25/month, or $34.25 on Tribal lands) is enough to fully cover a basic plan from approved Lifeline carriers — making it free at the point of use. You qualify by income (≤135% of poverty) or by participating in SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, or Federal Public Housing.

Is there any catch?

MVNOs are not a catch — they're a known industry structure used by ~50 million Americans. The downsides are: customer service is usually online-only or phone-only (no retail stores), some deprioritize data on the host network, and a few have spotty international roaming. We surface those tradeoffs on every plan card.
