Common questions

Is this only for people who need bill assistance?
No. The 2-Track Check helps both households looking for emergency or ongoing assistance and households who mainly want lower electric, gas, phone, or internet rates. Plenty of callers only use the savings track.
What kinds of help can I actually get?
Depending on your eligibility and situation, you may see things like heating and cooling credits, shut-off protections, lower electric or gas rates, discounted phone or internet, or bill-reduction options.
What if I probably will not qualify for assistance?
You can still benefit from the rate comparison track. We check your existing electric, gas, phone, and internet plans against current offers in your area and look for lower rates, better bundles, or available rebates.
Can I use this even if my bills are paid up?
Yes. Many people call simply because they feel they are paying too much every month and want to see if there is a cheaper option before their next bill.
How much does the 2-Track Check cost?
The check is completely free. There are no fees, subscriptions, or surprise charges for using UtilityBenefits.com.
Will this hurt my credit or require a Social Security number?
No credit check is done for the 2-Track Check. You do not need to give a Social Security number to start your screening.
How long does the call take?
Most households complete the intake in about 2 minutes, then get a clear list of potential programs and savings while still on the call.
What if I already called 211 or tried government websites?
Many callers tried 211 or state sites first and still found additional options through the 2-Track Check, because we screen multiple programs and rate offers together in one place.
Do I have to switch providers or enroll in anything?
You always decide what to do. Our partner reps can help you enroll or switch by phone if you choose, but you are never required to make a change.
Who is this best for?
Households that are behind on a bill (or close), on a fixed or tight income, tired of overpaying for essential utilities, or simply want a quick way to check which utility assistance programs, discounts, and lower-rate plans they qualify for often benefit most from the 2-Track Check.
Can I use this to check my eligibility before I apply anywhere else?
Yes. Many people use the 2-Track Check as a quick utility assistance and discount eligibility check before filling out long applications or calling multiple providers.
