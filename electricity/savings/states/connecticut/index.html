Connecticut Electricity: EnergizeCT, Standard Service, and the 2023 Reforms

Connecticut has retail electricity choice but high default-supply rates (among the highest in the country). The 2023 PURA reforms capped some predatory practices. Shopping makes sense for many CT households but always benchmark against Standard Service.

SKBy Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Connecticut has a deregulated retail electricity market. Shop on EnergizeCT.com — the state PUC's apples-to-apples site. Current 12-month fixed-rate plans range 14.5 – 17.5¢/kWh. Compare against your utility's default-supply rate before switching. Connecticut default supply is called Standard Service. Eversource and United Illuminating each have their own Standard Service rate that resets every 6 months via PURA-overseen procurement. CT Standard Service rates have been among the highest in the country — meaning shopping often saves real money in CT.

What makes Connecticut different

Connecticut has the highest residential electric rates in the continental U.S. — Standard Service often runs 18–24¢/kWh during winter months. The high rates are driven by limited natural gas pipeline capacity into New England (especially during winter heating peaks) and by long-term renewable contracts the state procured at above-market prices in the 2010s.

For CT residents, shopping for a competitive supplier often saves 15–25% — among the largest savings of any deregulated state. The 2023 PURA reforms (after years of predatory-pricing complaints) capped variable-rate spreads, restricted door-to-door enrollment, and tightened disclosure rules.

Income-qualified CT households should also know about Eversource New Start — an arrearage forgiveness program that can write off thousands of dollars in past-due electric bills. CT assistance guide →

Connecticut-specific things to watch for

  • The high Standard Service rates make CT a high-savings shopping state. Take advantage if you can.
  • Variable-rate cap (2023 reform). Capped at 115% of Standard Service trailing-12-month. Doesn't eliminate predatory pricing but limits the worst.
  • Eversource New Start arrearage forgiveness. If you're behind, this is the priority before shopping. CT guide →
  • Door-to-door restrictions tightened in 2023. Verify any pitch via EnergizeCT.

How to shop in Connecticut

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to EnergizeCT.com and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

If you're income-qualified for assistance

Most Connecticut households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level qualify for at least one assistance program. These often beat retail shopping savings:

  • LIHEAP — federal energy assistance, $300–$1,500/year typical. Connecticut LIHEAP guide →
  • State or utility-specific discount programs — varies by state. CARE/FERA in CA, PIPP in OH/IL, USF in NJ, CAP rates in PA, EAP in NH.
  • Crisis funds — for shutoff threats and emergencies.

Connecticut electricity FAQ

Why are CT electric rates so high?

Limited natural gas pipeline capacity into New England (especially winter) drives wholesale prices high. CT also procured long-term renewable contracts at above-market rates in the 2010s. The combination puts Standard Service rates among the country's highest.

Is shopping always worth it in CT?

Often, yes — the high Standard Service rates create real savings opportunity. But always benchmark current competitive offers against the current Standard Service rate before switching. The 60 days before each January 1 / July 1 reset is the most active window.

What's CEAP / CT Fuel Assistance?

Connecticut's federal LIHEAP — 3-tier benefit (Basic + Safety Net + Crisis). Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Apply through your local Community Action Agency. CT CEAP guide →

How does Eversource New Start work?

Income-qualified CT customers with arrears can enroll. Must make on-time payments going forward; earned credits forgive accumulated arrears over 24 months. Often $2,000–$5,000 in forgiven debt. Apply through Eversource.

How do I find my account number?

On your Eversource or UI bill. EnergizeCT enrollment requires it.

Can renters shop?

Yes if the electric account is in your name.

What's the variable-rate cap?

115% of trailing-12-month Standard Service. Doesn't prevent month-to-month spikes — limits the average.

Are there CT-specific solar programs?

Yes — Connecticut Green Bank residential solar programs and community solar pilots. Income-qualified residents can access subsidized enrollment.
