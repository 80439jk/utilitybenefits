New York Electricity: ESCO Shopping After the 2024 PSC Reforms

New York has retail electricity choice through ESCOs (Energy Service Companies). The PSC tightened the rules significantly in 2024 — fewer aggressive doorknockers, but consumers still need to read the fine print.

Quick Answer · Speakable schema

New York has a deregulated retail electricity market. Shop on NY PSC Power to Choose — the state PUC's apples-to-apples site. Current 12-month fixed-rate plans range 11.5 – 14.5¢/kWh. Compare against your utility's default-supply rate before switching. New York's default-supply rate is set by your utility (Con Ed, NYSEG, National Grid, RG&E, Central Hudson, O&R) and resets quarterly. Default rates have been competitive in some periods and high in others — always benchmark before shopping.

What makes New York different

New York's ESCO market has had a turbulent history. Multiple PSC investigations through the 2010s found that residential ESCO customers paid $1.2 billion more over a 5-year period than they would have on default supply. The 2024 PSC reforms tightened ESCO rules significantly: aggressive marketing limits, a residential rate cap requirement (ESCO rates capped at 105% of default-supply on a trailing-12-month basis for non-renewable plans), and stronger disclosure requirements.

The reforms have changed the math. As of 2026, ESCO shopping in NY can save real money for some households (especially Con Ed customers in summer when default rates spike), but the savings ceiling is lower than in TX or PA. For many NY households, the right answer is to stay on default supply and focus on TOU enrollment, efficiency, and assistance.

New York utilities (these stay the same; only your supplier changes)

  • Con Edison (Con Ed) — NYC + Westchester
  • National Grid — Upstate NY (Albany, Syracuse, Buffalo)
  • NYSEG (New York State Electric & Gas) — Various Upstate
  • RG&E (Rochester Gas & Electric) — Rochester area
  • Central Hudson — Hudson Valley
  • Orange & Rockland (O&R) — Lower Hudson Valley
  • PSEG Long Island — Long Island (limited shopping)

New York-specific things to watch for

  • The ESCO consumer history. NY ESCOs have the most fraught regulatory history of any state. The 2024 reforms help but don't eliminate predatory pricing. Always cross-check with the PSC site.
  • Door-to-door enrollment. Tightened in 2024 but still legal. If a salesperson at your door asks to "see your bill to verify your account," shut the door.
  • The 105% cap is on a trailing average. Doesn't prevent month-to-month spikes. Read the fine print.
  • Renewable plans are exempt from the cap. 100% renewable ESCOs can charge any rate, sometimes 30–50% above default supply. Read carefully.
  • Long Island has limited choice. PSEG Long Island operates as a quasi-utility — fewer retail options than the rest of the state.

How to shop in New York

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to NY PSC Power to Choose and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

New York electricity FAQ

Did the 2024 PSC reforms make ESCOs safer?

Meaningfully, yes. The 105% cap, marketing restrictions, and disclosure improvements have reduced the worst abuses. But ESCOs are still not always cheaper than default supply, and renewable plans are exempt from the cap.

How do I find my account number?

On your Con Ed / NYSEG / National Grid bill. PSC Power to Choose enrollment requires it.

Should I shop or stay on default supply?

It depends on your utility and season. Con Ed default supply spikes in summer, so Con Ed customers benefit from shopping more than upstate NY customers. Always compare current ESCO offers against your utility's current default-supply rate before switching.

What's NY HEAP?

New York's federal LIHEAP — Home Energy Assistance Program. Annual benefits + emergency + equipment-repair components. Income limits at 60% State Median Income. Apply through HRA (NYC) or DSS (rest of state). NY HEAP guide →

Are there NY-specific income discount programs?

Con Ed and NYSEG run "Energy Affordability Programs" with monthly bill credits for income-qualified households. Stacks with HEAP. Apply through the utility.

What about community solar in NY?

NY has an active community solar market — many residents can enroll for 5–10% bill credits without rooftop installation. Income-qualified residents can enroll in NY-Sun Solar for All at deeper discounts. More on community solar

Can I switch if I'm on a payment plan?

Most ESCOs won't enroll customers in active payment plans or with arrears. Address the arrears first via HEAP, your utility's hardship program, or 211 NY referrals.

What's CDG?

Community Distributed Generation — NY's term for community solar. Lets you subscribe to a share of an off-site solar project for bill credits. Available across most of NY.
