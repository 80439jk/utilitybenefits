Delaware Electricity: Delmarva, the Co-op, and Modest Shopping Savings

Delaware has retail electricity choice for Delmarva Power customers. The Delaware Electric Cooperative serves rural areas with member-owned rates. Shopping savings are modest in DE; assistance programs (DE LIHEAP) often deliver larger annual benefits.

SKBy Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Delaware has a deregulated retail electricity market. Shop on DE PSC Shopping Info — the state PUC's apples-to-apples site. Current 12-month fixed-rate plans range 11.0 – 13.5¢/kWh. Compare against your utility's default-supply rate before switching. Delaware Standard Offer Service (SOS) is set via competitive procurement by Delmarva Power. SOS rates reset every 6 months. DE SOS has historically been competitive — shopping savings are typically modest (5–10%). Delaware Electric Cooperative serves rural DE with member-owned rates that aren't part of the deregulated market.

What makes Delaware different

Delaware is one of the smaller deregulated markets — fewer competitive suppliers, less aggressive shopping infrastructure, and modest savings opportunities for most households. For Delmarva Power customers, retail shopping can save 5–10% versus SOS but often less.

For income-qualified DE households, federal LIHEAP at 200% FPL is unusually generous (DE has one of the highest income thresholds in the country). Combined with the Good Neighbor Energy Fund, annual benefits often exceed $1,000 — typically larger than retail shopping savings. DE LIHEAP guide →

Delaware Electric Cooperative members can't shop — they're served by their member-owned coop with member-set rates. Some DE municipal utilities (Newark, etc.) similarly don't allow shopping.

Delaware utilities (these stay the same; only your supplier changes)

  • Delmarva Power — Most of DE — Wilmington, Dover, Sussex County
  • Delaware Electric Cooperative — Rural DE — member-owned, not deregulated
  • Delaware Municipal Electric Corp. — Various municipal — not deregulated

Delaware-specific things to watch for

  • Delaware Electric Coop members can't shop. Confirm whether you're with Delmarva or the coop before assuming you can switch.
  • Modest shopping savings. SOS is competitively procured. Don't expect TX-level savings.
  • 200% FPL LIHEAP threshold. Among the country's highest. Many "too high for assistance elsewhere" households qualify in DE.
  • Door-to-door enrollment. Limited but not absent. Verify via DE PSC.

How to shop in Delaware

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to DE PSC Shopping Info and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

If you're income-qualified for assistance

Most Delaware households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level qualify for at least one assistance program. These often beat retail shopping savings:

  • LIHEAP — federal energy assistance, $300–$1,500/year typical. Delaware LIHEAP guide →
  • State or utility-specific discount programs — varies by state. CARE/FERA in CA, PIPP in OH/IL, USF in NJ, CAP rates in PA, EAP in NH.
  • Crisis funds — for shutoff threats and emergencies.

Delaware electricity FAQ

Am I eligible to shop in Delaware?

If you're a Delmarva Power customer, yes. If you're a Delaware Electric Cooperative member or with a municipal utility (Newark, Dover, etc.), no.

How much can I save by shopping in DE?

Typically 5–10% versus Delmarva SOS. Smaller savings than larger deregulated markets. Always benchmark against current SOS.

What's DE LIHEAP's income threshold?

200% of the federal poverty level — among the most generous in the country. Many households who don't qualify in PA or NJ do qualify in DE. DE LIHEAP guide →

How does Delmarva SOS reset?

Every 6 months — June 1 and December 1. Shop in the 60 days before reset for best comparison.

Is the Delaware Electric Coop deregulated?

No. Member-owned cooperatives aren't part of the deregulated market. Member rates are set by the coop board.

Can renters shop in DE?

Yes if your account is with Delmarva and in your name.

What's the Good Neighbor Energy Fund?

Statewide hardship fund administered by Catholic Charities Delaware. Provides emergency utility assistance for income-qualified households. Stacks with LIHEAP.

Are there DE community solar programs?

Yes — Delaware Sustainable Energy Utility (SEU) operates community solar programs with income-qualified subscriptions. Contact SEU for current availability.
