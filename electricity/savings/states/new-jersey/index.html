New Jersey Electricity: BPU Shopping, Default Supply, and USF

New Jersey has a deregulated retail electricity market via the BPU. Income-qualified households should also know about USF (Universal Service Fund), which often beats anything available through retail shopping.

Quick Answer · Speakable schema

New Jersey has a deregulated retail electricity market. Shop on NJ BPU Shopping — the state PUC's apples-to-apples site. Current 12-month fixed-rate plans range 10.5 – 13.5¢/kWh. Compare against your utility's default-supply rate before switching. New Jersey calls default supply Basic Generation Service (BGS), set via annual BPU auction. Each utility (PSE&G, JCP&L, Atlantic City Electric, Rockland Electric) has its own BGS rate that resets June 1. Many residential BGS rates have been competitive in recent years — meaning shopping doesn't always save money in NJ.

What makes New Jersey different

The most important program for many NJ households isn't shopping — it's USF (Universal Service Fund), a state-funded discount of up to $150/month on gas and electric bills for income-qualified residents. Combined with federal LIHEA, qualifying households can receive over $1,000/year in stacked benefits — typically larger than any retail shopping savings.

For non-income-qualified households, the BGS auction model means default supply is often competitive. Shopping savings in NJ are typically modest (5–15%) compared to TX or PA. The 60 days before BGS reset (June 1 each year) is the most active shopping window.

New Jersey utilities (these stay the same; only your supplier changes)

  • PSE&G (Public Service Electric & Gas) — Most of NJ — Newark, Trenton, central NJ
  • JCP&L (Jersey Central Power & Light) — Northern and Central NJ
  • Atlantic City Electric — South Jersey
  • Rockland Electric — Northwest NJ

New Jersey-specific things to watch for

  • BGS rate annual reset. June 1 each year. Don't compare retail offers to last year's BGS rate; compare to the new one.
  • USF eligibility cliff. If you qualify for USF, that's almost always better than shopping. Check before switching.
  • Door-to-door enrollment. NJ tightened rules in 2022 but they persist. Verify any pitch via the BPU site.
  • Slamming complaints. NJ has had multiple AG enforcement actions against suppliers who switched customers without authorization. Check your bill monthly.

How to shop in New Jersey

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to NJ BPU Shopping and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

If you're income-qualified for assistance

Most New Jersey households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level qualify for at least one assistance program. These often beat retail shopping savings:

  • LIHEAP — federal energy assistance, $300–$1,500/year typical. New Jersey LIHEAP guide →
  • State or utility-specific discount programs — varies by state. CARE/FERA in CA, PIPP in OH/IL, USF in NJ, CAP rates in PA, EAP in NH.
  • Crisis funds — for shutoff threats and emergencies.

New Jersey electricity FAQ

Should I shop or stay on BGS?

Compare. NJ BGS is set via competitive auction and is often hard to beat. The 60 days before June 1 reset is the most active shopping window.

How does USF work?

New Jersey Universal Service Fund — monthly bill credit of up to $150 for income-qualified gas and electric households. Apply through DCA. Combined with federal LIHEA, can exceed $1,000/year. USF + LIHEA guide →

Can I be on USF AND a competitive supplier?

USF applies to your default-supply portion. If you switch to a competitive supplier, the USF interaction is complex — usually USF customers should stay on default supply with the USF discount. Talk to DCA before switching.

What's the difference between PSE&G and JCP&L for shopping?

Different default-supply benchmarks (separate BGS auctions per utility) and different territories. Same retail providers serve both. Compare against your specific utility's BGS rate.

How do I find my account number?

On your PSE&G, JCP&L, ACE, or RECO bill. NJ BPU shopping site requires it.

Are renters in NJ allowed to shop?

Yes, if your electric account is in your name. Renters with utilities in their name have full shopping rights.

What's BGS-RSCP vs BGS-CIEP?

Two BGS products — RSCP (Residential Small Commercial) covers most homes; CIEP (Commercial & Industrial Energy Pricing) is for larger users. Residential customers care about the RSCP rate.

How does Solar for All apply in NJ?

NJ has a Community Solar Energy Program — income-qualified residents can enroll for bill credits typically reducing electricity costs by $300–$700/year. Stacks with USF.
