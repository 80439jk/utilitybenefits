Rhode Island Electricity: RI Energy, Last Resort, and the A-60 Rate

Rhode Island has retail electricity choice for Rhode Island Energy customers. The A-60 income-qualified discount rate often beats shopping savings for eligible households.

SKBy Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Rhode Island has a deregulated retail electricity market. Shop on RI PUC Shopping Info — the state PUC's apples-to-apples site. Current 12-month fixed-rate plans range 13.0 – 15.5¢/kWh. Compare against your utility's default-supply rate before switching. Rhode Island Last Resort Service is procured by Rhode Island Energy via PUC auction. Rates reset every 6 months — typically January 1 and July 1. RI Last Resort has been competitive in recent years; shopping savings are modest (5–15%) for non-A-60 households.

What makes Rhode Island different

Rhode Island Energy (the renamed National Grid RI) operates an A-60 discount rate for income-qualified residential customers — a permanent rate-tier reduction that reduces both gas and electric bills throughout the year. Apply through Rhode Island Energy with proof of LIHEAP enrollment or income documentation. A-60 is typically larger savings than retail shopping for eligible households.

RI also has one of the strongest winter shutoff protection rules in New England: blanket protection November 1 through April 15 for protected households (income-qualified, seriously ill, elderly, families with infants).

For non-A-60 households, shopping savings vs. Last Resort Service are modest (5–15%). RI is one of the smaller deregulated markets — fewer competitive suppliers than larger states.

Rhode Island utilities (these stay the same; only your supplier changes)

  • Rhode Island Energy (formerly National Grid RI) — Most of RI
  • Pascoag Utility District — Pascoag — not deregulated
  • Block Island Power Company — Block Island — not deregulated

Rhode Island-specific things to watch for

  • A-60 eligibility cliff. If you qualify, this beats shopping. Apply through RI Energy.
  • Modest shopping savings. Last Resort is competitively procured.
  • Pascoag and Block Island can't shop. Municipal utilities exempt from deregulation.
  • Door-to-door enrollment. Limited but not absent. Verify via PUC.

How to shop in Rhode Island

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to RI PUC Shopping Info and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

If you're income-qualified for assistance

Most Rhode Island households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level qualify for at least one assistance program. These often beat retail shopping savings:

  • LIHEAP — federal energy assistance, $300–$1,500/year typical. Rhode Island LIHEAP guide →
  • State or utility-specific discount programs — varies by state. CARE/FERA in CA, PIPP in OH/IL, USF in NJ, CAP rates in PA, EAP in NH.
  • Crisis funds — for shutoff threats and emergencies.

Rhode Island electricity FAQ

How does A-60 work?

Rhode Island Energy's permanent income-qualified rate-tier reduction. Reduces both gas and electric bills throughout the year. Apply through RI Energy with LIHEAP enrollment or income documentation. RI assistance guide →

Should I shop or apply for A-60?

If you might qualify for A-60, do that first. For non-eligible households, retail shopping can save 5–15% vs. Last Resort Service.

How do I find my RI Energy account number?

On your RI Energy bill. PUC shopping resources reference it.

Can renters shop?

Yes if your account is with RI Energy and in your name.

What's RI HEAP?

Rhode Island's federal LIHEAP — administered through OER and 5 community action agencies. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. RI LIHEAP guide →

How does the winter shutoff protection work?

Blanket protection November 1 through April 15 for protected households (income-qualified, seriously ill, elderly, families with infants under 2). Self-certify with RI Energy to claim.

Are there RI community solar programs?

Yes — Renewable Energy Growth program and community solar pilots. Income-qualified subscriptions provide bill credits.

What if I'm with Pascoag or Block Island?

Those municipal utilities aren't deregulated. Your only option is the municipal rate.
