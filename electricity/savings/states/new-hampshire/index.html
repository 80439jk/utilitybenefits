New Hampshire Electricity: Default Service, EAP, and the Discount Tiers

New Hampshire has retail electricity choice. The state-funded Electric Assistance Program (EAP) provides year-round monthly discounts of 8–76% on electric bills — often the largest savings opportunity for income-qualified NH households.

SKBy Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

New Hampshire has a deregulated retail electricity market. Shop on NH PUC Shopping — the state PUC's apples-to-apples site. Current 12-month fixed-rate plans range 12.0 – 15.0¢/kWh. Compare against your utility's default-supply rate before switching. New Hampshire calls default supply Default Service. Each utility (Eversource, Unitil, Liberty, NHEC) procures Default Service via PUC-overseen auction — most reset every 6 months. Default Service rates have varied widely; always benchmark current rates before switching.

What makes New Hampshire different

NH operates the strongest state-funded electric discount program in the country: EAP (Electric Assistance Program). EAP applies a year-round monthly discount of 8% to 76% to your electric bill, varying by income tier. Tier 1 households (≤75% FPL) get a 76% discount — for a typical $200/month bill, that's $152/month or $1,824/year in savings. Even Tier 6 (200% FPL) gets 8% off.

Most low-income NH households should be enrolled in BOTH Fuel Assistance (federal LIHEAP for heating fuel) AND EAP (state-funded electric discount). Combined annual benefits often exceed $2,000. NH FAP + EAP guide →

For non-EAP households, shopping savings in NH are modest. Default Service is competitively procured; retail offers typically beat it by 5–15%. The 60 days before each Default Service reset is the most active shopping window.

New Hampshire utilities (these stay the same; only your supplier changes)

  • Eversource NH — Most of NH electric
  • Unitil — Seacoast / Capital area electric
  • Liberty Utilities — Western NH
  • New Hampshire Electric Cooperative (NHEC) — Rural NH — member-owned

New Hampshire-specific things to watch for

  • EAP is enormous. Up to 76% off for Tier 1. If you might qualify, this beats any shopping savings.
  • NHEC members can't shop. Member-owned coop with member-set rates.
  • Default Service resets vary by utility. Eversource and Unitil typically reset every 6 months; some others quarterly.
  • Oil heat dominates rural NH. Like Maine, NH's biggest energy bill is often fuel oil, not electric.

How to shop in New Hampshire

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to NH PUC Shopping and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

If you're income-qualified for assistance

Most New Hampshire households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level qualify for at least one assistance program. These often beat retail shopping savings:

  • LIHEAP — federal energy assistance, $300–$1,500/year typical. New Hampshire LIHEAP guide →
  • State or utility-specific discount programs — varies by state. CARE/FERA in CA, PIPP in OH/IL, USF in NJ, CAP rates in PA, EAP in NH.
  • Crisis funds — for shutoff threats and emergencies.

New Hampshire electricity FAQ

How does EAP work?

New Hampshire's state-funded Electric Assistance Program — year-round monthly discount of 8–76% on your electric bill, depending on income tier. Tier 1 (≤75% FPL) gets 76% off. Apply through your local Community Action Agency. NH EAP guide →

How does EAP differ from Fuel Assistance?

Fuel Assistance is federal LIHEAP for heating fuel (one-time annual benefit). EAP is state-funded for electric (year-round monthly discount). They stack — most low-income NH households should apply for both at the same CAA.

Should I shop or apply for EAP?

If you might qualify for any EAP tier, do that first — even Tier 6 (200% FPL) beats most shopping savings. After EAP, retail shopping is the next layer.

Can NHEC members get EAP?

Yes — EAP applies regardless of utility. Apply through your CAA. NHEC then implements the discount on your member bill.

Where do I find my NH electric account number?

On your Eversource, Unitil, Liberty, or NHEC bill. PUC shopping page has supplier-specific enrollment info.

Are renters eligible to shop?

Yes if your account is in your name (Eversource, Unitil, Liberty). NHEC members can't shop regardless of homeowner/renter status.

What about oil heating in NH?

Most rural NH heats with oil. Fuel Assistance covers oil delivery directly — apply through your CAA. Per-fill benefits often $700–$1,800 for income-qualified households.

How does Eversource Neighbor Helping Neighbor work?

Eversource customer-funded hardship fund — emergency utility assistance for income-qualified Eversource customers. Stacks with EAP and Fuel Assistance.
