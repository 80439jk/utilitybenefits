Washington DC Electricity: Pepco Shopping and UDP Stacking

Washington DC has retail electricity choice for Pepco customers. Income-qualified residents should also enroll in UDP (Utility Discount Program) — a year-round monthly bill credit that often beats retail shopping savings.

SKBy Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Washington DC has a deregulated retail electricity market. Shop on DC PSC Shopping — the state PUC's apples-to-apples site. Current 12-month fixed-rate plans range 11.0 – 13.0¢/kWh. Compare against your utility's default-supply rate before switching. DC default supply is called Standard Offer Service (SOS), procured by Pepco via competitive auction. SOS resets every 6 months. DC SOS has been competitive in recent years — shopping savings are modest (5–15%) for non-UDP households.

What makes DC different

DC has only one electric utility (Pepco) but a deregulated supply market. The most important program for income-qualified DC residents isn't shopping — it's the Utility Discount Program (UDP), a year-round monthly bill credit on Pepco of up to $475/year, plus separate credits on Washington Gas and DC Water. Combined UDP enrollment can exceed $1,200/year in stacked savings — typically larger than any shopping savings.

DC also runs the Solar for All program — income-qualified residents can enroll in community solar at deeply discounted rates, often saving $500/year or more. Stacks with UDP and federal LIHEAP. DC LIHEAP + UDP guide →

For non-income-qualified residents, shopping savings vs. SOS are modest (5–15%) but still real. The 60 days before each January 1 / July 1 SOS reset is the most active window.

Washington DC-specific things to watch for

  • UDP eligibility cliff. If you qualify for UDP, that beats shopping. Apply through DOEE first.
  • SOS is competitively procured. Often hard to beat — modest shopping savings.
  • Solar for All for income-qualified residents — stacks with UDP and LIHEAP.
  • Single utility means simpler shopping than multi-utility states.

How to shop in Washington DC

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to DC PSC Shopping and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

If you're income-qualified for assistance

Most Washington DC households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level qualify for at least one assistance program. These often beat retail shopping savings:

  • LIHEAP — federal energy assistance, $300–$1,500/year typical. Washington DC LIHEAP guide →
  • State or utility-specific discount programs — varies by state. CARE/FERA in CA, PIPP in OH/IL, USF in NJ, CAP rates in PA, EAP in NH.
  • Crisis funds — for shutoff threats and emergencies.

Washington DC electricity FAQ

How does UDP work?

DC Utility Discount Program — year-round monthly bill credits on Pepco (up to $475/yr), Washington Gas (up to $276/yr), and DC Water (up to $480/yr). Apply once through DOEE; covers all three. Combined value over $1,200/year. DC LIHEAP + UDP guide →

Should I shop or stay on SOS?

Compare. DC SOS is competitively procured. Shopping savings are typically 5–15% — meaningful but smaller than TX or PA.

What's Solar for All in DC?

DOEE program providing free or discounted community solar enrollment for income-qualified DC residents — typically reduces electric bills by $500+/year. Stacks with UDP and LIHEAP.

How do I find my Pepco account number?

On your Pepco bill. DC PSC shopping site requires it.

Can renters shop in DC?

Yes if your Pepco account is in your name.

What's the income limit for DC LIHEAP?

200% of the federal poverty level — among the most generous in the country. Many households qualify. DC LIHEAP guide →

Is door-to-door enrollment legal in DC?

Restricted; PSC scrutiny is high. If pitched at your door, verify via the PSC site before signing.

How does DC Sustainable Energy Utility (DCSEU) help?

DCSEU runs energy efficiency programs — free LED bulbs, low-income weatherization, smart thermostat rebates. Stacks with everything else.
