Maine Electricity: CMP, Versant, and the Standard Offer Auction

Maine has retail electricity choice via the PUC. CMP and Versant Power are the two major utilities. Standard Offer rates are set via PUC auction and reset annually each March 1.

Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Maine has a deregulated retail electricity market. Shop on Maine PUC Shopping — the state PUC's apples-to-apples site. Current 12-month fixed-rate plans range 13.0 – 16.0¢/kWh. Compare against your utility's default-supply rate before switching. Maine Standard Offer is set via PUC-overseen annual auction. Resets every March 1. Standard Offer rates have been competitive in some years and high in others — always benchmark before shopping. Versant Power Standard Offer often runs higher than CMP because of higher procurement costs in northern Maine.

What makes Maine different

Maine has the highest oil-heating share in the country — over 60% of households heat with oil rather than natural gas or electric. That means Maine's biggest energy bill isn't electric, it's fuel oil. Standard Offer electric rates have been moderate to high in recent years (10–17¢/kWh varying by season), but the headline savings story for most Maine households is on the heating side.

For income-qualified ME households, HEAP (Home Energy Assistance Program) covers oil delivery directly — per-fill benefits often exceed $1,200. Joe-4-Oil and Operation Warm Heart are state and nonprofit hardship layers. ME HEAP guide →

For electric shopping specifically, CMP customers see more savings opportunity than Versant customers because CMP Standard Offer is typically lower, leaving less room for retail to beat. Always compare against the current Standard Offer rate for your utility.

Maine utilities (these stay the same; only your supplier changes)

  • Central Maine Power (CMP, Avangrid) — Southern and central Maine
  • Versant Power — Northern Maine and Bangor area
  • Maine consumer-owned utilities (Houlton Water, Madison Electric) — Limited; not deregulated

Maine-specific things to watch for

  • Annual Standard Offer reset (March 1). Different rhythm than most states. The 60 days before March 1 is the most active shopping window.
  • Oil heating dominates. Electric shopping savings are capped by usage. Maine households often save more by switching oil providers than electric.
  • CMP service quality. CMP has had multiple PUC complaints over billing accuracy. Verify your bill if numbers seem off.
  • Versant rates often higher. Northern Maine procurement is more expensive.

How to shop in Maine

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to Maine PUC Shopping and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

If you're income-qualified for assistance

Most Maine households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level qualify for at least one assistance program. These often beat retail shopping savings:

  • LIHEAP — federal energy assistance, $300–$1,500/year typical. Maine LIHEAP guide →
  • State or utility-specific discount programs — varies by state. CARE/FERA in CA, PIPP in OH/IL, USF in NJ, CAP rates in PA, EAP in NH.
  • Crisis funds — for shutoff threats and emergencies.

Maine electricity FAQ

Why is electric shopping less of a priority in ME?

Most Maine households heat with oil, not electric. The biggest energy bill is fuel oil. HEAP enrollment, oil price shopping, and weatherization typically save more than electric shopping. ME HEAP →

When does Standard Offer reset?

March 1 each year. The 60 days before March 1 is the best window for shopping comparisons.

How do I find my CMP or Versant account number?

On your utility bill. Maine PUC's shopping page provides links to the active competitive supplier list.

Can I switch oil providers?

Yes. Maine has dozens of oil dealers; rates vary meaningfully. Many participate in HEAP direct-pay if you're enrolled. Shop by phoning 3–5 dealers in your area for current per-gallon pricing.

What's Joe-4-Oil?

Maine's emergency oil delivery program — provides 100-gallon delivery for households facing empty tanks. Apply through your CAA. Stacks with HEAP.

Are there ME-specific solar programs?

Yes — Net Energy Billing for community solar; income-qualified subscriptions available. Reduces electric bills typically by $300–$700/year.

Does Maine have a winter shutoff moratorium?

PUC winter rules from November 15 through April 15 require regulated utilities to offer payment plans and avoid disconnections for income-qualified residential customers.

Can renters shop in Maine?

Yes if your account is with CMP or Versant and in your name.
