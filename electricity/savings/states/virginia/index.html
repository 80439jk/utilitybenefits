Virginia Electricity: Limited Choice, Dominion Default, and 100% Renewable Shopping

Virginia has technically retained electricity deregulation but in practice most residential customers can only shop for 100% renewable plans. The 2007 reforms re-regulated most of the market. Default Dominion supply is what 95% of VA residents pay.

Most VA residents can only choose between Dominion default supply and a 100% renewable alternative supplier.

What makes Virginia different

Virginia's deregulation history is unusual. The state opened to competition in 1999, then largely re-regulated in 2007 after the early competitive market struggled. What remains: Dominion Energy is the default supplier for most VA residents, and the only "choice" available to residential customers is to switch to a 100% renewable alternative supplier (e.g., Calpine, Direct Energy 100% renewable plans). Standard non-renewable choice is closed for residential.

For most VA residents, the savings track is:

  • Dominion's TOU rates — optional time-of-use plans for residential customers. Best for EV households and flexible schedules.
  • Efficiency rebates — Dominion + federal IRA stacking. Heat pumps, smart thermostats, weatherization.
  • EnergyShare — Dominion customer-funded hardship fund. Combined with federal LIHEAP.
  • VA Fuel Assistance + Crisis — federal LIHEAP. Income-qualified. VA assistance guide →

Renewable shopping in VA: usually a 1–2¢ premium for 100% renewable plans. Fine if green sourcing matters to you, but rarely a savings play.

Virginia-specific things to watch for

  • Most VA shopping is renewable-only. Don't expect price-competitive offers.
  • Co-op members can't shop. Old Dominion Electric Cooperative members are served by their coop.
  • Dominion TOU is widely available. Often the best single savings move.
  • Cold-weather rule. VA has a unique temperature-triggered cold weather rule (≤32°F). Automatic protection during cold snaps.

How to shop in Virginia

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to Virginia SCC Choice Info and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

If you're income-qualified for assistance

Most Virginia households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level qualify for at least one assistance program. These often beat retail shopping savings:

  • LIHEAP — federal energy assistance, $300–$1,500/year typical. Virginia LIHEAP guide →
  • State or utility-specific discount programs — varies by state. CARE/FERA in CA, PIPP in OH/IL, USF in NJ, CAP rates in PA, EAP in NH.
  • Crisis funds — for shutoff threats and emergencies.

Virginia electricity FAQ

Why is electric choice so limited in VA?

Virginia largely re-regulated in 2007 after the early competitive market struggled. Industrial customers retained meaningful choice; residential customers retained only 100% renewable alternative supply.

Can I save money by switching to a renewable supplier?

Usually no — renewable plans are typically 1–2¢/kWh more than Dominion default. Worth it if sourcing matters; rarely a savings play.

What's Dominion EnergyShare?

Dominion customer-funded hardship fund administered with the Salvation Army — emergency utility assistance for income-qualified Dominion customers. Apply through Salvation Army VA.

How does VA's cold weather rule work?

Unique temperature-triggered rule — utilities cannot disconnect residential customers when forecast lows are 32°F or below. Automatic protection. Doesn't have to be enrolled in any specific program.

Should I do TOU?

Often yes if you have an EV or smart thermostat. Dominion offers free 1-year TOU pilots — try it and switch back if your bill goes up. Best single savings move for non-shopping VA residents.

How do I find my Dominion account number?

On your Dominion Energy bill. SCC's Choice page links to active alternative suppliers.

What's Virginia LIHEAP?

Federal LIHEAP — Fuel Assistance + Crisis. Income limit: 150% FPL. Administered by DSS. VA LIHEAP guide →

Can I shop if I'm with Appalachian Power?

Limited — same renewable-only restriction as Dominion customers. Most APCo customers stay on default.
