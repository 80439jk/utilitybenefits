Maryland Electricity: MD PSC Shopping, Default Supply, and EUSP

Maryland has retail electricity choice via the PSC. The Electric Universal Service Program (EUSP) provides monthly bill credits for income-qualified households that often beat retail shopping savings.

SKBy Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Maryland has a deregulated retail electricity market. Shop on MD PSC Shopping — the state PUC's apples-to-apples site. Current 12-month fixed-rate plans range 9.5 – 12.5¢/kWh. Compare against your utility's default-supply rate before switching. Maryland default supply is called Standard Offer Service (SOS). Each utility (BGE, Pepco, Delmarva, Potomac Edison) has its own SOS rate that resets every 6 months. MD SOS rates are typically competitive — meaning shopping savings are modest (5–15%) for non-EUSP households.

What makes Maryland different

The most important program for income-qualified MD households is EUSP (Electric Universal Service Program) — a monthly bill credit (typically $30–$50/month) that can be combined with federal LIHEAP. Combined annual benefit can exceed $1,000/year — typically larger than retail shopping savings. MD MEAP + EUSP guide →

For non-EUSP households, shopping savings in MD are modest because SOS rates are procured competitively and often hard to beat. Pepco SOS in particular has been competitive vs. retail offers. The 60 days before each June 1 / December 1 reset is the most active shopping window.

Maryland utilities (these stay the same; only your supplier changes)

  • BGE (Baltimore Gas & Electric) — Greater Baltimore + central MD
  • Pepco — Greater Washington DC suburbs (Montgomery, Prince George's)
  • Delmarva Power — Eastern Shore
  • Potomac Edison (FirstEnergy) — Western MD

Maryland-specific things to watch for

  • EUSP eligibility cliff. If you qualify for EUSP, that's almost always better than shopping.
  • Door-to-door enrollment. MD tightened rules in 2023; PSC complaint volume is still meaningful. Verify via PSC site.
  • Pepco vs. BGE SOS spread. Sometimes one utility's SOS is meaningfully more beatable than the other's. Compare specifically.
  • Cold-call enrollment. Common in MD. If you didn't initiate the call, hang up.

How to shop in Maryland

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to MD PSC Shopping and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

If you're income-qualified for assistance

Most Maryland households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level qualify for at least one assistance program. These often beat retail shopping savings:

  • LIHEAP — federal energy assistance, $300–$1,500/year typical. Maryland LIHEAP guide →
  • State or utility-specific discount programs — varies by state. CARE/FERA in CA, PIPP in OH/IL, USF in NJ, CAP rates in PA, EAP in NH.
  • Crisis funds — for shutoff threats and emergencies.

Maryland electricity FAQ

How does EUSP work?

Maryland Electric Universal Service Program — monthly bill credit ($30–$50 typical) for income-qualified households. Stacks with federal MEAP/LIHEAP. Combined annual benefit can exceed $1,000. MD MEAP + EUSP guide →

Should I shop or stay on SOS?

Compare. MD SOS is competitively procured and often hard to beat — especially Pepco. The 60 days before June 1 / Dec 1 reset is the best shopping window.

How do I find my account number?

On your BGE, Pepco, Delmarva, or Potomac Edison bill. MD PSC shopping site requires it.

What's the difference between BGE and Pepco for shopping?

Different utility territories with different SOS rates. Same retail providers serve both. Compare specifically against your utility's current SOS.

Can renters shop?

Yes if the electric account is in your name.

What's MD MEAP?

Maryland's federal LIHEAP — Maryland Energy Assistance Program. Income-qualified, $300–$1,200 annual benefit. MD MEAP guide →

How does community solar work in MD?

Maryland has an active community solar program — income-qualified residents can subscribe for bill credits typically reducing electric costs by $300–$700/year. Stacks with EUSP.

Are there MD-specific weatherization programs?

Yes — through DHCD with priority for LIHEAP-eligible households. Free home weatherization including insulation, sealing, sometimes appliance replacement.
