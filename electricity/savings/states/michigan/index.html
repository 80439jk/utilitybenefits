Michigan Electricity: Limited Choice, DTE/Consumers Default, and MEAP

Michigan has only a limited deregulated market — capped at 10% of each utility's load via the AES Choice program. Most MI residents can't shop. The savings track is mostly TOU rates, efficiency, and MEAP for income-qualified households.

What makes Michigan different

Michigan is technically deregulated but the legislature capped competitive electric supply at 10% of each utility's load. This means most Michigan residents can't actually shop — they're on a multi-year wait list maintained by DTE and Consumers. Even households who get off the wait list often find limited competitive offers.

For the 90% of MI residents who can't shop, savings come from:

  • TOU (time-of-use) rates — DTE and Consumers offer optional TOU plans. Best for households with EVs, smart thermostats, or flexible schedules.
  • Efficiency rebates — federal IRA tax credits + utility rebates for heat pumps, smart thermostats, weatherization.
  • MEAP assistance — Michigan Energy Assistance Program for income-qualified households. MEAP guide →
  • DTE Low-Income Self-Sufficiency Plan (LSP) — caps electric bill at 6% of household income for qualifying households. Often the largest single savings opportunity.

Michigan utilities (these stay the same; only your supplier changes)

  • DTE Energy — Detroit metro and southeast MI
  • Consumers Energy — Most of Lower Peninsula outside Detroit
  • Indiana Michigan Power (AEP) — Berrien and Cass counties
  • Upper Peninsula Power Company (Upper Peninsula) — UP — not part of choice market

Michigan-specific things to watch for

  • 10% Choice cap. Most MI residents can't shop. Check the wait list status before assuming you can.
  • DTE LSP. 6%-of-income electric cap for qualifying customers. Often massive savings.
  • UP Power and rural coops. Not part of the Choice market. Member rates only.
  • TOU rates are widely available. Better for most households than expecting to shop.

How to shop in Michigan

  1. Pull a recent utility bill (you'll need your account number).
  2. Calculate your average monthly kWh from the last 12 months.
  3. Go to MPSC AES Choice Info and enter your service address.
  4. Filter for fixed-rate, 12 or 24 month, and sort by cost at your usage tier.
  5. Read the Electricity Facts Label for the top 3 options. Confirm rate at YOUR tier, base charge, ETF, and post-contract default rate.
  6. Enroll. Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before contract end.

If you're income-qualified for assistance

Most Michigan households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level qualify for at least one assistance program. These often beat retail shopping savings:

  • LIHEAP — federal energy assistance, $300–$1,500/year typical. Michigan LIHEAP guide →
  • State or utility-specific discount programs — varies by state. CARE/FERA in CA, PIPP in OH/IL, USF in NJ, CAP rates in PA, EAP in NH.
  • Crisis funds — for shutoff threats and emergencies.

Michigan electricity FAQ

Can I really not shop for electric supply in MI?

If you're on DTE or Consumers and not in the 10% Choice pool, you can't shop. You can join the wait list at MPSC. Wait times are often years.

How do I get on the AES Choice wait list?

Contact your utility (DTE or Consumers) or check MPSC. Each utility maintains its own list.

What's DTE LSP?

DTE Energy's Low-Income Self-Sufficiency Plan — caps your electric bill at 6% of household income. Apply through DTE with income documentation. Often the largest single savings opportunity for income-qualified DTE customers.

What about Consumers Energy?

Consumers has CARE — Customer Assistance and Recovery Effort — similar concept to LSP but structured differently. Apply through Consumers Energy.

How do I apply for MEAP?

Through Michigan DHHS or your local Community Action Agency. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. MEAP guide →

Are TOU rates worth it?

Often yes — DTE and Consumers offer optional TOU plans. Best for households with EVs, smart thermostats, or schedule flexibility. Both utilities allow free 1-year TOU pilots.

What about Upper Peninsula?

UP utilities aren't part of the Choice market. UP residents are stuck with their local utility — typically Upper Peninsula Power or Marquette BLP.

Can renters shop?

Same answer as homeowners — only 10% have access via Choice. Most can't.
