Electric Bill Assistance: Programs You May Qualify For in 2026

Federal, state, utility-specific, and demographic-targeted programs that help cover or reduce your electric bill. Most households qualify for more than one. Most don't realize it.

SKBy Sara Khan, Senior Editor · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer

The main federal program is LIHEAP, paying $400–$1,500/yr to households under 150% of the federal poverty level. On top of that, every state has its own programs (CARE, PIPP, USF, CAP) that often pay more than LIHEAP and have higher income limits. Utilities themselves run hardship programs (Reliant CARE, TXU Energy Aid, etc.). Seniors, veterans, and people on disability often qualify for additional discounts. The fastest way to see what fits your household is the eligibility check.

Federal programs

⭐ LIHEAP — Heating & Cooling Assistance

The federal backbone. $400–$1,500/year for heating, cooling, and crisis assistance. Available in every state.
Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP)

Free home energy improvements (insulation, sealing, HVAC) for qualifying low-income households. Reduces your bill long-term.
Federal IRA tax credits

Up to $14,000 in federal tax credits and rebates for heat pumps, electric appliances, and home efficiency upgrades.

State programs

State assistance is where the real money often lives. The programs below are in addition to federal LIHEAP — most states layer them. Programs vary by state administration but the categories are consistent:

CARE / FERA (California)

30–35% bill discount on PG&E, SCE, SDG&E. Income-based, easier to qualify than LIHEAP for many households.
PIPP / PIPP Plus (OH, PA, IL, NJ)

Caps your electric bill at a percentage of household income. For some households, much bigger than LIHEAP.
USF (New Jersey)

Universal Service Fund — a monthly credit on your gas/electric bill. Often more searched than "LIHEAP" in NJ.
CAP rates (Pennsylvania)

Customer Assistance Program — utility-administered discounted rate tier for qualifying low-income customers.
Medical Baseline (CA, NV, others)

Discounted rates for households where someone has a qualifying medical condition requiring electricity.

Demographic-targeted discounts

Senior discounts (60+)

Many utilities offer senior-specific rates. Some are automatic; most require you to apply.
Veteran benefits

VA-related programs, state veteran utility funds, and utility-specific veteran discounts.
Disability assistance

SSI/SSDI recipients, medical equipment users, and households with disabled members often qualify for additional aid.
Tribal energy programs

Specific federal funding streams for tribal members and reservation households.

Crisis & shutoff prevention

Facing shutoff right now?

Most states have crisis funds available year-round. Many utilities can stop a disconnection within 24 hours. Call us or use the calculator and select "shutoff notice."

LIHEAP Crisis component

The emergency arm of LIHEAP — funded year-round in most states for shutoff threats and broken heat/cooling.
Salvation Army utility help

Local chapters often have utility funds when state programs are tapped out. Highly variable by city.
Dollar Energy Fund

Utility-funded hardship grants in PA, OH, WV, MD, and parts of IN, KY, VA. Often pays off arrears.
211 referrals

Dial 211 from any phone for local emergency utility assistance and social service referrals.

Utility-specific hardship programs

Almost every major utility runs its own hardship fund — most are paid for by donations from other customers. They're under-publicized, easy to miss, and often the fastest source of help when state programs are slow.
