North Carolina LIEAP and CIP: Heating Assistance and Crisis Intervention

NC's LIEAP (Low Income Energy Assistance Program) is the federal LIHEAP. CIP (Crisis Intervention Program) handles emergencies year-round. Apply through your county DSS or NCWorks.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

LIEAP (Low Income Energy Assistance Program) is North Carolina's federal LIHEAP — a one-time annual cash benefit of $300–$600 for households at or below 130% of the federal poverty level. CIP (Crisis Intervention Program) provides up to $600 per crisis episode year-round for households facing shutoff or with no working heat or AC. Apply through your county Department of Social Services. Major utilities (Duke Energy Progress, Duke Energy Carolinas) run their own hardship programs that stack.

Why 130% FPL instead of 150%?

North Carolina sets its LIEAP threshold at 130% FPL — slightly below the federal default of 150% — to stretch limited annual funding across more applications. If your household income is between 130% and 150% FPL (above LIEAP cutoff but below the federal max), CIP for documented emergencies and utility-specific hardship programs (Duke Share the Light, Piedmont Natural Gas Share the Warmth) may still apply.

North Carolina eligibility

North Carolina uses 130% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in North Carolina

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

North Carolina-specific FAQ

When does LIEAP open in North Carolina?

December 1 for general applications and December 1 for vulnerable households (60+, disabled). Application window through March 31. Funds can deplete before March.

How is CIP different from LIEAP?

LIEAP is a one-time annual benefit. CIP is for emergency situations (shutoff, no fuel, no working heat or AC) and is available year-round. Both administered through county DSS.

What's the income limit?

130% of federal poverty level — about $19,500/yr for 1 person, $40,500/yr for 4 people. Lower than the typical 150% FPL standard.

Where do I apply?

Through your county Department of Social Services (DSS). All 100 NC counties have DSS offices. Find yours at ncdhhs.gov.

Can I get LIEAP if I rent?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder to access but CIP for emergencies may still apply.

Does LIEAP cover propane?

Yes. LIEAP covers all heating fuels including propane and oil. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is Duke Share the Light?

Duke Energy's customer-funded emergency assistance program — provides one-time grants for income-qualified Duke customers facing utility hardship. Apply through Salvation Army.

Does NC have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No statutory moratorium. NC PSC encourages payment plans for income-qualified customers but utilities can disconnect for non-payment year-round. CIP enrollment provides the practical protection.
