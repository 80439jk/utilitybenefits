Alabama LIHEAP: Heating, Cooling, and Crisis Assistance

Alabama's federal LIHEAP is administered through DECA and 18 local Community Action Agencies. Both heating and cooling components, with major utility hardship programs from Alabama Power and Spire Gas Alabama.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Alabama's federal LIHEAP is administered through DECA by 18 local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 150% FPL. Annual benefits typically $200–$700, split between heating (October-March) and cooling (June-September) seasons. Major utilities — Alabama Power, Spire Gas Alabama, Tennessee Valley Authority distributors — run additional hardship programs.

Alabama's heating and cooling demand

Alabama has mild winters but hot, humid summers — cooling demand often exceeds heating. Alabama Power serves most of the state for electricity; Spire Gas serves natural gas customers; rural areas often use propane or electric heat. LIHEAP funds in Alabama can be applied to either heating or cooling. Most demand comes from cooling crises during severe heat waves.

Alabama eligibility

Alabama uses 150% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Alabama

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Alabama-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in Alabama?

Through your local Community Action Agency. 18 agencies cover all AL counties. Find yours at adeca.alabama.gov.

When does Alabama LIHEAP open?

Year-round. Heating-season focus October through March; cooling-season focus June through September.

Does Alabama have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No statutory moratorium. Alabama PSC encourages payment plans but utilities can disconnect for non-payment year-round.

What is Alabama Power Project SHARE?

Customer-funded emergency utility assistance program — administered with the Salvation Army. Up to $250-$500 in emergency bill help for qualifying customers.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. Alabama LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including propane and oil. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; crisis components may still apply.

How does cooling assistance work?

Cooling LIHEAP is crisis-focused in Alabama — typically processed quickly during heat advisories or for households with vulnerable members (seniors, kids under 6, medical conditions).

What about TVA distributors?

Northern Alabama is served by TVA-distributed power through local distributors (Huntsville Utilities, Tennessee Valley Authority direct customers). LIHEAP works with TVA distributors as it does with Alabama Power.
