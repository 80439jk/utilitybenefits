South Dakota LIEAP: Heating Help Through DSS

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

South Dakota's federal LIHEAP is the Low Income Energy Assistance Program (LIEAP), administered by SD DSS. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $300–$1,800, with significantly higher amounts for rural propane and fuel oil households. Major utilities — Xcel Energy, MidAmerican, Black Hills Energy, NorthWestern Energy — and rural electric cooperatives run additional hardship and Operation Round-Up programs that stack with LIEAP.

South Dakota's rural-heavy fuel mix

Most South Dakotans outside Sioux Falls and Rapid City heat with propane, fuel oil, or wood — not natural gas. Rural propane households often see larger LIEAP benefits per delivery than urban natural gas households because federal funding scales to fuel cost. A typical western South Dakota propane household can receive $800–$1,800 across the heating season.

Rural electric cooperatives across the state run Operation Round-Up programs that stack with LIEAP — members opt to round up their monthly bill to the next dollar, with proceeds funding emergency assistance for members in hardship. Ask your coop how to apply.

South Dakota eligibility

South Dakota uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in South Dakota

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Facing shutoff in South Dakota?

South Dakota-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIEAP in South Dakota?

Through your county SD DSS office or online at dss.sd.gov. SD has DSS offices in all major counties; rural residents may apply by phone or mail.

When does SD LIEAP open?

October 1 for general applications. Heating-season window through May 31. Crisis components available throughout.

Does SD have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No statutory moratorium. SD PUC encourages payment arrangements but doesn't prohibit winter disconnections. LIEAP enrollment provides effective protection by addressing arrears.

Does LIEAP cover propane and fuel oil?

Yes. SD LIEAP covers all heating fuels including natural gas, electric, propane, fuel oil, and wood. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is Operation Round-Up?

Most SD rural electric cooperatives let members round up their bill to the next dollar; proceeds fund emergency utility assistance for member households in hardship. Ask your coop how to apply for assistance.

Can renters qualify in South Dakota?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; emergency components may still apply.

Are tribal members eligible?

Yes — tribal members can apply through their tribal LIHEAP program (Pine Ridge/Oglala Sioux, Cheyenne River, Rosebud, Standing Rock, Yankton, Crow Creek, Lower Brule, Sisseton-Wahpeton, Flandreau Santee) or through state LIEAP at DSS.

What is Black Hills Cares?

Black Hills Energy customer-funded hardship fund administered with the Salvation Army. Provides emergency utility assistance for income-qualified Black Hills Energy customers.
