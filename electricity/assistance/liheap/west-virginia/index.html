West Virginia LIEAP: Heating Help with Conditional Cold Weather Rule

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

West Virginia's LIEAP (Low Income Energy Assistance Program) is the federal LIHEAP, administered through DHHR. Income limit: 130% FPL. Annual benefits typically $200–$600. Application window is unusually short: January 1 through March 1 only. Conditional winter shutoff protection December 1 through February 28 for households with children under 18, members 65+, or disabled members.

West Virginia's propane-heavy rural context

West Virginia has high propane heating usage in rural counties — many households outside Charleston and the Northern Panhandle don't have natural gas service. Per-fill propane benefits during LIEAP can exceed $400-$800. Mon Power and Appalachian Power (both FirstEnergy subsidiaries) serve most of the state. Dominion Energy West Virginia serves natural gas customers in some areas; Mountaineer Gas in others.

West Virginia eligibility

West Virginia uses 130% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in West Virginia

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Facing shutoff in West Virginia?

West Virginia-specific FAQ

When can I apply for West Virginia LIEAP?

January 1 through March 1 ONLY. This is the entire annual application window. Apply early because funds can deplete.

Does West Virginia have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Conditional protection December 1 through February 28 for households with children under 18, members 65+, or disabled members. You typically need to be enrolled in LIEAP for automatic protection.

Why is the LIEAP window so short?

WV concentrates LIHEAP processing during winter months when heating costs peak. The short window ensures benefits flow to utilities quickly.

Does LIEAP cover propane?

Yes. West Virginia LIEAP covers all heating fuels including propane. Rural WV propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is Dollar Energy Fund?

Multi-state utility-funded hardship program — available in PA, OH, WV, MD, parts of IN, KY, VA. Provides hardship grants for utility arrearages.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder.

Where do I apply?

Through your local DHHR office. Find yours at dhhr.wv.gov.

What if I miss the application window?

If you miss January 1 – March 1, you'll have to wait until next year for regular LIEAP. Crisis assistance through Dollar Energy Fund, Salvation Army, and 211 is available year-round.

A West Virginia-aware specialist can walk through LIEAP, your utility's hardship program, and any crisis pathways in one call. Call 1-833-555-0143
