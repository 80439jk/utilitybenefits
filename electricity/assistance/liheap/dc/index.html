Washington DC LIHEAP and Utility Discount Program (UDP)

MR
Reviewed by Marcus Reid Energy Policy Analyst · former NARUC staff
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Washington DC's federal LIHEAP is administered by DOEE (Department of Energy & Environment), with income limits at 200% FPL. On top of LIHEAP, DC operates the Utility Discount Program (UDP) — a year-round monthly discount on Pepco electric (up to $475/year), Washington Gas (up to $276/year), and DC Water (up to $480/year). Most low-income DC households should be enrolled in both LIHEAP and all three UDP tiers — combined value can exceed $2,500/year.

UDP is the bigger program for most DC residents

The DC Utility Discount Program (UDP) operates year-round across three utilities: Pepco (electric), Washington Gas (natural gas), and DC Water. Each provides a separate monthly discount applied directly to your bill. Combined annual value is typically $1,200–$1,500 — often larger than LIHEAP for the same household. Apply once through DOEE; enrollment covers all three.

UDP is also separate from federal eligibility paperwork — DC residents enrolled in SNAP, TANF, Medicaid, or SSI qualify automatically without separate income verification.

Washington, D.C. uses 200% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Washington, D.C.

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Washington, D.C.-specific FAQ

What's the difference between LIHEAP and UDP?

LIHEAP is a federal once-per-year cash benefit applied to your heating bill ($250–$1,300). UDP is a DC year-round monthly discount tier on Pepco, Washington Gas, and DC Water ($1,200–$1,500/year combined). They stack — most low-income DC households should be on both.

How do I apply for UDP?

Online through DOEE at doee.dc.gov, by mail, or in person at DOEE offices. One application enrolls you in all three (Pepco, Washington Gas, DC Water) UDP tiers.

What's the income limit for DC LIHEAP?

200% of the federal poverty level — about $62,400/year for a family of four. Categorical eligibility (SNAP, TANF, Medicaid, SSI) qualifies you automatically.

Is there a winter shutoff moratorium in DC?

Yes — DC PSC requires utilities to offer extended payment plans and delay disconnections November 1 through March 31 for income-qualified residential customers.

Does LIHEAP cover oil and propane?

Yes. DC LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including natural gas, electric, propane, and oil. Most DC households heat with natural gas (Washington Gas) or electric.

What is Solar for All?

DOEE program providing free or discounted community solar enrollment to income-qualified DC residents — typically reduces electric bills by $500/year or more. Stacks with LIHEAP and UDP.

Can renters qualify in DC?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular LIHEAP harder; UDP enrollment may still help if you have an account in your name.

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in DC?

Online through DOEE at doee.dc.gov, by phone (311), or in person at DOEE offices. Same application can enroll you in UDP simultaneously.
