Georgia Energy Assistance Program: LIHEAP Plus Cooling Crisis Support

Georgia's federal LIHEAP is administered through DHS and 22 Community Action Agencies. Cooling-focused given the climate, with deregulated gas adding a switching dimension most states don't have.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Georgia's federal LIHEAP is the Energy Assistance Program, administered by DHS through 22 local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 60% of State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $350–$1,000. Heating-season application opens November 1; cooling-crisis funds available April through September. Georgia is one of 6 deregulated natural gas markets — customers can shop suppliers, though the EAP credit applies regardless of supplier.

Georgia's deregulated gas market

Georgia is one of only 6 states with a deregulated retail natural gas market. Atlanta Gas Light is the local distribution utility for most of the state, but you choose your supplier (Constellation, Gas South, Walton EMC Natural Gas, others). Switching suppliers can save 5–15% on the supply portion of your gas bill. EAP applies regardless of which supplier you choose.

For electric, Georgia is largely served by Georgia Power (vertically integrated investor-owned utility) plus 41 EMCs (electric membership cooperatives) and 53 municipal utilities. EMCs and munis typically have their own hardship programs.

Georgia eligibility

Georgia uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Georgia

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Crisis assistance and shutoff prevention

Facing shutoff in Georgia?

GA does not have a winter shutoff moratorium, but PSC requires payment plans for low-income customers. A specialist can engage your state's program AND your utility's hardship line in parallel.

Georgia-specific FAQ

When does Georgia EAP open?

Heating-season applications open November 1 for vulnerable households (seniors 65+, disabled, families with kids under 6) and December 1 for general applications. Heating window through March 31. Cooling crisis April 1 – September 30.

How do I apply through a Community Action Agency?

Find your local agency at dhs.georgia.gov/services/energy-assistance. Most have online portals or accept in-person applications. Bring documentation (Section 4).

Does cooling assistance work the same as heating?

Cooling EAP is crisis-only in Georgia — for households facing AC-related shutoffs or with no working AC during heat advisories. Annual cooling benefits are smaller than heating.

Can I switch gas suppliers in Georgia?

Yes. Georgia is deregulated for natural gas. Compare suppliers at psc.state.ga.us. Switching takes 1–2 billing cycles. EAP applies regardless of supplier.

Does EAP work with electric cooperatives?

Yes. EAP credits apply to whatever utility serves your address — Georgia Power, EMCs, or municipal utilities. Cooperative customers may also qualify for member-specific Operation Round-Up hardship funds.

What if my CAA runs out of funds?

Georgia EAP funds can deplete late in the heating season. If your CAA is out, options: Project SHARE (Georgia Power), WinterCare (Atlanta Gas Light), Salvation Army, or 211 referrals.

Does EAP cover propane?

Yes. EAP covers all heating fuels including propane. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is Project SHARE?

Georgia Power's customer-funded emergency assistance program — administered with the Salvation Army. Provides up to $250–$500 in emergency bill help for qualifying customers.
