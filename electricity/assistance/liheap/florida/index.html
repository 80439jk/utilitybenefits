Florida LIHEAP-FL: Summer Cooling Assistance and Shutoff Prevention

Florida's LIHEAP is unusual — it's cooling-crisis focused, not heating, because of the climate. Administered through the Department of Economic Opportunity and local Community Action Agencies. Plus FPL Care to Share and Duke Share the Light hardship programs.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Florida's federal LIHEAP-FL is administered through the Department of Economic Opportunity (DEO) via local Community Action Agencies. Because Florida's climate makes cooling more critical than heating, LIHEAP-FL prioritizes summer cooling assistance and year-round shutoff prevention. Florida households at or below 150% of the federal poverty level qualify. Typical annual benefits: $400–$1,000. Major Florida utilities run their own hardship programs that stack — FPL Care to Share, Duke Energy Florida Share the Light, TECO Tampa Electric Share.

Why Florida's LIHEAP is cooling-focused

Most LIHEAP programs nationally are heating-focused — the program was created in 1981 partly in response to cold-weather utility shutoff fatalities. Florida's climate makes that framing inverted: heat-related illness from lack of AC kills more Floridians annually than cold-weather fatalities elsewhere. LIHEAP-FL accordingly prioritizes summer cooling and operates under the same federal funding formula but with cooling-season service delivery.

Practical impact: if you're a Florida household and you can't pay your electric bill in July or August, LIHEAP-FL processes your application as a cooling-crisis emergency — typically within 18–48 hours — and pays your utility directly to keep AC running.

Florida eligibility

FL uses 150% FPL as the LIHEAP cutoff. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, or TANF qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Florida

  1. Find your local Community Action Agency. Find yours via Florida Association of Community Action (FACA) or call 211.
  2. Apply through your local CAA. Most major Florida counties (Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, Hillsborough, Orange) have online portals; rural counties may require in-person.
  3. Submit documentation — see Section 4 below. SNAP/SSI categorical applications skip income paperwork.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular LIHEAP-FL, 18–48 hours for cooling-crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — FPL, Duke Energy Florida, TECO, or your local provider.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF)
  • Most recent FPL, Duke Energy Florida, TECO, or local utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For cooling crisis: shutoff notice or proof that AC is non-functional

Florida-specific FAQ

Is LIHEAP-FL the same as LIHEAP?

Yes — LIHEAP-FL is just Florida's branded version of federal LIHEAP. Same federal funding source. The "FL" suffix and the cooling-focus are the main differences.

Does LIHEAP-FL cover air conditioning purchases?

Limited cases. LIHEAP-FL primarily pays utility bills, not equipment. Some local Community Action Agencies have separate funds for AC unit purchase or repair for vulnerable households (60+, certain medical conditions). Ask your local CAA — availability varies by county.

What about hurricane season — can LIHEAP help with reconnection after a storm?

Florida utilities typically waive reconnection fees after hurricanes for affected customers. LIHEAP-FL crisis funds can help with bill arrearages that built up during a storm-related outage. Call your CAA or your utility's emergency line.

Does Florida have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Florida does not have a formal winter shutoff moratorium (cold-weather rule) like northern states because cold weather is rarely life-threatening. However, FPL and other utilities offer payment plans and hardship programs year-round.

Can renters qualify in Florida?

Yes, as long as you pay your own electric bill. If utilities are bundled into rent, the regular benefit is harder to access but cooling-crisis funds may still apply.

What if my CAA has run out of LIHEAP-FL funds for the year?

Florida's LIHEAP-FL allocation can run out late in the cooling season. If your CAA is out, options include: utility-specific hardship programs (FPL Care to Share, etc.), Salvation Army, 211, or moving to the next federal fiscal year (October 1 reset).

How do I find my local Community Action Agency?

The Florida Association of Community Action (FACA) maintains a directory at faca-fl.org. Or call 211 from any Florida phone for routing. Major counties: Miami-Dade Action, Broward Community Action, Palm Beach County, Pinellas-Pasco, etc.

Does FPL's Care to Share require LIHEAP first?

No. FPL Care to Share is a separate utility-funded program, independent of LIHEAP. They stack — apply to both. FPL Care to Share is typically administered through Salvation Army or local agencies.
