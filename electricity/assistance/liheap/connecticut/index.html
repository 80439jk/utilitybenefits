Connecticut Energy Assistance Program: Heating Help and Winter Protections

UB

Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Connecticut's Energy Assistance Program (CEAP) is administered through DSS and 11 Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Three benefit tiers: Basic ($530–$1,500), Safety Net (additional $1,275 for vulnerable households), and Crisis Assistance (up to $700 for emergencies). CT has a strong winter shutoff moratorium November 1 through April 15 for income-qualified or hardship-designated households. Eversource New Start adds significant arrearage forgiveness for participating customers.

Connecticut's three-tier CEAP structure

Connecticut is unusual in having a tiered benefit structure. Most states pay one annual benefit; CT layers Basic, Safety Net (for very low income or vulnerable households), and Crisis (for emergencies). Combined, qualifying CT households can receive $2,000+ per heating season — among the most generous in the country.

CT also has high heating costs (cold winters, high oil prices) so the larger benefits reflect actual need. Most CT households heat with oil or natural gas; some with propane in rural areas; small share with electric heat in newer homes.

Connecticut eligibility

Connecticut uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Connecticut

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Connecticut-specific FAQ

How does CT's three-tier CEAP work?

Basic Benefit covers most LIHEAP-eligible households ($530-$1,500). Safety Net adds an extra benefit for households at very low income or with vulnerable members (60+, disabled, kids under 6). Crisis Assistance is for documented emergencies. Apply once; eligibility for each tier is determined automatically.

Where do I apply?

Through your local Community Action Agency. 11 agencies cover all CT counties. Find yours at portal.ct.gov/dss.

Does CEAP cover heating oil?

Yes — heating oil is one of the largest categories in CT CEAP. Per-fill benefits are typically $400–$1,200 for oil households. Paid directly to fuel vendors.

What is Operation Fuel?

Connecticut's statewide nonprofit emergency energy assistance program — independent of federal CEAP. Provides up to $500 in emergency assistance when CEAP is tapped out or you don't qualify. Funded by donors and utilities.

Does Connecticut have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Yes — November 1 through April 15 for income-qualified or hardship-designated households. You typically need to be enrolled in CEAP, Operation Fuel, or have a hardship designation with your utility for automatic protection.

Eversource New Start vs CEAP — both?

Yes, both. Eversource New Start forgives 1/24th of arrearages per consecutive on-time monthly payment for participating customers. CEAP pays new bills with annual benefits. Together they can clear large arrearages over 12-24 months.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; crisis components may still apply.

Spanish support available?

Yes. Most CT Community Action Agencies have bilingual Spanish staff. Application materials in Spanish.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in Connecticut?

A Connecticut-aware specialist can walk through CEAP, your utility's hardship program, and any crisis pathways in one call. Call 1-833-555-0143
