New York HEAP: Heating Bill Help, Shutoff Prevention, and Equipment Repair

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

The Home Energy Assistance Program (HEAP) is New York's name for LIHEAP. It has three components: Regular HEAP (annual heating benefit, $400–$900 typical), Emergency HEAP (additional crisis funds for shutoffs or empty fuel tanks), and Heating Equipment Repair/Replacement (broken furnaces and boilers). New York households at or below 60% of state median income qualify. Administered by the Office of Temporary and Disability Assistance (OTDA) through local Departments of Social Services.

The three HEAP components

1. Regular HEAP

The annual heating benefit. Typical amounts $400–$900, with vulnerable household supplements (60+, disabled, kids under 6) pushing some benefits higher. Paid as a one-time annual credit, usually directly to your utility (Con Ed, National Grid, NYSEG, etc.) or fuel vendor (for oil/propane households).

2. Emergency HEAP

Additional funds for households facing shutoff, with no fuel, or with broken heating equipment. Year-round in most cases (heating season + cooling crisis component). Up to $700 in additional benefits on top of Regular HEAP. Same eligibility, faster processing — typically within 18–48 hours when truly urgent.

3. Heating Equipment Repair/Replacement

HEAP can pay to repair or replace a broken furnace, boiler, or heating system for qualifying households. This is one of the lesser-known but highest-value components — a new boiler installation can be worth $5,000–$10,000. Eligibility requires HEAP qualification plus an inspection demonstrating the heating system is non-functional.

New York eligibility

NY uses 60% of State Median Income (SMI) as the primary cutoff — slightly different from states using 150% FPL. SMI is generally more generous in high-income states like New York. Categorical eligibility shortcuts: SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, TANF, or Code A SSI qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in New York

  1. Find your local Department of Social Services (DSS). NYC residents apply through the HRA HEAP page. Other counties: find your local DSS office.
  2. Apply online, in person, or by mail. NYC and most major counties have online portals. Rural counties may require in-person application.
  3. Submit documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, etc.), you can skip income paperwork.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for Regular HEAP, 18–48 hours for Emergency HEAP.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — Con Ed, National Grid, NYSEG, RG&E, Central Hudson, or your fuel vendor.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past four weeks, or proof of categorical participation (SNAP/Medicaid/SSI letter)
  • Most recent utility bill (Con Ed, National Grid, NYSEG, RG&E, etc.)
  • Lease, mortgage statement, or tax bill
  • For Emergency HEAP: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank
  • For Heating Equipment Repair: contractor's estimate or inspection report

New York-specific FAQ

Is HEAP the same as LIHEAP in New York?

Functionally yes — HEAP is New York's branded version of LIHEAP. Same federal funding source, same broad framework. The difference is just the name and the local DSS administration model.

When does the HEAP application window open?

Regular HEAP opens November 1 each year and typically runs through March 15. Emergency HEAP runs longer (often through April or May). Cooling assistance opens in May for vulnerable households (60+, disabled, certain medical conditions). Apply early — funding can run out late in the season.

Does HEAP cover oil and propane?

Yes. HEAP funds cover all heating fuels including oil and propane. Oil and propane households often see higher per-fill benefits because of higher unit costs.

What is the Cold Weather Rule?

New York's Public Service Commission requires utilities to delay disconnection of essential service between November 1 and April 15 for customers who are financially eligible — but you have to communicate with your utility. Don't ignore shutoff notices; call the utility AND apply for Emergency HEAP.

Can renters qualify in New York?

Yes, as long as you pay your own utility bill. If utilities are bundled into rent, the regular benefit is harder to access but Emergency HEAP and Heating Equipment Repair may still apply.

What if my heating system breaks?

HEAP's third component pays to repair or replace broken heating equipment for qualifying households. Apply through your local DSS with a contractor's estimate. This benefit is large — up to $4,000–$10,000 — but slow (typically 30+ days unless emergency).

How does the NYC HRA HEAP application work?

NYC residents apply through the Human Resources Administration (HRA) at nyc.gov/hra. The application is online or by phone (1-800-692-0557). Walk-in offices are also available.

Can I get HEAP in addition to my utility's hardship program?

Yes. HEAP is a federal benefit; utility hardship programs (Con Ed EAP, National Grid CARE, etc.) are utility-funded and stack on top. Most low-income NY households should apply to both.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in New York?

Talk to a NY-aware specialist who can run HEAP (all three components), invoke the Cold Weather Rule if needed, and engage Con Ed/National Grid hardship programs in parallel. Call 1-833-555-0143
