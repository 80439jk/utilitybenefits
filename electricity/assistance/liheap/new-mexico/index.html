New Mexico's federal LIHEAP is administered through HSD and 27 local Community Action Agencies. Plus utility-specific programs from PNM, El Paso Electric, and Xcel Energy.

Apply through YES NM (online) or your local CAA. Major utilities — PNM, El Paso Electric, Xcel Energy — run additional hardship programs that stack.

New Mexico's mixed climate dynamics

New Mexico has cold winters in the north (Albuquerque, Santa Fe, Taos) and milder winters in the south (Las Cruces). Both regions have significant cooling demand in summer. PNM (Public Service Company of New Mexico) serves most of the state for electricity. El Paso Electric serves the southern border region. New Mexico Gas Company serves much of the natural gas market; rural areas often use propane.

New Mexico eligibility

New Mexico uses 150% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in New Mexico

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

New Mexico-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in New Mexico?

Through YES NM (yes.state.nm.us) or your local Community Action Agency. 27 CAAs cover all NM counties.

Does New Mexico have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No general moratorium. NM PRC requires utilities to offer payment plans before shutoff. Medical hardship customers receive year-round protection with physician certification.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. New Mexico LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including propane. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is PNM Good Neighbor Fund?

PNM's customer-funded emergency utility assistance program. Provides emergency bill help for income-qualified PNM customers.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder.

What about tribal areas?

New Mexico has many federally recognized tribes (Navajo Nation, Pueblos, Apache nations). Tribal members can apply through tribal LIHEAP programs (where administered) or state HSD.

When does NM LIHEAP open?

Year-round. Heating-season priority October through May.

Spanish support available?

Yes. Most NM Community Action Agencies have bilingual Spanish staff — Spanish is spoken throughout the state. Application materials in Spanish.
