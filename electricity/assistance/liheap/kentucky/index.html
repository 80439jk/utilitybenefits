Kentucky LIHEAP: Subsidy and Crisis Components

Kentucky's federal LIHEAP has two phases: Subsidy (regular annual benefit) and Crisis (emergency funds). Administered through DCBS and 23 Community Action Agencies. Significant propane heating in rural areas.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Kentucky's LIHEAP is unusual: two distinct phases with limited application windows. The Subsidy phase runs only November 4 – December 13 for the annual benefit ($100-$400). The Crisis phase runs January 6 – March 31 for emergency funds (up to $500). Income limit: 130% FPL — slightly stricter than the typical 150%. Major utilities — LG&E, Kentucky Utilities, Atmos — run additional hardship programs. Rural KY is propane-heavy.

Kentucky's two-phase application structure

Kentucky LIHEAP is structured differently than most states — with strict, separate application windows for Subsidy and Crisis. The Subsidy phase opens November 4 each year and runs through December 13 only. Miss this window and you can't apply for regular LIHEAP that year. The Crisis phase opens January 6 — for households with active shutoff notices, empty fuel tanks, or no working heating equipment. Plan ahead: apply for Subsidy in November, then Crisis if you face emergency in January or later.

Rural Kentucky has high propane and electric heat usage. Per-fill propane benefits during Crisis can exceed Subsidy amounts.

Kentucky eligibility

Kentucky uses 130% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Kentucky

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Kentucky-specific FAQ

When can I apply for Kentucky LIHEAP?

Subsidy phase: November 4 – December 13 only. Crisis phase: January 6 – March 31. These windows are strict — apply early in the window because funds can run out.

Why are KY's application windows so short?

Kentucky chose a structured timing approach to ensure benefits are processed before the heating season peak. The Subsidy phase paces applications; the Crisis phase handles emergencies later.

Where do I apply?

Through your local Community Action Agency. 23 agencies cover all KY counties. Find yours at chfs.ky.gov.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. Kentucky LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including propane and oil. Rural KY propane households often see larger per-fill benefits during the Crisis phase.

What is WinterCare?

LG&E and KU's customer-funded emergency assistance program for income-qualified customers. Provides emergency utility bill help. Stacks with LIHEAP.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; Crisis component may still apply.

Does Kentucky have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No statutory moratorium. Kentucky PSC encourages payment plans for income-qualified customers but utilities can disconnect for non-payment year-round.

What if I miss the Subsidy window?

If you miss November 4 – December 13, you can still apply for Crisis (January 6 – March 31) if you have an active emergency. Next year's Subsidy reopens November 4.
