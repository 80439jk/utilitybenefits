Mississippi LIHEAP: Cooling-Focused with MS Power and Entergy MS

Mississippi's federal LIHEAP is administered through MDHS and 17 Community Action Agencies. Cooling-heavy given the climate. Mississippi Power and Entergy MS run additional hardship programs.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Mississippi's federal LIHEAP is administered through MDHS by 17 local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 150% FPL. Annual benefits typically $200–$700. Like Louisiana and Florida, MS LIHEAP is cooling-focused given the climate. Major utilities — Mississippi Power, Entergy Mississippi, Atmos Energy — run additional hardship programs.

Mississippi's cooling-focused context

Mississippi's hot, humid summers create extreme cooling demand. Heat-related illness from lack of AC is a public health risk for vulnerable populations. LIHEAP-MS prioritizes cooling crises during summer with expedited processing. Entergy Mississippi serves most of the state for electricity (excluding the Mississippi Power territory in southeast MS). Atmos Energy serves much of the natural gas market.

Mississippi eligibility

Mississippi uses 150% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Mississippi

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Mississippi-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in Mississippi?

Through your local Community Action Agency. 17 agencies cover all MS counties. Find yours at mdhs.ms.gov.

Does Mississippi have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No statutory moratorium. Mississippi PSC encourages payment plans for income-qualified customers but utilities can disconnect for non-payment year-round.

Why is Mississippi LIHEAP cooling-focused?

Heat-related illness from lack of AC kills more Mississippians annually than cold-related illness. LIHEAP funds prioritize summer cooling crises.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. Mississippi LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including propane. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is Mississippi Power Project SHARE?

Mississippi Power's customer-funded emergency utility assistance program — administered with Salvation Army.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder.

How fast is cooling crisis processing?

Typically 18–48 hours when truly urgent (no AC, vulnerable household member, severe heat advisory).

When does Mississippi LIHEAP open?

Year-round. Heating-season focus October through April; cooling-season focus May through September.
