Rhode Island LIHEAP, the LIHEAP Crisis Program, and Rhode Island Energy Hardship Help

Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Rhode Island's federal LIHEAP is administered by OER through 5 local Community Action Agencies (CAPs). Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $300–$1,200, with Crisis funds up to $1,500 for shutoffs and empty fuel tanks. On top of LIHEAP, qualifying RI Energy customers get the A-60 discount rate — a permanent rate-tier reduction. RI also has one of the strongest winter shutoff protection rules in New England, applying from November 1 through April 15 for protected households.

RI's A-60 discount rate stacks with LIHEAP

Rhode Island Energy (formerly National Grid Rhode Island) operates an A-60 discount rate for income-qualified residential customers — a permanent rate-tier reduction that reduces both gas and electric bills throughout the year. Apply through Rhode Island Energy with proof of LIHEAP enrollment or income documentation.

RI's winter shutoff protection is also stronger than most. From November 1 through April 15, regulated utilities cannot disconnect protected households (income-qualified, seriously ill, elderly, families with infants) for non-payment. Households self-certify protection status by contacting their utility — protection isn't automatic but is straightforward to claim.

Rhode Island eligibility

Rhode Island uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Rhode Island

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Rhode Island-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in Rhode Island?

Through your local Community Action Agency (CAP) — 5 CAPs cover all of RI: CAPP (Providence), Tri-County (Johnston), West Bay (Warwick), East Bay (Bristol), and Comprehensive (Westerly).

What is the A-60 discount rate?

Rhode Island Energy's permanent income-qualified rate-tier reduction. Reduces both gas and electric bills throughout the year. Apply through RI Energy with LIHEAP enrollment or income proof.

When does RI LIHEAP open?

November 1 for general applications, with priority intake earlier for vulnerable households. Heating-season window through April 30. Crisis available throughout.

Does RI have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Yes — strong protection November 1 through April 15 for protected households (income-qualified, seriously ill, elderly, families with infants under 2). Self-certify with your utility to claim.

Does LIHEAP cover oil and propane?

Yes. RI LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including natural gas, electric, oil, propane, and wood. Most RI households heat with natural gas or oil.

Can renters qualify in Rhode Island?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; crisis components may still apply.

What's the Henry Shelter Program?

RI-funded emergency utility assistance for households facing eviction-related shutoffs or imminent risk. Apply through your local CAP or social service agency.

How does the Good Neighbor Energy Fund work?

Customer-funded RI Energy hardship assistance administered by Salvation Army — one-time grants for arrears not covered by LIHEAP. Apply through Salvation Army RI.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in Rhode Island?

A Rhode Island-aware specialist can walk through LIHEAP, your utility's hardship program, and any crisis pathways in one call. Call 1-833-555-0143
