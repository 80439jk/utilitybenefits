Missouri's temperature-triggered Cold Weather Rule

Missouri's Cold Weather Rule is unusual in that it's both date-based AND temperature-triggered. Between November 1 and March 31, utilities (Ameren Missouri, Spire Gas, Empire District, others) cannot disconnect residential customers for non-payment when forecast low temperatures drop to 32°F or below. Protection is automatic — utilities monitor National Weather Service forecasts. The rule applies regardless of income, but the practical reality is that customers in arrears benefit most.

To maintain protection beyond winter, enroll in a payment plan with your utility. ECIP enrollment usually meets the requirement.

Missouri eligibility

Missouri uses 135% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Missouri

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Missouri-specific FAQ

How does Missouri's Cold Weather Rule work?

Between November 1 and March 31, utilities cannot disconnect residential customers for non-payment when forecast low temperatures drop to 32°F or below. Automatic protection — no enrollment required.

What's the difference between LIHEAP and ECIP?

Regular LIHEAP is a one-time annual benefit ($300–$800) for the heating season. ECIP (Energy Crisis Intervention Program) is for emergencies year-round (shutoff, no fuel, broken heating equipment) — up to $800 per crisis episode.

Where do I apply?

Through DSS Family Support Division (online at mydss.mo.gov) or your local Community Action Agency. Find local agencies at dss.mo.gov.

When does Missouri LIHEAP open?

October 1 for general applications. Heating-season window through May 31. ECIP available year-round for emergencies.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. Missouri LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including propane and oil. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What's Heat-Up Missouri?

Statewide nonprofit funded by utility customers and donors that provides emergency heating assistance — often the fastest source of help when LIHEAP and ECIP are tapped out.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; emergency components may still apply.

What is Ameren Dollar More Program?

Ameren Missouri's customer-funded hardship fund — administered with the Salvation Army. Provides emergency utility assistance for income-qualified Ameren customers.
