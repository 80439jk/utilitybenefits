Indiana EAP: Federal LIHEAP Plus Year-Round Crisis Support

Indiana's federal LIHEAP is the Energy Assistance Program (EAP), administered through IHCDA and local Community Action Agencies. Plus utility-specific programs from Duke Energy Indiana, AES Indiana, NIPSCO, and Vectren.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Indiana's Energy Assistance Program (EAP) is the federal LIHEAP, administered by IHCDA through local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $300–$1,000. Indiana has a winter shutoff moratorium December 1 through March 15 for LIHEAP-eligible households. Major utilities — Duke Energy Indiana, AES Indiana, NIPSCO, Vectren — run additional hardship programs that stack with EAP.

Indiana's split utility landscape

Indiana has multiple investor-owned utilities serving different parts of the state, plus dozens of cooperatives and municipal utilities. The major investor-owned electric utilities: Duke Energy Indiana (most of Indiana), AES Indiana (Indianapolis), NIPSCO (Northern Indiana), and Indiana Michigan Power (small parts of NE Indiana). Vectren serves SW Indiana for both gas and electric. Each runs its own customer hardship program; EAP applies to whatever utility serves your address.

Indiana eligibility

Indiana uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Indiana

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Indiana-specific FAQ

When does Indiana EAP open?

November 1 for general applications and earlier for vulnerable households (60+, disabled). Heating-season window through May 14. Cooling crisis funds available May through September.

Does Indiana have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Yes — December 1 through March 15 for LIHEAP-eligible households. You must have applied for or be enrolled in LIHEAP to receive automatic protection.

Where do I apply?

Through your local Community Action Agency. Find yours at in.gov/ihcda. Indianapolis residents can apply through Indianapolis Township Trustees as well.

What is Vectren USP?

Vectren's Universal Service Program — discount rate tier for income-qualified gas/electric customers. Apply through Vectren directly. Stacks with EAP.

How does Township Trustee Assistance work?

Indiana townships have emergency relief funds ("poor relief") that can pay utility bills for residents in financial hardship. Each township has its own application process and budget. Often faster than EAP for true emergencies.

Does EAP cover propane?

Yes. Indiana EAP covers all heating fuels including propane and oil. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular EAP harder; emergency components may still apply.

What is AES Indiana Energy Assistance?

AES Indiana's customer-funded emergency assistance program — bill help for qualifying customers. Administered with the Salvation Army.
