Texas LIHEAP (CEAP): Eligibility, How to Apply, and Local Offices

UB

Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

The Comprehensive Energy Assistance Program (CEAP) is Texas's LIHEAP, administered by TDHCA through local Community Action Agencies. Texas households at or below 150% of the federal poverty level may qualify for help with electricity, gas, or propane bills. Crisis assistance is available year-round for utility shutoffs, including summer cooling shutoffs. Average annual benefit ranges from $400 to $1,200.

What is CEAP, and how does it relate to LIHEAP?

CEAP is Texas's branded version of LIHEAP. The federal funding flows from HHS to TDHCA, which distributes it to roughly 39 local Community Action Agencies across the state. Those agencies — not TDHCA directly — accept applications and pay benefits.

This matters because Texas, unlike California or New York, doesn't have a single statewide application portal. Where you apply depends on which county you live in. Houston (Harris County) is served by Gulf Coast Community Services Association; Dallas by Community Council of Greater Dallas; Austin by EXPECTACT and others; San Antonio by Family Service Association; rural counties by regional agencies.

Texas eligibility

Texas uses the federal baseline of 150% FPL as its primary income test, which is on the more generous end of state implementations. Categorical eligibility shortcuts also apply: if your household receives SNAP, TANF, or SSI, you can typically skip the full income paperwork.

How to apply in Texas

  1. Find your local Community Action Agency. Use the office finder below or call 211.
  2. Confirm appointment style. Some agencies accept walk-ins; most prefer scheduled appointments. Houston and Dallas have online application portals.
  3. Gather documents — see Section 4 below.
  4. Submit application in person, by mail, or online (varies by agency).
  5. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular applications. Crisis applications: within 18 hours.
  6. Benefit paid directly to your utility — Reliant, TXU, CenterPoint, Atmos, etc.

Documents required (Texas-specific)

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards or numbers for everyone in the household
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days (pay stubs, benefits letters, child support, etc.)
  • Most recent electric or gas bill (or fuel delivery receipt for propane)
  • Lease or mortgage statement to verify residency
  • Citizenship/residency documentation (varies by agency)
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or empty-tank documentation

Crisis assistance: Texas summer cooling shutoffs

Texas is one of the states where cooling crisis assistance is as important as heating — Austin, Houston, San Antonio, and Dallas reach 100°F+ for weeks. Texas crisis CEAP is funded year-round. If you have a shutoff notice in summer, your local Community Action Agency can pay your bill directly within 18–48 hours.

Texas benefit ranges

Regular CEAP heating and cooling benefits typically range from $400 to $1,200 per year, depending on income tier, household size, and energy burden (your bill as a percentage of income). Crisis benefits are paid on top and typically range from $200 to $800 per crisis episode.

Texas-specific FAQ

Is CEAP the same as LIHEAP in Texas?

Functionally yes — CEAP is Texas's name for LIHEAP. The federal funding source, eligibility framework, and what it covers are all the same. The difference is just branding and the local intake model.

Can I get help if I'm with a deregulated REP (retail electric provider)?

Yes. CEAP works regardless of whether your provider is regulated (like El Paso Electric) or deregulated (like Reliant or TXU in most ERCOT areas). The benefit is paid directly to your provider.

Does CEAP cover propane?

Yes. CEAP funds cover heating costs regardless of fuel — natural gas, propane, electric, or wood. Propane households often see higher per-fill benefits because of the higher unit cost.

Can I apply online in Texas?

It depends on your county. Houston, Dallas, and Austin have online portals through their local CAAs. Many rural counties still require in-person or mailed applications.

What if I'm facing shutoff today?

Call our specialist line at 1-833-555-0143 immediately, or call 211. Your local Community Action Agency can submit a CEAP crisis application within hours, and many Texas REPs will pause disconnection while a CEAP application is pending.

Does CEAP run out of funding in Texas?

Sometimes. Funding is allocated annually and some counties exhaust their allocation by mid-summer in cooling-heavy years. Apply early in the season for best chances. Crisis funding usually persists through the year.

Can renters qualify in Texas?

Yes — as long as you pay your own utility bill. If utilities are bundled into rent, the regular benefit is harder to access but the crisis component may still help.

What if utilities are in my landlord's name?

This complicates eligibility. Some Texas counties accept indirect arrangements where the landlord receives the benefit on the household's behalf. A specialist can clarify based on your specific situation.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in Texas?

Talk to a Texas-aware specialist who can run CEAP, your REP's hardship program, and any local options at the same time. Call 1-833-555-0143
