Oregon Energy Assistance: Federal LIHEAP Plus State Supplements

Oregon's federal LIHEAP is administered through DCS. Plus the Oregon Energy Assistance Program (OEAP) for higher-income households not eligible for federal LIHEAP. Strong cold weather rule.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Oregon has two energy assistance programs. Federal LIHEAP is administered through DHS by local Community Action Agencies — income limit 60% State Median Income, benefits $200-$1,200. Plus OEAP (Oregon Energy Assistance Program), funded by Oregon utility customers, serving households at higher incomes (up to 80% SMI) not eligible for federal LIHEAP. Oregon shutoff moratorium November 1 – April 15 for households on payment plans.

Oregon's federal + state energy assistance combo

Oregon is one of the few states that operates both federal LIHEAP and a state-funded supplemental program (OEAP). OEAP is funded by surcharges on Oregon utility customers and serves households at slightly higher incomes (up to 80% SMI) who don't qualify for federal LIHEAP. Together they extend assistance to a wider range of low-income Oregon households. Apply through your local Community Action Agency.

Oregon eligibility

Oregon uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Oregon

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Oregon-specific FAQ

What's the difference between LIHEAP and OEAP?

Federal LIHEAP serves households up to 60% State Median Income. OEAP serves households at slightly higher income (up to 80% SMI) and is funded by Oregon utility customers. Some households can use both.

Where do I apply?

Through your local Community Action Agency. Find yours at oregon.gov/oha. Both LIHEAP and OEAP applications are typically combined.

Does Oregon have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Yes — November 1 through April 15 for households on payment plans. You must actively engage your utility to invoke.

What is PGE Income Qualified Bill Discount?

Portland General Electric's ongoing discount program — 25% off monthly bills for income-qualified customers (60% SMI). Apply through PGE directly. Stacks with LIHEAP.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. Oregon LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including propane. Rural Oregon propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is Eugene Water & Electric Board CCP?

EWEB's Customer Care Program — discount rates for income-qualified Eugene/Springfield customers. Separate from federal LIHEAP.

Can renters qualify in Oregon?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; emergency components may still apply.

Spanish support available?

Yes. Most Oregon Community Action Agencies have bilingual Spanish staff. Application materials in Spanish.
