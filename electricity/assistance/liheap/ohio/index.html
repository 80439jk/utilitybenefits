Ohio PIPP Plus & LIHEAP: Cap Your Bill at 6% of Income

PIPP Plus is Ohio's flagship utility assistance program — it caps your electric (or gas) bill at 6% of household income, regardless of actual usage. For most qualifying Ohioans, it's bigger than LIHEAP. The two stack.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Ohio's flagship utility assistance program is PIPP Plus (Percentage of Income Payment Plan Plus), which caps your electric bill at 6% of household income — and your gas bill at 6% — for households at or below 175% of the federal poverty level. PIPP Plus also forgives 1/24th of your past arrearages for every on-time monthly payment, eventually clearing past-due balances. Federal LIHEAP is a separate annual benefit ($400–$1,500) that stacks on top. Lead with PIPP Plus — it usually saves more than LIHEAP for most Ohio households.

Why PIPP Plus, not LIHEAP, is the lead program

Most state-level LIHEAP programs are paid as a one-time annual credit. PIPP Plus works differently: it caps your monthly utility bill at 6% of household income, every month, all year. For a typical Ohio household earning $30,000/year ($2,500/month), PIPP Plus caps the electric bill at $150/month and the gas bill at $150/month. If your actual bills would have been $250/month each, PIPP Plus saves you $200/month — $2,400/year. That's bigger than even the largest LIHEAP benefit.

The arrearage forgiveness is the second piece: 1/24th of your past-due balance is forgiven for every consecutive on-time monthly payment. Two years of consistent payments wipe the arrearage to zero. This makes PIPP Plus uniquely valuable for households with significant past-due bills.

Ohio eligibility

PIPP Plus uses 175% FPL as the cutoff — more generous than LIHEAP's 150% standard. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, or OWF/TANF qualify automatically.

How to apply in Ohio

  1. Apply for PIPP Plus first — through the Ohio Development Services Agency at development.ohio.gov or your local Community Action Agency. Year-round application.
  2. Apply for LIHEAP separately — also through your local Community Action Agency. Heating-season application opens November 1.
  3. For Winter Crisis Program (Nov 1 – Mar 31) — additional emergency funds for shutoff or fuel emergencies during the heating season.
  4. Submit documentation — see Section 5 below.
  5. Make on-time monthly PIPP Plus payments to qualify for arrearage forgiveness (1/24th forgiven per consecutive on-time payment).

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 12 months, OR proof of categorical participation
  • Most recent utility bill (AEP Ohio, Duke Energy Ohio, FirstEnergy/Ohio Edison, Columbia Gas, Vectren, Dominion Energy)
  • Lease, mortgage, or property tax statement
  • For Winter Crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Ohio-specific FAQ

How is PIPP Plus different from LIHEAP?

PIPP Plus caps your monthly bill at 6% of income, all year, and forgives past-due balances over time. LIHEAP is a one-time annual credit ($400–$1,200) paid to your utility. They serve different purposes and stack — most low-income Ohio households should apply to both.

Can I get PIPP Plus for both electric and gas?

Yes. If you have separate electric and gas providers, PIPP Plus caps your electric bill at 6% of income AND your gas bill at 6% — meaning your total energy spend is capped at 12% of household income.

What happens if I miss a monthly PIPP Plus payment?

You break the arrearage forgiveness streak (the 1/24th-per-month forgiveness restarts), but you stay enrolled in PIPP Plus and your monthly cap stays in place. Two consecutive missed payments may put you at risk of removal — call your utility immediately if you can't make a payment.

What if my income is between 175% and 200% FPL?

You're above PIPP Plus eligibility but may qualify for utility-specific hardship programs (AEP Neighbor to Neighbor, Duke Share the Light, etc.) or for ISP-specific low-cost programs. Talk to a specialist — there's almost always a layered option.

Can renters qualify in Ohio?

Yes — as long as you pay your own electric or gas bill. Renters are common PIPP Plus participants.

What's the Winter Crisis Program (HEAP Crisis)?

From November 1 through March 31, Ohio offers up to $750 in Winter Crisis funds for households facing shutoff, with no fuel, or whose utility has been disconnected. Apply through your local Community Action Agency. Crisis applications process within 18–48 hours when truly urgent.

Does Columbia Gas WarmChoice cost anything?

No. Columbia Gas WarmChoice provides free weatherization (insulation, sealing, sometimes furnace replacement) for qualifying low-income gas customers in Columbia's service territory. Fully grant-funded — no copay.

How long does PIPP Plus enrollment take?

Initial application is typically processed within 30 days. The PIPP Plus rate goes into effect on the next billing cycle after approval. Arrearage forgiveness starts accruing immediately upon enrollment.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in Ohio?

An Ohio specialist can sort out PIPP Plus first, layer LIHEAP and Winter Crisis, plus engage AEP/Duke/Columbia Gas hardship programs. The right application order can mean $1,000+ more in annual savings.
