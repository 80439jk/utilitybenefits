Vermont's federal LIHEAP runs as Seasonal Fuel Assistance ($400–$2,000 paid to your fuel dealer) and year-round Crisis Fuel (emergency vouchers for empty tanks or shutoffs). Income limit: 200% FPL — among the most generous in the country. Vermont also operates LIHWAP for water and wastewater arrears. Apply through your local Community Action Agency or DCF Economic Services. Most Vermonters heat with fuel oil, propane, or wood — Vermont's per-household LIHEAP benefits run high to match those costs.

Vermont's heating fuel mix and big benefits

Vermont is mostly oil and propane country — only a small slice of households (Burlington area, parts of Chittenden County) have natural gas access. The rest heat with fuel oil, propane, wood, or pellets. Combined with Vermont's long, cold winters (averaging 8,000+ heating degree days), per-household heating costs routinely exceed $3,000/year. Vermont's Seasonal Fuel Assistance benefits scale to match — average per-household amounts among the country's largest.

Vermont also funds Crisis Fuel year-round — emergency vouchers when tanks run empty, shutoffs are imminent, or heating systems fail. Apply through your local CAA. Crisis funds are available even after the Seasonal Fuel Assistance window closes April 30.

Vermont eligibility

Vermont uses 200% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Vermont

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Vermont-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for Vermont Fuel Assistance?

Through your local Community Action Agency (CAA) or DCF Economic Services Division. 5 CAAs cover Vermont: Capstone (Central), CVOEO (Chittenden/Franklin/Grand Isle/Addison), NEKCA (Northeast Kingdom), SEVCA (Southeast), and BROC (Bennington/Rutland).

When does VT Seasonal Fuel Assistance open?

October 1 for general applications. Heating-season window through April 30. Crisis Fuel available year-round.

What's the income limit?

200% of the federal poverty level — about $62,400/year for a family of four. Vermont's threshold is among the most generous in the country.

Does Vermont have a winter shutoff moratorium?

VT PUC winter disconnection rules apply November 1 through March 31 for regulated electric and gas utilities. Customers must engage with the utility to claim protection.

Does Fuel Assistance cover wood and pellets?

Yes. Vermont Fuel Assistance covers all heating fuels including fuel oil, propane, kerosene, natural gas, electric, wood, and pellets. Wood and pellet households typically receive a flat seasonal benefit.

What's Crisis Fuel?

Year-round emergency fuel voucher program for households facing empty tanks, imminent shutoffs, or heating system failures. Apply through your local CAA. Crisis Fuel is available even after the Seasonal Fuel Assistance window closes April 30.

What is LIHWAP Vermont?

Vermont's water/sewer arrears assistance program — pays past-due water and wastewater bills for income-qualified households. Apply through DCF or your CAA.

Can renters qualify in Vermont?

Yes if you pay your own heating fuel. Tenant heat bundled into rent: a portion of Fuel Assistance can sometimes flow to the landlord, but the regular benefit is harder to access. Crisis Fuel may still apply.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in Vermont?

A Vermont-aware specialist can walk through Seasonal Fuel Assistance, your utility's hardship program, and any crisis pathways in one call. Call 1-833-555-0143
