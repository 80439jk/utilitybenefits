Massachusetts Fuel Assistance: One of the Largest State Programs in the U.S.

Massachusetts Fuel Assistance (federal LIHEAP) is one of the most generous in the country — both higher income limits (60% SMI) and larger benefits than most states. Cold-weather state, heating-focused, with strong utility hardship programs from Eversource and National Grid.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Massachusetts Fuel Assistance (the state's name for federal LIHEAP) is administered by DHCD through 19 local Community Action and Community Service agencies. MA uses 60% of State Median Income as the threshold — more generous than the typical 150% FPL standard most states use. Typical benefits: $700–$1,800 per heating season. Plus the MA Cold Weather Rule protects income-qualified, seniors, and disabled customers from shutoff November 15 through March 15. Major utilities run additional hardship programs that stack.

Why Massachusetts Fuel Assistance is unusually large

Two reasons MA's program is bigger than most states. First, the income threshold uses 60% State Median Income rather than 150% FPL — and Massachusetts has a high state median income, so the dollar threshold is higher (about $52,000 for a 1-person household, $80,000 for a 4-person). Second, Massachusetts winters are cold and heating costs are high, so federal LIHEAP allocations to MA are larger per household than warmer states.

For oil and propane households (common in MA), per-fill benefits are particularly large because fuel costs are higher per BTU than natural gas or electric.

Massachusetts eligibility

Massachusetts uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Massachusetts

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Crisis assistance and shutoff prevention

Facing shutoff in Massachusetts?

MA Cold Weather Rule prohibits shutoffs Nov 15 – Mar 15 for income-qualified, seniors, disabled. A specialist can engage your state's program AND your utility's hardship line in parallel.

Massachusetts-specific FAQ

Why does Massachusetts use 60% SMI instead of 150% FPL?

Federal LIHEAP rules let states use whichever threshold is higher. Because Massachusetts has high state median income, 60% SMI produces a more generous threshold than 150% FPL — so MA uses SMI.

Which agency do I apply through?

Massachusetts is divided into 19 service territories, each with its own designated agency. ABCD covers Boston/metro Boston. Action for Boston Community Development. Find your local agency at mass.gov.

Does MA Fuel Assistance cover oil?

Yes. Heating oil is one of the largest categories in MA Fuel Assistance because so many MA households heat with oil. Per-fill benefits are typically $400–$1,200.

What is HEARTWAP?

Heating Emergency Assistance Retrofit Task Weatherization Program — pays to repair or replace broken heating equipment for income-qualified MA households. Up to $7,000 per system replacement.

How do Eversource New Start and Fuel Assistance interact?

They stack. Eversource New Start forgives 1/24th of arrearages per consecutive on-time monthly payment. Fuel Assistance pays down arrears with the annual benefit. Together they can clear a large arrearage in 12–24 months.

Can renters qualify in MA?

Yes if you pay your own heating bill. Renters with utilities included in rent can sometimes qualify for indirect assistance — application asks about your housing/heating situation.

What is Joe-4-Oil?

Citizens Energy Corporation's program providing free or discounted heating oil to qualifying low-income MA households, funded by Citgo and other partners. Separate from Fuel Assistance but stackable.

Is there Spanish support?

Yes. Most major Community Action Agencies in MA have bilingual Spanish staff. Application materials available in Spanish, Portuguese, Vietnamese, Haitian Creole, and other languages.
