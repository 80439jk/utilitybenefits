Arizona LIHEAP: Summer Cooling Crisis and Year-Round Bill Help

Arizona's federal LIHEAP is cooling-crisis focused given the climate. Layered on top: APS and Tucson Electric Power discount programs, SRP economic price plans, and the unique Critical Care medical-equipment protection.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Arizona's federal LIHEAP is administered by DES through local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 60% of State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $300–$1,200. Unlike most LIHEAP programs, Arizona prioritizes summer cooling crisis assistance — heat-related illness from lack of AC is a public health emergency for vulnerable Arizonans. The AZ Corporation Commission prohibits APS, TEP, and Trico shutoffs when forecast temperatures hit 95°F+ (typically June through September). Critical Care medical-equipment customers receive year-round protection.

Arizona's summer shutoff moratorium

After multiple heat-related deaths in Arizona linked to utility shutoffs in the late 2010s, the Arizona Corporation Commission established a temperature-triggered shutoff moratorium for investor-owned utilities (APS, Tucson Electric Power, Trico Electric Cooperative). When forecast high temperatures hit 95°F or above (typically June through September), residential disconnections for non-payment are prohibited. Protection is automatic — utilities monitor forecasts.

This does not apply to Salt River Project (SRP), which is publicly owned and has its own customer assistance policies, or to municipal utilities. SRP's Economy Price Plan provides discount rates for income-qualified customers year-round.

Arizona eligibility

Arizona uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Arizona

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Arizona-specific FAQ

Why is Arizona's LIHEAP cooling-focused?

Heat-related illness from lack of AC kills more Arizonans than cold-related illness elsewhere. AZ LIHEAP allocates significant funding to summer cooling crises — typically processing applications within 18–48 hours when truly urgent.

How does the summer shutoff moratorium work?

When forecast high temperatures hit 95°F+ in APS, TEP, or Trico territory, the utilities are prohibited from disconnecting residential customers. Protection is automatic — utilities monitor National Weather Service forecasts. Does not apply to SRP or municipal utilities.

What is APS Critical Care Program?

Year-round shutoff protection for APS customers with medical equipment (oxygen, ventilator, dialysis, etc.) regardless of income. Requires physician certification. Apply through APS directly.

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in Arizona?

Through your local Community Action Agency. Maricopa County: Maricopa County Human Services. Pima County: Community Action Agency. Find yours at des.az.gov.

How does APS Energy Support Program (E-3) work?

APS's discount rate tier for income-qualified customers — typically 25-50% off standard rates. Apply through APS with income documentation. Renews annually.

Does Arizona have winter heating assistance?

Yes — winter heating crisis funds available through LIHEAP, though most demand is for summer cooling. Northern Arizona (Flagstaff, etc.) has meaningful winter heating needs.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; cooling crisis funds may still apply.

What about reservation residents?

Tribal members on reservations can apply through their tribal LIHEAP program (Navajo Nation, Tohono O'odham, etc.) or through state DES.
