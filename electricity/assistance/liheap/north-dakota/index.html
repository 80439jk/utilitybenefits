North Dakota LIHEAP: One of the Most Generous Per-Household Benefits

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

North Dakota's federal LIHEAP is administered by DHHS through county social service offices. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $500–$2,500 — among the largest per-household amounts in the country, reflecting brutal North Dakota winters and high heating fuel costs. The federal LIHEAP funding formula weights heating degree days heavily, and ND consistently ranks at or near the top. Plus state-funded supplements during especially cold winters, and rural electric cooperative member assistance programs.

Why North Dakota benefits are so large

North Dakota averages 9,000+ heating degree days per year — about double Texas and 50% more than Pennsylvania. A typical Bismarck household burns 1,000+ therms of natural gas or 800+ gallons of propane per heating season. Combined with rural fuel-delivery premiums in western ND, household heating costs can exceed $3,500/year. The federal LIHEAP allocation formula weights heating degree days, fuel costs, and low-income population — pushing North Dakota's per-household benefits to among the country's largest.

The state legislature has also funded supplemental heating assistance during particularly cold winters, and most ND rural electric cooperatives run Operation Round-Up or member assistance programs that stack with LIHEAP.

North Dakota eligibility

North Dakota uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in North Dakota

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

North Dakota-specific FAQ

Are North Dakota LIHEAP benefits really that large?

Yes. Per-household average benefits in ND consistently rank among the country's largest — often $1,000–$2,500. The federal LIHEAP formula weights heating degree days and fuel costs, both of which are high in ND.

Where do I apply?

Through your county social service office or online at hhs.nd.gov. ND DHHS administers LIHEAP through county-level intake.

When does ND LIHEAP open?

October 1 for general applications. Heating-season window through May 31. Crisis components available throughout.

Does ND have a winter shutoff moratorium?

ND PSC winter rules from November 1 through April 15 require regulated utilities to offer payment plans and delay disconnections for income-qualified customers.

Does LIHEAP cover propane and fuel oil?

Yes. ND LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including natural gas, electric, propane, fuel oil, and wood. Rural propane households often see particularly large per-fill benefits.

What is Operation Round-Up?

Most ND rural electric cooperatives let members round up their bill to the next dollar; the difference funds emergency assistance for members facing utility hardship. Stacks with LIHEAP.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; emergency components may still apply.

Are tribal members eligible?

Yes — tribal members can apply through their tribal LIHEAP program (Standing Rock, Spirit Lake, Three Affiliated Tribes/Mandan-Hidatsa-Arikara, Turtle Mountain Chippewa, Sisseton-Wahpeton) or through state LIHEAP at the county level.
