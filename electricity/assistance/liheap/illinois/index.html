Illinois LIHEAP & PIPP: Monthly Bill Caps for ComEd Customers

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Illinois has two main programs. LIHEAP is the federal annual benefit ($300–$1,500), administered by DCEO through local Community Action Agencies. PIPP (Percentage of Income Payment Plan) is available to ComEd electric customers only and caps your monthly electric bill at 6% of household income. For ComEd-area Illinoisans below 200% FPL, PIPP usually saves more annually than LIHEAP. Apply for both — they stack. Downstate Illinoisans served by Ameren get LIHEAP only.

ComEd customers vs downstate (Ameren) customers

The biggest split in Illinois utility assistance: ComEd electric customers (Chicago and northern Illinois) qualify for PIPP, which caps electric bills at 6% of income. Downstate customers (Ameren Illinois service territory) do not have a PIPP equivalent for electric.

Both groups qualify for federal LIHEAP statewide, plus utility hardship programs (ComEd CHAMP, Ameren Warm Neighbors Cool Friends, Nicor Gas Sharing). If you're a ComEd customer and below 200% FPL, PIPP enrollment is usually your biggest savings — typically $800–$2,000/year.

Illinois eligibility

Illinois uses 200% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Illinois

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Crisis assistance and shutoff prevention

Facing shutoff in Illinois?

Reconnection Assistance year-round; IL has no statutory winter shutoff moratorium but PUC encourages payment plans. A specialist can engage your state's program AND your utility's hardship line in parallel. Call 1-833-555-0143

Illinois-specific FAQ

Am I eligible for PIPP if I'm not with ComEd?

No. PIPP is currently only available to ComEd electric customers. Downstate customers (Ameren Illinois) qualify for federal LIHEAP and utility-specific hardship programs but not PIPP.

How does PIPP enrollment work?

Apply through ComEd directly (online or by phone) once you've been LIHEAP-approved. Your monthly bill caps at 6% of household income, with the difference paid by program funds. Annual recertification required.

When does Illinois LIHEAP open?

Illinois LIHEAP opens September 1 for vulnerable households (60+, disabled, families with kids under 6) and October 1 for general applications. Heating-season window runs through May 31.

Does Illinois have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Not a statutory moratorium like Wisconsin or Minnesota. Illinois PUC encourages payment plans for income-qualified customers but utilities can still disconnect for non-payment. LIHEAP and CHAMP enrollment provides effective protection by paying down arrears.

Where do Chicago residents apply?

Through CEDA (Community and Economic Development Association) — the primary Chicago LIHEAP intake agency. Online at cedaorg.net or by phone. Suburban Cook County: contact your township office.

Can I get LIHEAP if I rent?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit is harder to access but emergency components may apply.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. Illinois LIHEAP covers natural gas, propane, oil, and electric heat. Rural propane households often see higher per-fill benefits.

What is CHAMP?

ComEd's CARE Helping Affordable Monthly Payments — a program that combines PIPP enrollment with arrearage forgiveness for participating customers. 1/24th of past-due balance forgiven per consecutive on-time monthly payment.
