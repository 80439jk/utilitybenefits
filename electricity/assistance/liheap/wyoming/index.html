Wyoming LIEAP: Energy Assistance and Weatherization Statewide

MR
Reviewed by Marcus Reid Energy Policy Analyst · former NARUC staff
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Wyoming's federal LIHEAP is the Low Income Energy Assistance Program (LIEAP), administered by DFS. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $300–$1,500, with higher amounts for rural propane and fuel oil households across western Wyoming. Major utilities — Rocky Mountain Power, Black Hills Energy, MDU — and rural electric cooperatives across Wyoming run additional hardship programs that stack with LIEAP. Plus the Wyoming PSC's winter disconnection protections from November 1 through April 30 — among the longest moratorium windows in the country.

Wyoming's long winter moratorium window

The Wyoming PSC winter disconnection rule covers November 1 through April 30 — six months of protection, among the longest in the country. The rule applies to regulated electric and gas utilities; protected households must contact the utility to claim protection (it's not fully automatic). Income-qualified, seriously ill, elderly, and households with infants are all protected.

Wyoming's heating fuel mix is split: natural gas in cities (Cheyenne, Casper, Rock Springs), and propane or fuel oil across rural western Wyoming. LIEAP benefits scale to match — rural propane households can receive larger per-delivery benefits than urban natural gas households because federal funding scales to actual heating costs.

Wyoming eligibility

Wyoming uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Wyoming

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Wyoming-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIEAP in Wyoming?

Through your county DFS office or online at dfs.wyo.gov. Wyoming DFS administers LIEAP through county-level intake; rural residents may apply by phone or mail.

When does WY LIEAP open?

October 1 for general applications. Heating-season window through April 30. Crisis components available throughout.

Does Wyoming have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Yes — one of the longest in the country. WY PSC rules protect residential customers from disconnection November 1 through April 30. Income-qualified, seriously ill, elderly, and households with infants are all protected. Contact your utility to claim protection.

Does LIEAP cover propane and fuel oil?

Yes. Wyoming LIEAP covers all heating fuels including natural gas, electric, propane, fuel oil, and wood. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is Operation Round-Up?

Most Wyoming rural electric cooperatives let members round up their bill to the next dollar; proceeds fund emergency utility assistance for member households in hardship. Stacks with LIEAP.

Can renters qualify in Wyoming?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; emergency components may still apply.

Are tribal members eligible?

Yes — tribal members can apply through the tribal LIHEAP program for Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho tribes (Wind River Reservation) or through state LIEAP at DFS.

What is Lend a Hand?

Rocky Mountain Power's customer-funded hardship fund administered with the Salvation Army. Provides emergency utility assistance for income-qualified Rocky Mountain Power customers.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in Wyoming?

A Wyoming-aware specialist can walk through LIEAP, your utility's hardship program, and any crisis pathways in one call. Call 1-833-555-0143
