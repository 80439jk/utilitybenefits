New Hampshire Fuel Assistance and the Electric Assistance Program (EAP)

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

New Hampshire stacks two strong programs. Fuel Assistance (FAP) is federal LIHEAP for heating fuel costs ($300–$1,800 annually, paid directly to your fuel dealer). Electric Assistance Program (EAP) is state-funded — a year-round monthly discount of 8% to 76% on your electric bill, varying by income tier. EAP runs through Eversource, Unitil, NH Electric Coop, and Liberty Utilities. Both programs use a 60% SMI income limit and apply through your local Community Action Agency.

The EAP discount tiers — up to 76% off

NH's Electric Assistance Program is one of the most generous utility discount programs in the country. EAP applies a percentage discount to your electric bill based on income tier:

  • Tier 1 (≤75% FPL): 76% discount
  • Tier 2 (76–100% FPL): 61% discount
  • Tier 3 (101–125% FPL): 46% discount
  • Tier 4 (126–150% FPL): 31% discount
  • Tier 5 (151–175% FPL): 16% discount
  • Tier 6 (176–200% FPL): 8% discount

Even Tier 6 households (200% FPL) get a meaningful annual discount. Most low-income NH households should be enrolled in both Fuel Assistance and EAP.

New Hampshire eligibility

New Hampshire uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in New Hampshire

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Crisis assistance and shutoff prevention

Facing shutoff in New Hampshire?

NH PUC requires utilities to defer disconnection Nov 15 – Mar 31 for income-qualified. A specialist can engage your state's program AND your utility's hardship line in parallel. Call (800) 780-9218

New Hampshire-specific FAQ

What's the difference between FAP and EAP?

FAP (Fuel Assistance Program) is federal LIHEAP — a one-time annual benefit for heating fuel ($300–$1,800), paid to your dealer. EAP (Electric Assistance Program) is state-funded — a year-round monthly discount on your electric bill (8–76%). Both stack. Apply for both at your local Community Action Agency.

How much can EAP save me?

Tier 1 households (≤75% FPL) get a 76% discount. For a $200/month electric bill, that's $152/month or $1,824/year. Even Tier 6 (200% FPL) gets 8% off — about $200/year on the same bill.

Where do I apply?

Through your local Community Action Agency. NH has 6 CAA networks covering all 10 counties. Find yours at energy.nh.gov.

When does Fuel Assistance open?

December 1 for general applications, with priority intake earlier for vulnerable households. Heating-season window through April 30.

Does FAP cover oil and propane?

Yes. NH FAP covers all heating fuels including fuel oil, propane, kerosene, natural gas, electric, wood, and pellets. Most NH households heat with oil — FAP is critical to those budgets.

Does NH have a winter moratorium?

NH PUC requires utilities to defer disconnection November 15 through March 31 for income-qualified residential customers. EAP enrollment provides additional protection.

Can renters qualify in NH?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular FAP harder; EAP can still apply if you have an electric account in your name.

Can EAP enrollment be retroactive?

EAP enrollment applies starting the month you're approved — not retroactively. Apply as soon as you suspect eligibility; the discount applies to all future bills until annual recertification.
