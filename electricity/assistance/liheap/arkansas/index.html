Arkansas LIHEAP: Heating, Cooling, and Crisis Help

Arkansas's federal LIHEAP is administered through DHS Division of County Operations and 16 Community Action Agencies. Both heating and cooling components, with utility hardship programs from Entergy AR and OG&E.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Arkansas's federal LIHEAP is administered through DHS by 16 local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $200–$700, split between heating (November-April) and cooling (May-October) seasons. Major utilities — Entergy Arkansas, OG&E, Black Hills Energy, SourceGas — run additional hardship programs.

Arkansas's heating and cooling balance

Arkansas has both meaningful winter heating demand and hot summer cooling demand. Entergy Arkansas serves most of the state for electricity. Most households heat with electric or natural gas; rural areas use propane.

Arkansas eligibility

Arkansas uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Arkansas

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Arkansas-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in Arkansas?

Through your local Community Action Agency. 16 agencies cover all AR counties. Find yours at humanservices.arkansas.gov.

Does Arkansas have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No statutory moratorium. Arkansas PSC encourages payment plans but utilities can disconnect for non-payment year-round.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. Arkansas LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including propane. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is Entergy The Power to Care?

Entergy's customer-funded emergency utility assistance program — administered with Salvation Army across multiple Entergy states (AR, LA, MS, TX).

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder.

When does Arkansas LIHEAP open?

Heating: November 1 through April 30. Cooling: May 1 through October 31. Both have crisis components year-round.

How does cooling assistance work?

Cooling LIHEAP is processed quickly during heat advisories or for households with vulnerable members. Apply through your CAA.

What about Tennessee Valley Authority distributors?

Northeast Arkansas is served by TVA-distributed power. LIHEAP works with TVA distributors as it does with Entergy.
