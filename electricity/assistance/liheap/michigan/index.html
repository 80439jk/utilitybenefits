Michigan MEAP and State Emergency Relief: Heating Help and Crisis Assistance

Michigan combines federal LIHEAP (called MEAP) with State Emergency Relief (SER) for shutoffs and crisis. Layered on top: DTE Energy and Consumers Energy each run their own significant low-income programs.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

MEAP (Michigan Energy Assistance Program) is Michigan's federal LIHEAP, administered by MDHHS through MI Bridges and local DHHS offices. State Emergency Relief (SER) covers emergency utility situations — shutoffs, no fuel, broken heating equipment — with up to $1,000 per crisis episode. Plus DTE Energy and Consumers Energy each have significant low-income programs that stack: DTE Low-Income Self-Sufficiency Plan (LSP) caps your bill at 6% of income (similar to Ohio PIPP); Consumers CARE provides discount rates for income-qualified customers. Cold Weather Rule (Nov 1 – Mar 31) protects vulnerable customers from shutoff.

DTE Low-Income Self-Sufficiency Plan (LSP)

DTE Energy serves Detroit and southeastern Michigan with both gas and electric. DTE's LSP works similarly to Ohio's PIPP Plus: caps your monthly utility bill at 6% of household income, with arrearage forgiveness for consistent on-time payments. For DTE customers below 200% FPL, LSP enrollment is often the largest single savings — $1,000–$2,500/year.

Consumers Energy (Lower Peninsula gas/electric except DTE territory and west Michigan) runs CARE — a tiered discount-rate program for low-income customers. Less generous than LSP but easier to qualify for and stack with MEAP.

Michigan eligibility

How to apply in Michigan

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Michigan-specific FAQ

What's the difference between MEAP and SER?

MEAP is the regular annual heating benefit. SER (State Emergency Relief) is for emergencies — shutoffs, no fuel, broken heating equipment. Both are administered by MDHHS and use the same MI Bridges portal.

Does Michigan have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Yes — the Cold Weather Rule protects seniors 65+, low-income customers (below 150% FPL), and medical-equipment customers from disconnection November 1 through March 31. You must actively engage your utility and may need to enroll in a payment plan.

How does DTE LSP work?

DTE's Low-Income Self-Sufficiency Plan caps your monthly utility bill at 6% of household income. Arrearage forgiveness for consistent on-time payments — 1/24th of past-due balance forgiven per month. Apply through DTE directly after MEAP approval.

Can I get help with my furnace?

Yes. MEAP and SER both include heating equipment repair and replacement for qualifying households. Apply through MI Bridges with a contractor's estimate.

How does the Home Heating Credit work?

Michigan offers a tax credit for low-income households that paid heating costs. Claim on Michigan Form MI-1040CR-7 with your state taxes. Up to $700+ depending on household size and income.

Does MEAP cover propane?

Yes. MEAP covers all heating fuels — natural gas, electric, propane, oil, wood. Propane households often have larger benefits because of higher fuel costs.

What is THAW?

The Heat And Warmth Fund — a Michigan nonprofit funded by utility customers and donors that provides emergency utility assistance, especially when MEAP and SER are tapped out. THAW is often the fastest source of help.

Can I apply for MEAP online?

Yes. MI Bridges at newmibridges.michigan.gov is the online application portal. You can apply for MEAP, SNAP, Medicaid, and other programs in one form.
