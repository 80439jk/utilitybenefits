Virginia Energy Assistance Program: LIHEAP Plus the Cold Weather Rule

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Virginia's Energy Assistance Program has three components: Fuel Assistance (annual heating benefit, $200–$700), Crisis Assistance (year-round emergencies, up to $1,000), and Cooling Assistance (summer cooling for vulnerable households). Income limit: 150% FPL. Plus Virginia's unique Cold Weather Rule — a temperature-triggered shutoff moratorium that activates whenever forecast temperatures drop below 32°F, statewide. Application windows are unusually short: Fuel Assistance October 15 – November 15.

Virginia's temperature-triggered Cold Weather Rule

Virginia is one of a small number of states (along with Missouri, Kansas, Colorado) that uses a temperature-triggered shutoff moratorium rather than fixed date ranges. Whenever forecast temperatures drop to 32°F or below, Dominion Energy Virginia and other utilities are prohibited from disconnecting residential customers for non-payment. Protection is automatic — you don't need to enroll.

This is more flexible than fixed-date moratoriums in unusual weather years. The trade-off: protection ends as soon as the forecast warms up, which can leave households exposed in March/April warm spells.

Virginia eligibility

Virginia uses 150% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Virginia

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Virginia-specific FAQ

When does Virginia Energy Assistance open?

Fuel Assistance has an unusually short window: October 15 through November 15 only. Apply early. Crisis Assistance is year-round; Cooling Assistance opens June 15 for vulnerable households.

What is Virginia's Cold Weather Rule?

A temperature-triggered shutoff moratorium that prohibits utilities from disconnecting residential customers whenever forecast temperatures drop to 32°F or below. Statewide, automatic — no enrollment required.

Where do I apply?

Through your local Department of Social Services or online at CommonHelp (commonhelp.virginia.gov). Find local DSS at dss.virginia.gov.

Why is the application window so short?

Virginia's federal LIHEAP allocation is processed in a tight window so that benefits can be applied before the heating season begins in earnest. Apply October 15 – November 15 to receive Fuel Assistance for the season.

Can I get Crisis Assistance outside the Fuel window?

Yes. Crisis Assistance is year-round for documented emergencies (shutoff, no heat, no fuel).

Does VA Energy Assistance cover propane?

Yes. Covers all heating fuels including propane and oil. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is Dominion EnergyShare?

Dominion Energy's largest customer-funded emergency assistance program — provides bill payment help for income-qualified Dominion customers. Administered through partnered nonprofits.

Can renters qualify in Virginia?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular Fuel Assistance harder; Crisis Assistance for emergencies may still apply.
