Colorado LEAP: Heating Help and Year-Round Crisis Support

Colorado's LEAP (Low-Income Energy Assistance Program) is administered by CDHS and 64 county departments. Plus Colorado's unique cold-weather rule that's temperature-triggered, not date-based.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Colorado's LEAP (Low-Income Energy Assistance Program) is the federal LIHEAP, administered by CDHS through 64 county departments. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $300–$1,000 with crisis components for shutoffs and broken heating equipment. Application window November 1 through April 30. Plus Colorado's temperature-triggered Cold Weather Rule blocks shutoffs during extreme weather. Major utilities — Xcel Energy, Black Hills Energy, Atmos — run additional hardship programs.

Colorado's high-altitude heating dynamics

Colorado's high altitude makes for cold, dry winters with significant heating demand even in the Front Range cities. Mountain communities (Aspen, Vail, Steamboat) have extreme heating costs — natural gas and propane are the main heating fuels. Xcel Energy serves most of the population (Denver metro, Boulder, Fort Collins). Black Hills Energy serves Pueblo and Southern Colorado. Mountain communities often rely on Atmos Energy or Source Gas. LEAP applies regardless of utility.

Colorado eligibility

Colorado uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Colorado

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Colorado-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LEAP in Colorado?

Through your county Department of Human Services or online via PEAK (peak.colorado.gov). 64 counties have application offices. Find yours at cdhs.colorado.gov/leap.

How does Colorado's Cold Weather Rule work?

Colorado uses temperature-triggered protections rather than fixed dates. During extreme cold, utilities cannot disconnect residential service. Specifics vary by utility — Xcel Energy follows broader PUC guidelines.

What is Energy Outreach Colorado?

EOC is a statewide nonprofit funded by Xcel Energy customers and others — provides emergency utility assistance for households who don't qualify for or have exhausted LEAP. Often the fastest source of help.

Can renters qualify in Colorado?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; crisis components may still apply.

Does LEAP cover propane?

Yes. Colorado LEAP covers all heating fuels including propane and oil. Mountain communities relying on propane often see larger per-fill benefits.

How much can LEAP actually save?

Typical $300–$1,000 per heating season for cash assistance. Crisis Intervention can add $300+ for emergencies. Large mountain heating bills can exceed program benefits — EOC and utility hardship programs fill the gap.

What about LEAP recipients in mountain communities?

Mountain LEAP recipients often need both LEAP and EOC for adequate winter coverage. Mountain heating costs can run $300–$500/month. Apply to both programs early in the season.

Does Xcel offer a discount rate?

Xcel Energy participates in Colorado's CARE program (with EOC) — emergency assistance rather than ongoing discount rate. Apply through EOC or your CAA after LEAP enrollment.
