Tennessee LIHEAP: Heating, Cooling, and Crisis Help Statewide

Tennessee's federal LIHEAP is administered through THDA and 19 local Community Action Agencies. TVA serves most of Tennessee through 154 local power companies. Plus utility-specific programs from Memphis Light, NES, and Chattanooga EPB.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Tennessee's federal LIHEAP is administered through THDA by 19 local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $300–$800, with crisis components for both heating and cooling shutoff threats. Most of Tennessee's electricity comes from TVA (Tennessee Valley Authority), distributed through 154 local power companies including Memphis Light Gas & Water, Nashville Electric Service (NES), Chattanooga EPB, and many municipal/cooperative utilities. Each runs its own hardship program.

TVA distribution model and what it means for assistance

Unlike most states, Tennessee doesn't have a few large investor-owned utilities. TVA generates wholesale power and 154 local distributors (municipal utilities and cooperatives) sell it retail. Each distributor sets its own retail rates and hardship programs. Memphis Light Gas & Water has Plus-1 emergency fund; Nashville Electric Service has Project Help; Chattanooga EPB has the Helping Hand fund.

For LIHEAP applications, your local CAA is the entry point regardless of which utility serves you. Benefits are paid to the utility on your behalf.

Tennessee eligibility

Tennessee uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Tennessee

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Crisis assistance and shutoff prevention

Facing shutoff in Tennessee?

TN does not have winter or summer shutoff moratorium; TPUC encourages payment plans. A specialist can engage your state's program AND your utility's hardship line in parallel.

Tennessee-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in Tennessee?

Through your local Community Action Agency. 19 agencies cover all 95 TN counties. Find yours at thda.org.

Does Tennessee have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No statutory moratorium. TN PUC encourages payment plans for income-qualified customers but utilities can disconnect for non-payment year-round. LIHEAP enrollment provides effective protection by addressing arrears.

Why are there so many different Tennessee utilities?

TVA is wholesale-only; 154 local distributors sell power retail. Each is a municipal utility or electric cooperative. Each sets its own hardship policies. For LIHEAP, your CAA is the entry point regardless of which distributor serves you.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. TN LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including propane and oil. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is TVA EnergyRight?

TVA's energy efficiency program — free home energy audits and weatherization upgrades for income-qualified TVA customers. Available through participating local utilities.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; emergency components may still apply.

What is Plus-1?

Memphis Light Gas & Water's emergency utility assistance fund — customers can voluntarily round up their bill to support the fund. Administered with the Salvation Army.

Can I apply if I'm with a cooperative utility?

Yes. LIHEAP works regardless of utility type — investor-owned, municipal, or cooperative. Cooperative members may also have member-specific assistance through Operation Round-Up programs.
