Montana LIEAP: Heating, Energy Share, and Weatherization

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Montana's federal LIHEAP is the Low Income Energy Assistance Program (LIEAP), administered by DPHHS through 10 local Human Resource Development Councils (HRDCs). Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $300–$1,200, with significantly higher amounts for rural propane and fuel oil households. Plus Energy Share of Montana — a statewide nonprofit that fills emergency gaps when LIEAP is depleted — and LIWAP weatherization for permanent home efficiency upgrades.

Montana's rural fuel mix and big benefits

Outside Billings, Bozeman, Missoula, and Great Falls, much of Montana heats with fuel oil, propane, or wood. Winter heating costs in eastern Montana can exceed $3,000/year for a typical household. LIEAP benefit amounts reflect that — Montana's per-household average is among the highest in the country, often $700–$1,200.

For households that don't qualify for LIEAP (60% SMI cap), Energy Share of Montana is the next stop. It's a statewide nonprofit funded by utility customers and donors, providing emergency heating assistance to households facing shutoffs or empty fuel tanks. Apply through your local HRDC.

Montana eligibility

Montana uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Montana

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Crisis assistance and shutoff prevention

Facing shutoff in Montana?

MT PSC requires utilities to offer payment plans Nov 1 – Apr 1 for residential customers. A specialist can engage your state's program AND your utility's hardship line in parallel. Call 1-833-555-0143

Montana-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIEAP in Montana?

Through your local Human Resource Development Council (HRDC) — 10 HRDCs cover all 56 MT counties. Find yours at dphhs.mt.gov.

When does Montana LIEAP open?

October 1 for vulnerable households (60+, disabled, kids under 6) and a week or two later for general applications. Heating-season window through April 30.

Does Montana have a winter moratorium?

Not a strict statutory moratorium, but Montana PSC requires regulated utilities to offer payment plans and avoid disconnections November 1 through April 1 for households making good-faith payment efforts.

What's Energy Share of Montana?

Statewide nonprofit providing emergency heating assistance to households who don't qualify for LIEAP or whose LIEAP benefit is exhausted. Funded by utility customer donations. Accessed through local HRDCs.

Does LIEAP cover propane and fuel oil?

Yes. Montana LIEAP covers all heating fuels including natural gas, electric, propane, fuel oil, wood, and pellets. Rural propane and oil households often see larger per-fill benefits.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder; emergency components may still apply.

What is LIWAP?

Montana's Low Income Weatherization Assistance Program — free home weatherization (insulation, sealing, sometimes window/furnace work) for income-qualified households. Apply through your local HRDC.

Are tribal members eligible?

Yes — tribal members can apply through their tribal LIHEAP program (Blackfeet, Crow, Northern Cheyenne, Salish-Kootenai, etc.) or through state LIEAP at their HRDC.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in Montana?

A Montana-aware specialist can walk through LIEAP, your utility's hardship program, and any crisis pathways in one call. Call 1-833-555-0143
