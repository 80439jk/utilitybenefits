Utah HEAT: Home Energy Assistance Target Program

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Utah's HEAT (Home Energy Assistance Target) is the federal LIHEAP, administered through DWS. Income limit: 150% FPL. Annual benefits typically $300–$700, with crisis funds for emergencies. Application window November 1 through April 30. Major utilities — Rocky Mountain Power, Dominion Energy, Questar Gas — run additional hardship programs.

Utah's heating dynamics

Utah has cold winters along the Wasatch Front (Salt Lake City, Provo) and very cold conditions in mountain communities (Park City, Heber). Most households heat with natural gas (Dominion Energy, formerly Questar Gas). Rural areas often use propane. Rocky Mountain Power serves most of Utah for electricity. HEAT works regardless of utility.

Utah eligibility

Utah uses 150% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Utah

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Crisis assistance and shutoff prevention

Facing shutoff in Utah?

UT does not have statutory winter moratorium; PSC encourages payment plans. A specialist can engage your state's program AND your utility's hardship line in parallel. Call 1-833-555-0143

Utah-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for HEAT?

Online at heat.utah.gov or through your local DWS office. Online is fastest.

Does Utah have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No statutory moratorium. Utah PSC encourages payment plans for income-qualified customers but utilities can disconnect for non-payment.

What is Rocky Mountain Power Lend a Hand?

Customer-funded emergency utility assistance program — administered with Salvation Army. Provides emergency bill help for income-qualified RMP customers.

Does HEAT cover propane?

Yes. Utah HEAT covers all heating fuels including propane. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder.

How does HEAT differ from utility hardship programs?

HEAT is a one-time annual benefit (federal LIHEAP). Rocky Mountain Power Lend a Hand and Dominion Helping Hands are utility-funded emergency programs that stack on top.

What about Park City and mountain communities?

Mountain communities have higher heating costs but the same income limits apply. Apply through Summit County DWS or Wasatch County DWS for local processing.

Spanish support available?

Yes. DWS offices have bilingual Spanish staff. Application materials in Spanish.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in Utah?

A Utah-aware specialist can walk through HEAT, your utility's hardship program, and any crisis pathways in one call. Call 1-833-555-0143
