Nebraska LIHEAP: Heating Help and Conditional Winter Protection

Nebraska's federal LIHEAP is administered through DHHS and 9 local Community Action Agencies. Plus a conditional winter shutoff moratorium that protects households on payment plans.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Nebraska's federal LIHEAP is administered through DHHS by 9 local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 150% FPL. Annual benefits typically $300–$800. Nebraska shutoff moratorium November 1 through March 31 for households with active payment arrangements. NPPD and OPPD (the state's two largest electric utilities) are publicly owned. Black Hills Energy serves much of the gas market.

Nebraska's publicly-owned utility model

Nebraska is unique in being the only state with 100% publicly-owned electric utilities — Nebraska Public Power District (NPPD) and Omaha Public Power District (OPPD) are the two largest, alongside dozens of municipal utilities and rural cooperatives. This means no investor-owned electric utility hardship programs in the traditional sense, but each public/cooperative utility has its own assistance programs. LIHEAP works with all of them.

Nebraska eligibility

Nebraska uses 150% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Nebraska

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Nebraska-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in Nebraska?

Through ACCESSNebraska (online) or your local Community Action Agency. 9 agencies cover all NE counties.

Does Nebraska have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Yes — November 1 through March 31, but only for households with active payment arrangements with their utility. Engage your utility before winter to set up a payment plan.

How does the public utility model affect LIHEAP?

LIHEAP works with public utilities (NPPD, OPPD, LES) the same way it works with investor-owned utilities elsewhere — benefits are paid directly to your utility account.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. Nebraska LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including propane. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is OPPD EAP?

OPPD's customer-funded Energy Assistance Program — provides emergency utility assistance for income-qualified Omaha-area customers.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder.

When does Nebraska LIHEAP open?

Year-round for general applications. Heating-season priority November through April.

Spanish support available?

Yes. ACCESSNebraska and most Community Action Agencies have Spanish-language support.
