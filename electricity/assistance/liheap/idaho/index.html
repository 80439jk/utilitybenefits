Idaho LIHEAP: Heating Assistance Through Community Action Agencies

Idaho's federal LIHEAP is administered through Idaho Health & Welfare and 6 Community Action Agencies. Plus utility-specific programs from Idaho Power, Avista, and Intermountain Gas.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Idaho's federal LIHEAP is administered through DHW by 6 local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $200–$1,000. Application window: November 1 – March 31 for general households; October 1 for vulnerable households (60+, disabled, families with kids under 6). Major utilities — Idaho Power, Avista, Intermountain Gas — run additional hardship programs.

Idaho's geographic split

Idaho's geography splits service territories: Idaho Power serves most of southern Idaho (Boise area). Avista serves northern Idaho (Coeur d'Alene/Lewiston). Intermountain Gas serves much of the natural gas market. Rocky Mountain Power serves some eastern Idaho areas. Mountain communities (Sun Valley, Stanley) have higher heating costs. Most Idaho households heat with natural gas; rural areas often use propane or wood.

Idaho eligibility

Idaho uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Idaho

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Idaho-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in Idaho?

Through your local Community Action Agency. 6 agencies cover all ID counties. Find yours at healthandwelfare.idaho.gov.

Does Idaho have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No statutory moratorium. Idaho PUC encourages payment plans for income-qualified customers. Medical hardship customers receive year-round protection with physician certification.

Does LIHEAP cover propane?

Yes. Idaho LIHEAP covers all heating fuels including propane. Rural propane households often see larger per-fill benefits.

What is Idaho Power Project Share?

Idaho Power's customer-funded emergency utility assistance program — administered with Salvation Army.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder.

When does Idaho LIHEAP open?

Vulnerable households (60+, disabled, families with kids under 6) October 1; general applications November 1. Heating-season window through March 31.

What about Avista vs Idaho Power?

Avista serves northern Idaho (Coeur d'Alene area); Idaho Power serves southern Idaho (Boise area). LIHEAP works with both — your CAA will route benefits to whichever utility serves your address.

Spanish support available?

Yes. Most Idaho Community Action Agencies have bilingual Spanish staff.
