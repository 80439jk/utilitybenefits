Maine HEAP: Heating Help with Heavy Oil Heat Coverage

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Maine's HEAP (Home Energy Assistance Program) is the federal LIHEAP, administered through MaineHousing by 9 local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 60% State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $500–$1,800. Maine has the highest oil-heating share of any U.S. state — per-fill oil benefits can exceed $1,200. Maine shutoff moratorium November 15 through April 15 for households with active payment plans. Major utilities — Versant Power, Central Maine Power — run additional hardship programs.

Maine's heating oil dominance

Maine has the highest share of heating oil usage of any state — over 60% of Maine households heat with oil, compared to under 5% nationally. Per-fill oil prices can exceed $1,000 for a tank refill, especially in cold winters. HEAP-Maine accordingly has larger per-household benefits than warm-weather states. Benefits are paid directly to oil delivery companies. Versant Power serves Eastern and Northern Maine for electricity; Central Maine Power serves Southern and Central Maine. Natural gas is concentrated in southern/coastal cities (Portland, Lewiston).

Maine eligibility

Maine uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Maine

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Maine-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for HEAP in Maine?

Through your local Community Action Agency. 9 agencies cover all ME counties. Find yours at mainehousing.org.

Does Maine have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Yes — November 15 through April 15 for households with active payment arrangements. You typically need a payment plan with your utility for automatic protection.

Why are Maine's heating bills so high?

Maine has cold winters AND high oil-heating dependence. A typical Maine household using 800 gallons/year at $4/gallon spends $3,200/year on heating oil — much higher than gas-heated households elsewhere.

How are oil benefits paid?

HEAP pays oil delivery companies directly on behalf of qualifying households. The oil company schedules deliveries; you don't see the money.

What is Joe-4-Oil?

Citizens Energy Corporation's program providing free or discounted heating oil to qualifying low-income Maine households, funded by Citgo and other partners. Stacks with HEAP.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own heating bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular HEAP harder; emergency components may apply.

Does HEAP cover propane and wood?

Yes. HEAP covers all heating fuels including propane, wood, and electric. Rural Maine households burning wood can get assistance for wood purchases through some CAAs.

What about heating system repair?

Maine ARRA provides heating system repair/replacement for income-qualified households — up to $4,500. Apply through your local CAA with a contractor's estimate.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in Maine?

A Maine-aware specialist can walk through HEAP, your utility's hardship program, and any crisis pathways in one call. Call 1-833-555-0143
