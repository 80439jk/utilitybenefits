Pennsylvania LIHEAP, CRISIS, LIURP, and Utility CAP Rates

PA stacks four programs: federal LIHEAP for cash assistance, CRISIS for emergencies, LIURP for weatherization, and Customer Assistance Program (CAP) discount rates with each major utility. Most low-income PA households should be on at least two.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Pennsylvania's LIHEAP has three components: Cash Assistance ($300–$1,000 typical, paid annually to your utility), CRISIS (emergency funds for shutoffs and broken heating equipment), and LIURP (Low-Income Usage Reduction Program — free weatherization). Stacked on top: Customer Assistance Program (CAP) rates from PECO, PPL, Duquesne Light, FirstEnergy, and PGW that cap your monthly bill at a percentage of income. Plus the PA Cold Weather Rule protects income-qualified customers from disconnection December 1 through March 31.

Why CAP rates matter as much as LIHEAP

Pennsylvania's Customer Assistance Programs (CAPs) are utility-administered discount-rate tiers that cap your monthly electric or gas bill at a percentage of household income — typically 4–10% depending on the utility. For a $30,000-income household with $200/month bills, CAP can reduce the bill to $100/month. That's $1,200/year in savings on top of LIHEAP's annual benefit. Most PA low-income households should be on CAP and LIHEAP.

CAP enrollment is through your utility, not DHS. PECO has CAP. PPL has OnTrack. Duquesne Light has Smart Comfort. PGW (Philadelphia Gas Works) has CRP. Each requires separate enrollment and annual recertification.

How to apply in Pennsylvania

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Pennsylvania-specific FAQ

Is PA LIHEAP the same as Cash Assistance?

LIHEAP Cash Assistance is one of three PA LIHEAP components. The other two are CRISIS (emergency funds) and LIURP (weatherization). All three are administered by DHS. Apply through your County Assistance Office.

How is CAP different from LIHEAP?

CAP (Customer Assistance Program) is a utility-administered discount-rate tier that caps your monthly bill at a percentage of income. LIHEAP is a federal annual cash benefit. They stack — most low-income PA households should be on both. Apply for CAP through your utility (PECO, PPL, Duquesne, etc.); apply for LIHEAP through DHS.

What is the Cold Weather Rule?

PA's PUC requires utilities to delay disconnection of essential service December 1 through March 31 for residential customers with incomes at or below 250% FPL who actively engage with their utility. You must contact your utility — protection isn't automatic.

Can renters qualify in Pennsylvania?

Yes for both LIHEAP and CAP if you pay your own utility bill. If utilities are bundled into rent, the regular benefit is harder to access but CRISIS may still help for verified emergencies.

What's LIURP?

Low-Income Usage Reduction Program — free home weatherization (insulation, sealing, sometimes appliance replacement) for income-qualified PA utility customers. Funded by utility ratepayers; administered by your utility or DHS-contracted agencies.

Does LIHEAP cover oil and propane?

Yes. PA LIHEAP covers all heating fuels — natural gas, oil, propane, electric. Oil and propane households often see higher per-unit benefits because of higher costs.

How do I apply through COMPASS?

Pennsylvania's COMPASS portal at compass.state.pa.us is the online application for LIHEAP, SNAP, Medicaid, and other programs. You can apply for all three in one form. Application opens November 1 each year.

What if I'm with a deregulated electric supplier?

PA is a deregulated market for electricity supply. Your CAP enrollment is with your distribution utility (PECO, PPL, Duquesne, FirstEnergy) and applies to the distribution portion of your bill. The supply portion can still be shopped competitively, though many CAP customers benefit from staying on default service.
