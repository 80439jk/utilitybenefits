California LIHEAP & CARE: The Most Generous Stacked Utility Benefits in the U.S.

California stacks four programs: federal LIHEAP through CSD, CARE/FERA discount rates from PG&E/SCE/SDG&E, Medical Baseline for households with medical conditions, and ECIP for crisis. Most CA households qualify for at least two.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

California uses 200% of the federal poverty level as the LIHEAP cutoff — one of the most generous in the country. The state's stacked benefits include CARE (30–35% bill discount through PG&E, SCE, and SDG&E), FERA (an 18% discount for households slightly above CARE income limits), Medical Baseline (discounted rates for households with medical conditions), and federal LIHEAP through the California Department of Community Services and Development (CSD). Combined, qualifying households often save $1,000–$2,500 per year.

How California's stacked benefits work

California is unusual because it operates four overlapping low-income utility programs — each with slightly different eligibility, each independent of the others. The strategy that maximizes savings is to apply to all qualifying programs simultaneously, not sequentially:

  • LIHEAP (federal, administered by CSD) — annual benefit of typically $400–$1,500 paid as a one-time credit
  • CARE (utility-administered) — ongoing 30–35% monthly bill discount
  • FERA (utility-administered) — ongoing 18% discount for households slightly above CARE income limits
  • Medical Baseline (utility-administered) — discounted baseline rate for households with qualifying medical conditions
  • ECIP (state, crisis-only) — energy crisis intervention payment when a shutoff is imminent

You can combine LIHEAP + CARE + Medical Baseline simultaneously. CARE and FERA are mutually exclusive (you qualify for one or the other based on income tier).

California eligibility

CA uses 200% FPL for LIHEAP, the more generous federal cutoff. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: if your household receives SNAP/CalFresh, SSI, Medi-Cal, or CalWORKs (TANF), you qualify without income paperwork.

How to apply in California

  1. Apply for CARE/FERA first — directly through your utility's website (PG&E, SCE, SDG&E). Online application takes ~5 minutes; discount typically applies within 1–2 billing cycles.
  2. Apply for LIHEAP through CSD — through your local CSD-funded service provider. Find yours at csd.ca.gov. The list is organized by county.
  3. Apply for Medical Baseline — if anyone in your household has a qualifying medical condition (heart, lung, diabetes, life-support equipment, etc.). Application requires a doctor's certification. Through your utility.
  4. For ECIP / Crisis — if you have a shutoff notice, contact your local CSD provider OR call us at (800) 555-1234.
  5. Recertify annually for CARE/FERA; LIHEAP requires fresh annual application.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP/CalFresh letter, SSI letter, etc.)
  • Most recent PG&E, SCE, or SDG&E bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For Medical Baseline: doctor's certification of the qualifying condition

California-specific FAQ

What's the difference between CARE and LIHEAP in California?

CARE is an ongoing 30–35% discount on your monthly utility bill, administered by PG&E, SCE, or SDG&E directly. LIHEAP is a one-time annual benefit of $400–$1,500 administered by the federal government through California's CSD. They stack — most CARE-eligible households also qualify for LIHEAP and should apply for both.

I'm on CARE — do I need to apply for LIHEAP separately?

Yes. CARE and LIHEAP are administered by different agencies (your utility vs CSD) and require separate applications. They don't auto-enroll each other.

Does Medical Baseline require a doctor's note?

Yes. Medical Baseline requires a physician's certification of a qualifying medical condition (heart, lung, kidney, diabetes, multiple sclerosis, life-support equipment, paraplegia, or others). The form is on your utility's website. Once approved, the increased baseline rate is automatic.

What if my income is between CARE and FERA?

You qualify for FERA (the 18% discount for slightly higher income tiers). FERA is for 4+ person households with income between roughly 250% and 400% FPL. Smaller households use CARE.

Does CARE work in deregulated areas like LADWP?

CARE is an investor-owned utility program (PG&E, SCE, SDG&E). LADWP is a municipal utility and runs its own equivalent program called EZ-SAVE. The application is through LADWP directly. SMUD (Sacramento) has SMUD's own discount program.

Can renters qualify in California?

Yes for CARE and LIHEAP — as long as you pay your own utility bill. If utilities are bundled into rent, the regular benefit is harder to access but the crisis component (ECIP) may still help.

How fast does CARE start?

1–2 billing cycles after approval. PG&E, SCE, and SDG&E typically apply the discount on the next bill after they receive proof of eligibility (pay stubs or categorical letter).

What happens if I move within California?

If you stay with the same utility, just update your address — your CARE/FERA enrollment carries over. If you move to a different utility's service area, you need to reapply with the new utility. LIHEAP is portable across CSD providers.
