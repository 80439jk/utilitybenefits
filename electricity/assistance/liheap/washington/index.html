Washington LIHEAP: Heating Help Through Local Community Action Agencies

UB
Reviewed by UtilityBenefits Editorial Team Energy & utilities research desk · cited primary sources
Last reviewed May 4, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Washington's federal LIHEAP is administered through the state Commerce Department by 30 local Community Action Agencies. Income limit: 60% of State Median Income. Annual benefits typically $200–$1,200, with vulnerable households (seniors, disabled, kids under 6) eligible for higher amounts. Applications accepted year-round but priority given October through April for heating-season needs. Major utilities — Puget Sound Energy, Avista, Pacific Power — run additional hardship programs that stack.

Washington's mixed climate dynamics

Western Washington (Seattle, Olympia, Bellingham) has mild winters but increasingly hot summers — air conditioning is becoming a meaningful share of household energy spend. Eastern Washington (Spokane, Tri-Cities) has colder winters and hotter summers. LIHEAP funds in WA can be applied to either heating or cooling, with most going to heating. Crisis funds work for both.

Washington eligibility

Washington uses 60% of State Median Income — a state-specific threshold that's typically more generous than 150% FPL. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Washington

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Crisis assistance and shutoff prevention

Facing shutoff in Washington?

Washington-specific FAQ

Where do I apply for LIHEAP in Washington?

Through your local Community Action Agency. Find yours at commerce.wa.gov/serving-communities/community-services/energy-assistance. 30 agencies cover all WA counties.

Is Seattle City Light's discount different from LIHEAP?

Yes. Seattle's Utility Discount Program is a 60% rate discount for income-qualified Seattle City Light customers — administered by the city, separate from LIHEAP. Apply for both if you live in Seattle.

Does Washington have a winter shutoff moratorium?

No statutory winter moratorium. Washington UTC requires utilities to offer payment plans before shutoff for income-qualified customers. Medical equipment customers receive year-round protection with physician certification.

Can I get help in summer for AC bills?

Yes. WA LIHEAP can be applied to cooling bills, especially for vulnerable households (seniors, kids under 6, medical conditions). Crisis funds work year-round.

How does PSE HELP work?

Puget Sound Energy's Home Energy Lifeline Program — provides bill credits for income-qualified PSE customers. Apply through PSE directly or through your CAA.

Does LIHEAP cover propane and oil?

Yes. Covers all heating fuels. Rural Washington households on propane or oil often see larger per-fill benefits.

Can renters qualify?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Renters with utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder to access.

How do I apply if I'm in a tribal area?

Tribal members can apply through their tribal LIHEAP program (if their tribe administers federal LIHEAP) or through the state CAA. Tribal-specific programs may have additional benefits.

Want to know exactly what you qualify for in Washington?

A Washington-aware specialist can walk through LIHEAP, your utility's hardship program, and any crisis pathways in one call. Call 1-833-555-0143
