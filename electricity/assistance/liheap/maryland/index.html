Maryland MEAP and EUSP: LIHEAP Plus Monthly Electric Bill Credit

Maryland's MEAP (the state's LIHEAP) provides annual cash assistance. EUSP (Electric Universal Service Program) provides monthly bill credits for qualifying electric customers. Both administered through the Office of Home Energy Programs.

By Sara Khan · Reviewed by Marcus Reid · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Maryland's MEAP (Maryland Energy Assistance Program) is the state's federal LIHEAP — annual cash assistance of $300–$2,000 for households at or below 175% FPL. Stacked on top: EUSP (Electric Universal Service Program), a monthly credit on your electric bill that varies based on income and usage. USPP (Utility Service Protection Program) provides shutoff protection for households on payment plans. All three administered by the Office of Home Energy Programs (OHEP).

Maryland is deregulated — gas and electric supply choice

Maryland is one of the 14 deregulated electricity markets and one of the 6 deregulated gas markets. Customers can shop for supply separately from their distribution utility (BGE, Pepco, Delmarva Power, Potomac Edison for electric; BGE, Washington Gas, Columbia Gas for gas). MEAP and EUSP apply regardless of which supplier you choose — the credit is on the distribution portion of your bill, paid by OHEP through your utility.

Maryland eligibility

Maryland uses 175% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in Maryland

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

Crisis assistance and shutoff prevention

Facing shutoff in Maryland?

Maryland PSC requires payment plans before shutoff for income-qualified customers Nov 1 – Mar 31. A specialist can engage your state's program AND your utility's hardship line in parallel.

Maryland-specific FAQ

What's the difference between MEAP and EUSP?

MEAP is a one-time annual cash benefit ($300–$2,000) paid directly to your utility. EUSP is an ongoing monthly credit on your electric bill. They stack — apply for both.

Does Maryland have a winter shutoff moratorium?

Maryland PSC requires utilities to offer payment plans before shutoff for income-qualified customers November 1 through March 31. USPP enrollment provides year-round shutoff protection while you remain on the payment plan.

Can I apply for MEAP if I'm shopping for a deregulated supplier?

Yes. MEAP and EUSP work regardless of which supply company you choose. Credits apply to the distribution portion of your bill (BGE, Pepco, etc.).

How do I find my local OHEP office?

Application is online through MyDHR or in person at your county's Department of Social Services / OHEP office. List at dhs.maryland.gov.

Does MEAP cover oil?

Yes. MEAP covers all heating fuels including oil and propane. Oil benefits are paid directly to fuel vendors.

What is BGE ARP?

BGE's Arrearage Retirement Program — forgives part of past-due balances for income-qualified customers who maintain payment plans. Up to $2,400 in arrearage forgiveness over 24 months.

Can renters qualify in Maryland?

Yes if you pay your own utility bill. Tenant utilities bundled into rent: regular benefit harder to access but emergency components available.

Is Spanish support available?

Yes. OHEP application materials are in Spanish; bilingual staff at most county offices.
