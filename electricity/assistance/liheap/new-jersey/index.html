Why USF, not LIHEAP, is the lead program in NJ

New Jersey's Universal Service Fund (USF) was created by state law in 1998 specifically to cap utility costs at a percentage of household income — meaningfully larger than federal LIHEAP for most participants. USF caps your annual gas and electric bills combined at 6% of household income (or 3% if you heat with electricity). The credit is applied monthly to your utility bill — you see it on every bill, not as a one-time annual payment.

LIHEA (federal LIHEAP in NJ) is a separate annual benefit, typically $200–$700, paid as a one-time credit during the heating season. Apply for both — they stack and use the same application.

New Jersey eligibility

New Jersey uses 200% of the federal poverty level. Categorical eligibility shortcuts apply: SNAP, SSI, TANF, and (in many states) Medicaid recipients qualify automatically without income paperwork.

How to apply in New Jersey

  1. Confirm eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or contact your local administering agency.
  2. Gather documentation — see Section 4 below. If categorical (SNAP, SSI, TANF), you can skip income paperwork.
  3. Apply — through your local Community Action Agency, county DSS, or state online portal.
  4. Wait for approval — typically 30–45 days for regular benefits, 18–48 hours for crisis applications.
  5. Benefit paid directly to utility — or fuel vendor for oil/propane households.

Documents you'll need

  • Government-issued photo ID for the head of household
  • Social Security cards (or numbers) for all household members
  • Proof of income for the past 30 days, OR proof of categorical participation (SNAP, SSI, TANF letter)
  • Most recent utility bill
  • Lease or mortgage statement
  • For crisis: shutoff notice or proof of empty fuel tank

New Jersey-specific FAQ

Is USF the same as LIHEAP?

No. USF (Universal Service Fund) is a New Jersey state program — a monthly credit on your bill. LIHEA is the federal LIHEAP program in NJ — an annual cash benefit. They stack and share an application. Apply for both.

How does the USF monthly credit work?

USF caps your combined annual gas and electric bills at 6% of household income (or 3% if you heat with electricity). The state pays the difference monthly directly to your utility. You see the credit on every bill.

What is the Winter Termination Program?

NJ's Winter Termination Program prohibits utility shutoffs for income-qualified residential customers from November 15 through March 15. You must be enrolled in USF, LIHEA, or another qualifying program to receive automatic protection.

Can I apply for USF and LIHEA in one application?

Yes. The state DCA application form covers both programs. If you qualify for one, you typically qualify for the other (same 200% FPL income limit).

Does USF cover oil and propane?

USF covers gas and electric utility bills. Oil and propane households can use LIHEA instead. NJ's heating oil component pays vendors directly for delivery.

How much can USF actually save me?

Up to $1,800/year in monthly credits, depending on income and bill size. For a household with $250/month combined gas+electric bills and $30,000 income, USF caps the bills at $150/month — saving $100/month, $1,200/year.

Can renters qualify?

Yes for USF and LIHEA if you pay your own utility bill. If utilities are bundled into rent, the regular benefit is harder to access.

Is there Spanish support?

Yes. DCA materials are available in Spanish, and most local Community Action Agencies have bilingual staff. Application is at nj.gov/dca/divisions/dhcr.
