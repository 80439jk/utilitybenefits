Internet Bill Assistance: Programs That Replaced ACP and the Ones That Came Before It

Federal Lifeline, ISP-specific low-cost programs, state initiatives, and tribal broadband programs. The honest summary of what's actually available since ACP ended in mid-2024.

MRBy Marcus Reid, Senior Editor · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer

The federal Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP) ended in mid-2024 and nothing federal replaced it at the same scale. The two largest still-active federal options are Lifeline ($9.25/month, applied to phone or internet) and ISP-specific low-cost programs like Comcast Internet Essentials ($9.95/mo), AT&T Access ($5–$30/mo), and Spectrum Internet Assist ($24.99/mo). Most households that lost ACP qualify for at least one of these.

Federal programs

⭐ Lifeline — Federal Phone or Internet Discount

$9.25/month off home internet or phone (or $34.25 on tribal lands). FCC-administered. Predates ACP and remains active. Eligibility at 135% FPL or via SNAP/Medicaid/SSI.
📰 What replaced ACP?

The honest answer (nothing federally), what partially filled the gap, and what to do if you lost the benefit. Updated for 2026.
Tribal Broadband Connectivity Program

NTIA-administered grants funding broadband on tribal lands. Distinct from Tribal Lifeline (which is a household-level discount). Active and expanding through 2027.

ISP-specific low-cost programs

Most major ISPs offer their own low-cost programs — usually for households that meet income thresholds, qualify for Lifeline, or have school-aged children on free/reduced lunch. These are independent of Lifeline, meaning you can typically combine them.

Most of these programs require existing subscribers to qualify before ordering — you usually can't switch your existing retail plan to the low-cost tier without going through their separate signup. The application typically takes 5–10 minutes online plus 1–2 weeks for approval.

Demographic-targeted assistance

Senior internet discounts (60+)

Some ISPs and state programs offer senior-specific tiers. Spectrum Internet Assist requires SSI for 65+ as one qualifying path; California Lifeline has dedicated senior outreach.
Veteran internet benefits

Veterans Pension recipients qualify for Lifeline and most ISP-specific programs. Some ISPs (notably AT&T) have additional veteran discounts on retail plans.
Disability assistance

SSI and SSDI recipients qualify for Lifeline and most ISP-specific low-cost tiers. Some state programs add stackable benefits.
K-12 student households

Households with children on free/reduced school lunch programs qualify for most ISP-specific programs (Internet Essentials, Cox Connect2Compete, Optimum Advantage). T-Mobile Project 10Million is K-12-only.
SNAP / Medicaid recipients

SNAP and Medicaid are the broadest categorical eligibility doors — they unlock Lifeline plus most ISP-specific programs. Often the easiest path if you already receive these benefits.
Tribal Lifeline

Households on federally recognized tribal lands qualify for $34.25/month (vs $9.25 standard) plus distinct connection assistance. Stacks with the federal Tribal Broadband Connectivity Program at the infrastructure level.

Rural and tribal options

Rural households face two distinct challenges: fewer ISP options at the address level, and lower household incomes on average. Federal programs targeting rural broadband:

  • Federal RDOF (Rural Digital Opportunity Fund) — building out fiber and fixed wireless in underserved areas through 2030. If you live in an RDOF area, expect new options to come online.
  • Tribal Broadband Connectivity Program — $3 billion-plus in NTIA grants for tribal broadband infrastructure.
  • BEAD (Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment) — $42.45 billion state-allocated infrastructure program; deployments are starting in 2026.
  • Satellite — Starlink, HughesNet, Viasat. Starlink has reset rural expectations; the others remain options where Starlink isn't yet available.

How to actually apply

The application path depends on which program you're applying for. The general flow:

  1. For Lifeline — apply at lifelinesupport.org (or your state's equivalent if you're in California, Texas, or Oregon, which run their own portals).
  2. For ISP-specific programs — apply directly with the ISP. Each has its own portal: Comcast at internetessentials.com, AT&T at att.com/internet/access, etc.
  3. For state programs — vary by state. NY ConnectALL goes through participating ISPs.

Most households qualify for multiple programs. The easiest way to figure out the optimal stack is the eligibility check or a phone call.
