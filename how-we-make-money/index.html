How we make money

How UtilityBenefits.com makes money

Honest disclosure: this is exactly how the business pays for itself. The model is built so that what's good for our readers is also what makes us money — but we'd rather show you the wiring than have you guess.

Two revenue sources

1. Call-center referral fees (primary revenue)

The phone number on this site — (800) 555-1234 — connects to a benefits-help call center that we have a referral relationship with. The call center is staffed by trained specialists who help callers identify which utility assistance programs they may qualify for and apply on their behalf.

The call center pays us a referral fee for qualified calls. The fee structure varies by call quality (a long, productive call where the caller is enrolled in a program is worth more than a short call that doesn't lead anywhere). We do not receive payment for calls based on whether the caller is sold any particular product or service.

Why we run this model:

  • For most low-income households, navigating the assistance system is hard. The call center exists because applying to LIHEAP, your state program, your utility's hardship program, and Lifeline separately is a multi-week effort with confusing paperwork. A specialist can do this in 15–30 minutes.
  • For us, the call-routing model is the only way to monetize a content site about government programs. Government programs themselves don't pay referral fees. The call center, which provides actual labor in helping people apply, can.
  • We chose call-center referral over direct lead-selling (where lead-gen sites sell consumer contact info to multiple buyers) because the call-center model keeps the household in control of their own information — they call us, we don't sell their info to anyone.

2. Affiliate revenue from comparison products (secondary revenue)

For a small number of consumer products — primarily home security systems, cell phone plans, and home internet plans where there's a meaningful comparison-shopping decision — we use affiliate links. If you click through one of these links and sign up for the product, the company pays us a referral fee.

Where this applies:

  • Home security: Ring, SimpliSafe, Vivint, ADT, Cove, and other DIY/professional system reviews use affiliate links. This is the standard model for security comparison content.
  • Some MVNO cell carriers and ISPs: When we recommend a specific provider as the best fit for a use case, the link to sign up may be an affiliate link. We disclose where this applies.
  • We do NOT use affiliate links inside the eligibility calculator results. The eligibility check is for assistance program research, not consumer product sales.
  • We do NOT use affiliate links for federal or state programs. LIHEAP, Lifeline, CARE, PIPP Plus, etc. — there's no commission to earn on these and we don't pretend there is.

What we don't do

  • We do not sell your personal information. The eligibility check is anonymous — we collect state, household size, and income range, none of which can identify you. If you choose to call our specialists, the call center has its own privacy practices but does not sell your data without your consent.
  • We do not run display advertising. No banner ads, no programmatic ad networks, no targeted re-advertising on this site. (This may change in the future. If it does, we'll update this page.)
  • We do not accept payment from utility companies for editorial coverage. The fact that we cover Reliant, TXU, Comcast, AT&T, Spectrum, and others doesn't mean we're paid by them.
  • We do not accept payment from government program administrators. Federal LIHEAP coverage and state program coverage is editorial.
  • We do not promote products or services we wouldn't recommend to our families. Affiliate revenue is a secondary consideration after editorial fit.

Editorial firewall

Our editorial team and our business team are operationally separate. Editorial decisions about what to cover, how to cover it, and which programs to highlight are made by editors based on what serves readers best. Business decisions about call-center routing, affiliate partners, and revenue strategy are made separately and do not influence editorial coverage.

Specifically:

  • The decision to lead with PIPP Plus rather than LIHEAP on our Ohio state page is editorial — based on what saves Ohio households the most money — not commercial.
  • The decision to write a 2,200-word "What Replaced ACP?" page is editorial — that's a high-value answer for readers — even though there's no obvious commercial revenue from a "what happened to a discontinued program" page.
  • If a utility company offered to pay us to omit unflattering information about their hardship programs, we would refuse.

What our call center actually does

When you call (800) 555-1234, you reach a benefits specialist who will:

  • Ask about your state, household size, income, and circumstances
  • Identify which federal, state, and utility-specific programs you may qualify for
  • Walk you through the application process for each, in priority order
  • For some programs (especially state programs and utility hardship), apply on your behalf with your authorization
  • For crisis situations (shutoff notices), engage your utility's hardship line and emergency LIHEAP simultaneously

The call center does not:

  • Try to sell you any product
  • Charge you for the help
  • Share your information with marketing partners without your explicit consent

TCPA and consent

If you fill out a callback form on this site, the form requires your affirmative consent (an unchecked checkbox) for us and our marketing partners to contact you about utility assistance and savings programs. The exact consent language is shown on every form and is reviewed by TCPA-experienced legal counsel. You can opt out anytime by replying STOP to any text message or by emailing privacy@utilitybenefits.com.

If you'd rather not call

The eligibility check on this site is anonymous and gives you actionable program recommendations without ever requiring a phone number or email. If you'd rather apply to programs yourself using the official agency websites linked in our content, that works too. We'd prefer you get the help, by whichever path works for you.
