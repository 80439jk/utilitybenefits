Best MVNOs of 2026: Detailed Comparison

Seven MVNOs worth considering, ranked by who they fit best. Pricing, networks, and the trade-offs.

SKBy Sara Khan · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

Best MVNOs by user type: Visible+ ($45/mo) for Verizon-network users wanting unlimited data and full priority. US Mobile ($25–$45) for flexibility — pick Verizon or T-Mobile network. Mint Mobile ($15–$30 with annual prepaid) for the absolute lowest price on T-Mobile network. Cricket ($35–$60) for AT&T-network users wanting full priority. Metro by T-Mobile ($25–$60) for T-Mobile-network users wanting full priority. Boost Mobile for 3-network roaming. Total Wireless for prepaid Verizon-network with broad retail availability.

Visible / Visible+ (Verizon-owned)

  • Network: Verizon (full priority on Visible+)
  • Pricing: Visible $30/mo (unlimited, deprioritized) · Visible+ $45/mo (unlimited, full priority + 50GB hotspot)
  • Best for: Verizon users who want lower price without sacrificing the network
  • Strengths: No deprioritization on Visible+. eSIM activation in minutes. Includes some Mexico/Canada roaming.
  • Weaknesses: No physical retail support — all online. No subsidized phones. Lighter customer service.

US Mobile

  • Network: Verizon OR T-Mobile (you choose per line)
  • Pricing: Lite plans from $8/mo (limited data) · Unlimited plans $25–$45/mo
  • Best for: Flexibility — easy to switch networks, mix family members across networks
  • Strengths: Network choice. Strong customizability — pick your data tier, bring your own phone, easy port. Annual plans available for additional savings.
  • Weaknesses: Some plans deprioritize. Less brand recognition. Customer service is online-first.

Mint Mobile

  • Network: T-Mobile
  • Pricing: $15/mo (5GB), $20/mo (15GB), $25/mo (20GB), $30/mo (unlimited) — all require 12-month prepaid
  • Best for: Lowest price for T-Mobile coverage; users willing to prepay annually
  • Strengths: Cheapest mainstream MVNO. Simple pricing. Owned by T-Mobile (since 2024).
  • Weaknesses: Annual upfront commitment. Renewal pricing higher than intro rates — must reshop. Deprioritization in heavy congestion (uncommon issue).

Cricket Wireless

  • Network: AT&T (full priority — Cricket is owned by AT&T)
  • Pricing: $35/mo (5GB) · $45/mo (15GB) · $60/mo (unlimited)
  • Best for: AT&T-network users wanting full priority and physical retail support
  • Strengths: Full AT&T priority. Wide retail presence (Cricket stores). Strong family plan discounts.
  • Weaknesses: More expensive than no-priority MVNOs. International features lighter than parent AT&T.

Metro by T-Mobile

  • Network: T-Mobile (full priority — Metro is owned by T-Mobile)
  • Pricing: $25/mo (5GB) · $40/mo (10GB) · $50/mo (unlimited) · $60/mo (unlimited + perks)
  • Best for: T-Mobile-network users wanting full priority + physical retail
  • Strengths: Full T-Mobile priority. Wide retail presence. Strong introductory deals on subsidized phones.
  • Weaknesses: Less aggressive than Mint on price. Multi-line discount math can be complex.

Boost Mobile

  • Network: Boost's own network plus T-Mobile, AT&T, Verizon roaming
  • Pricing: $25/mo (15GB) · $40/mo (unlimited) · $60/mo (premium unlimited)
  • Best for: Users wanting 3-network access — Boost roams between Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile depending on coverage
  • Strengths: Multi-network roaming finds best coverage anywhere. Built own 5G network 2022–2024 for additional capacity.
  • Weaknesses: Customer service has had complaints during DISH ownership transition. Network mix can be confusing.

Total Wireless (formerly Total by Verizon)

  • Network: Verizon
  • Pricing: $25/mo (5GB) · $35/mo (15GB) · $50/mo (unlimited)
  • Best for: Verizon-network users wanting prepaid simplicity + Walmart-distributed retail availability
  • Strengths: Sold in Walmart and other big-box retail. Prepaid simplicity. No credit check. Owned by Verizon.
  • Weaknesses: Some plans deprioritize. Less aggressive pricing than Visible+.

Other MVNOs worth knowing

  • Tello — T-Mobile network, very flexible plans, $5–$30/month. Good for low-data users.
  • Tracfone family (Straight Talk, Walmart Family Mobile, etc.) — Verizon family of brands, sold heavily in big-box retail. Often used by lower-tech households.
  • Consumer Cellular — Senior-targeted, AT&T network. Highly rated customer service. Slightly higher prices but human support.
  • Google Fi — T-Mobile network + international roaming. $20+/mo. Best for travelers; not pure value play.
  • Ting Mobile — Reorganized in 2025; T-Mobile network. Boutique service; somewhat higher pricing.
  • Republic Wireless — Now part of DISH/Boost ecosystem; legacy customers only.

FAQ

Is the deprioritization issue real?

It exists but most users never notice. In extreme network congestion (stadiums during games, big concerts, rush hour on a packed highway), deprioritized MVNO traffic gets handled after major-carrier traffic. For typical home/work usage, you won't experience any difference. If you frequent congested venues, pay the small premium for Visible+ (Verizon priority), Cricket (AT&T priority), or Metro (T-Mobile priority).

What about 5G?

All major MVNOs include 5G access. The exact 5G bands depend on the underlying network — Verizon's mmWave, T-Mobile's mid-band, AT&T's mid-band. MVNOs typically include the same 5G access as their parent network.

How do I check coverage at my specific address?

Verizon, AT&T, and T-Mobile each have coverage maps. Whatever they show is what your MVNO on that network will get. Cross-reference with FCC's National Broadband Map for ground-truth verified coverage.

Can I keep my phone number?

Yes — number porting is mandated by federal law. Free, takes 1–4 hours typically. Don't cancel your old service before the port completes.

What about hotspot data?

Most MVNO plans include some hotspot data. Visible+ has 50GB. US Mobile lets you customize. Mint typically caps at 10–20GB hotspot. Heavy tethering users should specifically check the plan's hotspot allowance.

Will my subsidized phone work after switching?

Yes if it's unlocked. Carrier-locked phones must be unlocked first — federal law requires carriers to unlock paid-off devices on request. Initiate the unlock with your current carrier before porting out.
