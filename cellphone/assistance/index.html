Cell Phone Assistance: Free Government Phone, Lifeline, and Carrier Discounts

The federal Lifeline program covers free phones and free monthly service for low-income households through dedicated MVNOs. Senior tiers, military and veteran discounts, and California's stacked state Lifeline cover the rest.

PSBy Priya Sharma, Telecom Policy Analyst · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer

The federal Lifeline program — what most people search for as "free government phone" — covers free monthly cell service plus a free basic smartphone for households at or below 135% FPL or receiving SNAP/Medicaid/SSI/Veterans Pension/Federal Public Housing. The benefit is delivered through MVNOs like SafeLink, Assurance Wireless, Q Link, StandUp Wireless, and TruConnect. On tribal lands, the enhanced benefit is $34.25/month. California adds the most generous state stack in the country.

"Free government phone" — what is it really?

The honest explainer: it IS Lifeline. Here's how it works, who qualifies, and which MVNO to use.

Tribal Lifeline ($34.25/mo)

Enhanced benefit on federally recognized tribal lands. Additional categorical eligibility paths via BIA programs. Stacks with the federal Tribal Broadband Connectivity Program.

Lifeline MVNO providers

Lifeline phone service is delivered through dedicated MVNOs — most don't sell retail plans, just Lifeline. Coverage and offerings vary by state because each MVNO is approved at the state level.

Demographic-targeted programs

Free phone for seniors (60+)

Lifeline applies regardless of age. Plus T-Mobile Essentials 55+, Consumer Cellular AARP-partnered plans, and Lively (formerly Jitterbug) for simplified senior phones.
Veteran & military discounts

Veterans Pension recipients qualify for Lifeline categorically. Plus Verizon, AT&T, and T-Mobile each have additional veteran/military discount tiers on retail plans.
Disability programs

SSI and SSDI recipients qualify for Lifeline. Some MVNOs offer disability-specific plans with text-to-speech-friendly hardware. Hearing aid-compatible phone requirements per FCC.
SNAP / Medicaid recipients

SNAP and Medicaid are the most-used categorical paths to Lifeline. If you receive either, apply directly without income paperwork.

The phone-vs-internet decision

One Lifeline benefit per household. You apply it to phone OR internet — not both. For most former ACP households, the right choice is to apply Lifeline to phone (manifesting as a "free phone + free service" through an MVNO) and get internet through an ISP-specific low-cost program (Comcast Internet Essentials, AT&T Access, Spectrum Internet Assist) which is independent of Lifeline.

State Lifeline programs

Two states stack state-level subsidies on top of federal Lifeline:

StateProgramWhat it adds
CaliforniaCalifornia LifeLineState subsidies on top of federal Lifeline; some MVNO plans become free with much higher data allowances. The most generous combined benefit in the country.
TexasTexas LifelineState-administered through PUC; some additional carriers and minor variations.
OregonOTAP (Oregon Telephone Assistance Program)State telephone assistance running alongside federal Lifeline.

Other states use federal Lifeline directly via the National Verifier at checklifeline.org.

How to actually apply

  1. Confirm Lifeline eligibility — use this site's eligibility check or check at lifelinesupport.org. If you receive SNAP, Medicaid, or SSI, you qualify categorically.
  2. Apply through the National Verifier — at checklifeline.org. California, Texas, and Oregon residents use their state portal.
  3. Choose an MVNO — pick one with good coverage at your address. SafeLink, Assurance, or Q Link cover most of the U.S.
  4. Order the free phone and activate service — usually arrives within 5–10 business days.
  5. Recertify annually — Lifeline requires yearly recertification to keep the benefit active.

Don't pick the wrong MVNO

Carriers vary by coverage and data tier. A specialist can match you with the Lifeline MVNO that has the strongest signal at your address, plus check whether you qualify for stacked state Lifeline benefits.
