What we do

UtilityBenefits.com covers utility savings and assistance programs across six verticals: electricity, natural gas, home internet, cell phone, home security, and water/sewer. For each vertical, we explain how to lower your bill (switching providers, comparing rates, negotiating, optimizing usage) and which assistance programs you may qualify for (LIHEAP, Lifeline, state-specific programs like CARE and PIPP Plus, utility-specific hardship funds, and demographic-targeted discounts for seniors, veterans, and households on disability).

Most of our visitors come to us looking for one specific thing — "Do I qualify for LIHEAP?" "What replaced ACP?" "How do I lower my Texas electric bill?" — and discover that the answer involves more programs than they realized. Households at the intersection of low-income and high-utility-cost typically qualify for 3–5 stackable programs but apply to one and stop. We exist to fix that.

Why we exist

Three things prompted this work:

  • The post-ACP confusion. When the Affordable Connectivity Program ended in mid-2024, 23 million households lost their internet subsidy and the federal government did not communicate clearly about alternatives. Most existing internet-help websites still have outdated ACP information live, which is actively confusing readers. The honest answer (Lifeline plus ISP-specific programs plus emerging state programs) wasn't available in one place.
  • Fragmented assistance landscape. A typical low-income household applying for utility help has to navigate LIHEAP at one agency, state programs (CARE in California, PIPP Plus in Ohio, USF in New Jersey) at another, utility hardship programs at a third, and Lifeline at a fourth. Each application has its own paperwork, eligibility quirks, and timeline. Most people give up after one or two.
  • Most "save on utilities" content is shallow. The web has plenty of articles about lowering your electric bill, but most are written for SEO rather than for actual savings. We try to write the version we'd want for our own families.

How we operate

Three principles shape our work:

Editorial independence

Our editorial team chooses what to cover and how to cover it. We do not accept payment from utility companies, government agencies, or assistance program administrators in exchange for coverage decisions. We make money in ways that are visible and disclosed (see How We Make Money) — but those revenue sources do not influence what we write.

Honesty over optimism

The honest answer to "What replaced ACP?" is "Nothing federally." The honest answer to "Will switching providers save me money?" is often "Maybe, but only if you're in a deregulated state." We don't pretend assistance programs are larger or easier to access than they are, and we don't pretend savings tactics work better than they do. The site exists for the people reading it, not for traffic metrics.

Verified, dated information

Every page shows a "Last verified" date and the editor responsible. We re-verify state-specific information annually (when HHS updates the federal poverty level each January, when state LIHEAP windows open, when programs change) and update every page accordingly. See our full editorial standards.

Who we are

The site is run by a small editorial team including federal benefits researchers, state utility-program analysts, and consumer journalists. Our authors are listed on our author bios page with their credentials and beats. Every article carries a byline.

We work with a call center staffed by trained benefits specialists who can walk callers through their qualifying programs and apply on their behalf. The call center is a separate operation from the editorial team — see How We Make Money for the relationship.

What we don't do

  • We are not a government agency. We are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or funded by HHS, the FCC, the FTC, your state utility commission, or any government program administrator. We provide information about government programs as a public service and we link to the official agencies for actual applications.
  • We do not collect SSNs or sensitive financial data on this site. The eligibility check uses anonymized inputs (state, household size, income range). If you choose to call our specialists, they may ask for additional information needed to actually apply for programs on your behalf — but only with your explicit consent.
  • We do not sell or share your personal data without consent. See our privacy policy and the TCPA disclosure on every form.
  • We do not write content using AI alone. Every page has a named human author and a named human editor. AI-assisted research and drafting is fine; AI-only publication is not.
