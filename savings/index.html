Lower Your Utility Bills: Savings Guides Across Six Verticals

Most U.S. households leave $1,500–$3,000/year on the table by not actively shopping their utility bills. The savings playbooks for each utility differ — pick yours below.

SKBy Sara Khan, Senior Editor · Last verified: May 3, 2026
Quick Answer · Speakable schema

The biggest savings opportunities by utility: electricity ($300–$900/yr by switching providers in 16 deregulated states; $200–$500/yr via TOU + efficiency in regulated states), gas (small savings via switching in 6 states; $300–$600/yr via weatherization; $500–$1,500/yr via heat pump conversion), internet ($180–$360/yr via retention call; $600+/yr via cord-cutting), cell phone ($1,200–$2,000/yr by switching to MVNO), water (mostly leak detection — $200–$500/yr typical), home security ($500–$2,000 over 5 years by going DIY + insurance discount). Total achievable savings for an average household: $1,500–$3,000/year.

Pick your utility

⚡ Electricity savings

16 deregulated states (TX, PA, OH, IL, NY, MA, CT, MD, NJ, DE, DC, ME, NH, RI, MI, VA): switch providers, save 10–30%. 35 regulated states: TOU + efficiency rebates + IRA stacking.
🔥 Gas savings

6 deregulated states (GA, OH, PA, NY, MD, DC): shop suppliers. Everywhere: weatherization, furnace upgrade, heat pump conversion (federal IRA $14K+).
📡 Internet savings

Negotiate retention ($15–$30/mo off), switch to fiber or 5G home internet, cord-cut from cable bundles, and ISP low-cost programs for income-qualified households.
📱 Cell phone savings

Switch from Verizon/AT&T/T-Mobile to MVNO (Mint, Visible, US Mobile, Cricket): save $50–$80/line per month. Same towers, half the price.
💧 Water savings

Leak detection (toilet flappers are #1 culprit), conservation rebates, lead pipe replacement, and city utility programs.
🛡️ Security savings

DIY systems vs. professional monitoring (1/3 the cost), homeowners insurance discount (5–20%), and Medicaid/VA medical alert coverage.

Savings vs. assistance

UtilityBenefits.com tracks two parallel paths. The savings track (this page) is for households who pay full retail for utilities and want to manage costs through shopping, switching, and efficiency upgrades. The assistance track is for income-qualified households who can stack federal LIHEAP, state programs (CARE, PIPP, USF, EUSP, A-60, EAP, etc.), Lifeline, and utility-specific discount tiers — often $1,000–$3,000+/year in stacked benefits.

Most low-income households should run the assistance check FIRST. For those who don't qualify or want to layer additional savings, the shopping/switching guides above are the next layer. Run the eligibility check →

When to revisit

  • Annually for internet, cellphone, and electricity — promo rates expire, contracts come up, market changes.
  • Quarterly for default-supply rate resets in deregulated states (typically June 1 / December 1).
  • End of life for furnaces, water heaters, AC units — replacement decisions are 15-year choices, worth research.
  • When you move — every utility decision is fresh. Don't accept default supplier, default ISP, default carrier.
  • When IRA programs roll out in your state — HEEHRA and HOMES rebates are launching state-by-state through 2026. Watch for your state's launch.
